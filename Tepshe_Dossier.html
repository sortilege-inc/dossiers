I. Identity & Overview

Tepshe is a Jinxed Tengu — a raven-kind artificer whose very existence seems to attract misfortune, then cheerfully weaponise it. Where other inventors build toward elegant solutions, Tepshe builds toward interesting ones. The fact that "interesting" and "explosive" share considerable operational overlap is, she would insist, merely a happy coincidence of engineering.

Her background tells the whole story in miniature: she was saved by clockwork. Some mechanical contrivance — whether of her own devising or another's — intervened at a critical moment in her past, and she has been in the debt of gears and springs ever since. The Inventor class was not merely a career choice. It was a reckoning with what she owes the machines.

As a Jinxed Tengu, Tepshe carries the ancestral trait of accumulated misfortune — a reputation for being followed by bad luck that, in her hands, has become something very close to a precision instrument. She has learned to step around the disasters she attracts, and to ensure that when the jinx finally lands, it lands on whomever she is currently annoyed with.

The distinction between an accident and an invention is largely one of paperwork.
⚙ Campaign Role — Convention One-Shot

Tepshe excels as a battlefield control specialist and utility inventor. Her Construct Companion, net launcher, grappling gun, and binding snares allow her to physically reshape the geometry of any encounter. She is rarely the direct damage dealer — she is the one who ensures the damage dealer has a stationary, thoroughly inconvenienced target.

⚙ Crafting Note — Assurance

Via Assurance (Crafting), Tepshe may choose to treat her Crafting roll as a flat 18, forgoing the dice entirely. For routine construction and repair this guarantees success on tasks up to a certain difficulty ceiling. She does not gamble on the craft. She gambles on the application.

V. Tengu Heritage — The Jinxed

Standard Tengu already carry the weight of omen and ill portent in many of the cultures they move through. The Jinxed variant carries something more specific: a persistent aura of misfortune that has become so reliable as to be almost mechanical. Tepshe does not believe in fate. She believes in probability, and she has learned to read the clustering of bad luck as a pattern that can be engineered around — and occasionally, engineered toward.

Ancestry Feats

Scavenger's Search
Ancestry Feat 1st  ·  Tengu
When Tepshe takes a minute to search an area, she finds hidden items, treasure, and useful detritus that others overlook. Her eye for overlooked components is not mystical — it is the eye of someone who has had to build from scraps for most of her life. She knows what is useful before she knows what it is.
Magpie Snatch
Ancestry Feat 5th  ·  Tengu
Tepshe may attempt to Steal an item from a creature as a free action when she hits them with a Strike. The feather-quick hands that pick locks also pick pockets, and what better moment to relieve an enemy of something inconvenient than the split second they are focused on the pain of being hit?
The thing about being jinxed is that the misfortune has to go somewhere. I have simply spent considerable effort ensuring it goes toward whomever I am currently annoyed with. // Tepshe, in conversation with a concerned bystander

VI. The Inventor's Craft

The Inventor class is built around a central innovation — Tepshe's masterwork creation, in this case a Construct Companion. Everything else in the class radiates outward from that core invention. She is not merely a fighter who happens to build things; she is a builder who happens to fight, and the distinction matters enormously in how she approaches a problem.

Core Class Features

Construct Companion Innovation Advanced at 4th
Feature 1st  ·  Advanced Construct Companion: Feat 4th
Tepshe's central innovation: a mechanical creature built, maintained, and perpetually modified by her own hand. By 4th level she has taken Advanced Construct Companion, significantly improving its combat and utility capabilities. It acts on her turn, can Strike or Aid, and represents both her greatest tactical asset and her most emotionally significant possession. When it takes damage, she feels it. When it breaks down, she takes it personally.
Explode Signature
Feature 1st  ·  Inventor  ·  Two-Action Activity
The innovation can detonate in a controlled burst, dealing fire damage in an area. Tepshe can trigger this deliberately as a tactical weapon, catching multiple enemies in the blast. Crucially, the innovation can be rebuilt after any destruction — the schematics are in her head and her hands, not in the object itself. The explosion is not a failure state. It is a feature.
Peerless Inventor
Feature 1st  ·  Inventor
Tepshe has a perpetual supply of raw materials for crafting and can construct items far faster than normal. She may use Crafting for the Earn Income downtime activity at enhanced rates. In practical terms: if she has a few minutes and access to scrap, she can build something useful. She is never truly out of resources — only ever out of time.
Tamper
Feat 1st  ·  Inventor  ·  Single Action
Tepshe sabotages a creature's equipment, environment, or the mechanical systems of an enemy. Touching an adjacent object or creature, she imposes penalties — armour that binds, weapons that misfire, shields that buckle at inconvenient moments. The jinx, externally applied and precisely targeted.
Collapse Construct
Feat 2nd  ·  Inventor
Tepshe can cause her Construct Companion to fold or collapse into a portable configuration, and can later rapidly redeploy it. This prevents enemies from targeting the construct when it is not needed, and enables dramatic mid-combat reveals when the clockwork mechanical erupts from what appeared to be an innocuous bag.
Expert Overdrive
Feature 3rd  ·  Inventor
Tepshe can push her innovation — and herself — beyond standard operating parameters. Overdrive grants bonus damage on Strikes but carries the risk of a misfire on failure. The Expert tier significantly reduces that risk. She is not reckless. She is precisely calibrated.
Inventor Weapon Expertise
Feature 5th  ·  Inventor
At 5th level, Tepshe's proficiency with her innovation's weapon capabilities reaches Expert rank, increasing her attack accuracy. Her hands are as steady as her schematics.

Snare Crafting

Tepshe can craft and place snares using her Crafting skill. In concert with her binding snares inventory, she can reshape a battlefield's approach lanes before a single initiative is rolled.

General & Bonus Feats

Prescient Planner
General Feat 3rd
Once per day, Tepshe declares she "planned ahead" and produces a common mundane item as though she had packed it. She always had the right tool. She had simply not mentioned it yet.
Shield Block
Bonus Feat
When a shield reduces incoming damage, Tepshe can trigger Shield Block as a reaction to reduce it further. Her shields are battered but functional — like most of her equipment.

⚙ The Construct Companion

The Construct Companion is not listed in inventory because it is not inventory — it is a collaborator. It travels with Tepshe at all times when not folded for transit. It has been repaired, modified, and extensively argued with. It does not argue back. This is, Tepshe concedes, among its more admirable qualities.

IX. Character Notes & Play Guidance

The Jinx as Asset

The Jinxed heritage is not merely flavour — it is Tepshe's core personality driver. She has spent her life managing the probability of misfortune clustering around her, and this has made her exceptionally good at contingency planning. Prescient Planner is a mechanical expression of this: she always has the right tool because she always anticipated needing it. The jinx taught her to think several disasters ahead.

Tactical Philosophy

Tepshe does not fight. She arranges fights. The ideal Tepshe encounter begins before initiative is rolled: snares placed, the Construct deployed, the net launcher primed. Once combat begins, her job is to ensure the environment works against her enemies — binding snares restrict movement, the net launcher removes a target from freedom of action, Tamper degrades their equipment, and the Construct handles direct engagement while she repositions. She is the architect. Not the hammer.

One-Shot Recommendations

⚙ Convention One-Shot Notes

For a one-shot context, Tepshe's best moments come from Prescient Planner — dramatically producing the right tool at the right moment rewards both mechanical creativity and theatrical timing. The Collapse Construct / sudden deployment sequence makes for an excellent reveal. Lead with the net launcher or binding snares to immediately demonstrate her control-focused identity; let the Construct do the heroic front-line work while she manoeuvres and tinkers. She shines brightest when given even a moment to prepare. If dropped directly into combat with no warning, lead with Tamper on the most dangerous target, then deploy the net launcher on the second. She has planned for this too. She always plans for this.
