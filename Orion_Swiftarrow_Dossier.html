Bioα · Vita

Orion is more comfortable around animals than around people, and watches over his friends as though they were a litter of newborn pups. He was a champion of Heliod once — until Klothys emerged from the Underworld and showed him the danger of the Sun-God's hubris. The shift cost him a community, and gave him a charge: tend the strands of destiny that civilization keeps trying to cut.

His father was an oread, and the Nyxborn marks of that lineage have been on Orion since birth — a cloak of stars when he wills it, a hide that turns aside necrotic chill and radiant fire alike. He left his Lagonna band on his protoporos and has not returned. The Fallow Year has changed what that journey is for.

Why Orion is on the Expeditionο · Hodós

The Strands of Destiny. Orion's devotion to Klothys aligns perfectly with investigating how the web of destiny has been disrupted by Karametra's absence. His Ranger's duty could see him tasked to protect caravans or sacred groves — and a centaur who can outpace any horse and a Hunter who specializes in monstrosities is exactly who you want when a famine drives strange things out of the wild places.

The Fallow Year is, in Klothys's terms, a wound dealt to destiny by the gods' ambitions; that places Orion's piety and his vocation in the same direction.
