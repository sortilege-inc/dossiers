Class Features
Umbral Corruption Class
As Mellan accumulates scars, he is slowly consumed by the Umbra. When he deals damage, he deals extra damage equal to his number of scars. When he marks his last Hope slot with a scar, he does not retire — instead he succumbs to the Umbra's influence and charges forever into the dark to join the terrors that stalk lightless spaces.
Hold Them Off Hope Feature
Spend 3 Hope when you succeed on an attack with a weapon to immediately use that weapon's feature again. With the Shortbow, this means a second arrow in the same breath — two marks on the same target, or two different targets held at range.
Ranger's Focus Beastbound
Spend a Hope and make an attack against a target. On a success, your attacks temporarily make that target your Focus. While they are your Focus, you gain the following:
  • Deal normal attack damage and the target must mark a Stress
  • You know precisely what direction they are in at all times
  • When you deal damage to them, they must mark a Stress
  • If you fail an attack against them, you may reroll your Duality Dice
The feature ends when the scene ends or another condition breaks it.
Companion — Wivek Beastbound
Mellan has an animal companion — Wivek, a hawk — who stays by his side unless told otherwise. Wivek is commanded via a Spellcast Roll; spending a Hope to add a relevant Companion Experience. On a success with Hope, if Mellan's next action builds on Wivek's success, he gains advantage. When Mellan levels up, he also chooses a level-up option for Wivek from the companion training list.
Evasive at Range Ranger
When targeted by an attack made from beyond Melee range, Mellan can mark a Stress to roll a d4 and add the result as a bonus to his Evasion against that attack. Combined with his base Evasion of 13, this can make him nearly impossible to hit at distance.

Character

Appearance

Mellan is Lenvar — human-built in the Ridgeborne way, which means lean and conditioned rather than bulked. His hair is thick and close-cropped in the way of someone who learned early that loose hair catches on branches. He carries a shortbow on his back and wears whatever the weather requires plus slightly less than that. He is often scraped. He does not always notice. There is frequently blood on the shoulder of his cloak from where Wivek lands without being gentle about it.

Manner

He is not unfriendly. He is simply operating at a slight remove from whatever room he is in, as though part of his attention is still on the ridge. When he focuses on you, he focuses completely — the Instinct that serves him on the ranges turns on people with the same unhurried precision. This can be disarming. He does not intend it to be. He has a Presence score of −1 and this is accurate.

The Stone

He pressed a smooth black river-stone with a white ring into Ygva's hand the night he left the circle. He did not explain it. He did not wait for her to give anything back. He has not sent word since, and this is not because he has forgotten — it is because he does not know what the message would say. He has thought about it on many quiet nights at altitude, with Wivek asleep on his arm, and each time he thinks about it the stone is the right thing to have given and he cannot improve on it.

Wivek

The hawk's name was given by Mellan at fifteen, in a language he invented on the spot and has only ever applied to her. She is not gentle. She is loyal in the specific way of a creature that has decided your company is worth tolerating, which is the only loyalty that counts. She lands on his injured shoulder when she is not paying attention, which is often. He does not correct this.

History

Mellan grew up in Ulmira's Step to a family of rangers — his mother worked the mountain routes, his father maintained the settlement's supply lines, and the expectation that he would eventually go north was so ambient it was never discussed as a choice. He came up in the circle with Ygva, Vaetra, Sarru, Tamros, and Kelun, and distinguished himself as the one most likely to already know where the exits were.

He attended Renavir's lessons with his body present and his attention divided. The lector's texts were interesting but the thing the texts described — the shape of the world, the movement of creatures through terrain, the grammar of the high places — was more interesting to learn directly. He was fifteen when he found Wivek as a fledgling in a crevasse above the settlement's upper terrace, and the relationship that developed over the following months clarified something he had not had words for before. He left the circle at fifteen, pressed a stone into Ygva's hand without explanation, and walked north.

He has been ranging for five years. He is good at it in the particular way of someone for whom a skill and a temperament have aligned so completely that the skill does not feel like effort. The ranges have given him two things in abundance: silence and evidence. He has learned to read both. He does not yet know what they are adding up to, but the sum is growing and Wivek is restless in a way she has not been before, and he trusts this.

Rite of Passage · Kept · Trophy
Trophy from His First Kill
He carries it. He has not decided whether carrying it is sentiment or record-keeping. Both, probably. He made the kill alone, with Wivek watching from a crag above. He did not feel what he expected to feel. He felt something he still does not have a name for. He kept the trophy as a marker for that feeling.
