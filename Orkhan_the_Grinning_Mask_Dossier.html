On the Subject

Orkhan was born under the stars on the open steppe to a Cuman raiding clan, the fourth of six surviving children, with one more buried before he could ride. His clan called itself a tribe; settled lords called it criminal. The distinction never meant much to him as a boy — by ten he was on a small mare with a short bow across his back, by sixteen he had ridden in three raids and killed a man whose face he can still see when he closes his eyes.

The clan ate well by raiders' standards. The horses were good ones. He grew up comfortable in a way that did not involve walls. Then, in his twenty-first year, his small command was caught between a Seljuk regiment and a Mongol scouting party, and only he and two horses came out of the valley. He walked into the nearest caravanserai with his mask in his hand and asked who was hiring guards. Three months later he took shahada from a Sufi who fed him for the asking. He is not certain whether he believes — but the prayers steady him before a fight, and that is enough.

He has worn the mask of his line for ten years now. It is steel, leering, a rictus grin hammered into the face plate by his own hand. He does not take it off while his blade is bare; the old steppe tradition forbids it, and even now that he prays five times a day and sets the mask down on its cloth to bow, he respects the older law. The mask of the Cuman grins still.

He has two enemies he could name. One is a governor in a Persianate city who lost a cousin in a raid Orkhan rode in at sixteen. One is another mercenary — a rival caravan guard from a competing brotherhood — who took a contract Orkhan wanted, and who walked away from a knife-fight Orkhan started in a tavern in Isfahan. Neither has caught up with him yet.

Family
Close relatives — clan-raised. Six siblings present at birth.
Siblings
Five siblings. Some are likely dead by now; he does not always know which.
Lifestyle
Comfortable. Successful raiders eat and sleep better than the lord's tax-paying farmers across the valley. His clan called itself a tribe; settled lords called it raiders, horse-thieves, and criminals — the labels are not in dispute.
Strings to Arcanum
Community believes in the supernatural superstitiously. Stories, charms, fear of evil spirits — no real arcane practice in the clan. He grew up sure that magic was a thing that happened to other people, in other tents.
Past Education
Unorthodox — taught by family, clan elders, and the occasional captured or hosted scribe. He can read Cuman runes and a little Mongolian; he learned Persian on the road as an adult. Grants the dice set proficiency.

Profession

Silk Road PEX system · Apprentice rank

Beast Tamer
Apprentice · Rank 1 PEX 0

Beast Taming (free starting ability): Proficiency in Animal Handling. He can attempt to soothe and tame wild beasts (horses, donkeys, mules, bears, boars, lions, panthers, etc.) by spending 1 hour with proper tools and succeeding on a CR-scaled Animal Handling check. Already-tamed mounts will obey him on a DC 10 Animal Handling check if their original owner is not visible.

Orkhan learned the profession by necessity on the caravan trails — stabled mounts that change hands every few weeks, half of them strangers to him before the morning's saddle. (Aymara Kül-Tegin practices the same Apprentice rank but very differently — she grew up training her clan's herds and warhorses. Their methods diverge sharply where the profession's mechanics overlap.)

Connections

  • Aftab Singh the Arbiter — They met at a caravanserai outside Merv four years ago, both hired to the same merchant's escort for a six-day haul east. Orkhan was the captain of the small mercenary brotherhood Aftab fell in with; Aftab was the new contracted blade. They recognized each other inside an hour — Seasoned Warrior on Aftab's side, the same gift on Orkhan's — and the unspoken acknowledgment between two career-killers from different traditions held them civil through six days of dust. They have crossed paths twice since, at other caravanserais. Orkhan pays for Aftab's tea at the caravanserai bar when their work overlaps; Aftab pretends not to notice. Orkhan suspects, sometimes, that if his life had taken three small turns differently he would have ended up where Aftab is — and that Aftab, in turn, would have ended up where he is. He does not say this.
  • Aymara Flameshaper — They have met once, briefly, at a Yamak summer-camp gathering when Aymara was eleven and Orkhan was seventeen — Aymara's clan was visiting a clan that had visiting kin from the Yamak, and Orkhan was riding with his uncle. They do not remember each other. They share, without knowing it, a Beast Tamer's gift; if the campaign throws them together, they will recognize each other's hands on a horse before they recognize each other's faces.
