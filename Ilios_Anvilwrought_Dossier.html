Ilios Anvilwrought

Forge-orphaned, forge-rebuilt — a man of iron sheen and visible joints who has not slept in the mortal sense since the night the forge burned. His art is the only language he and Purphoros still share.

Stage of Career

Bioα · Vita

Ilios was orphaned by a fire at his parents' forge. The same fire — somehow — gave him to Purphoros, who took him under his protection and rebuilt him from iron and flame. His skin bears a metallic sheen, his joints are visible, and he has not slept (in the mortal sense) since the night the forge burned. He stands in for the lit hour and the inert hour both: a working artisan who must close his eyes for six unmoving hours and trusts the work to wait. His art is more than labor; it is the only language he and his god still share.

He dedicates his life to perfecting his craft and honoring his creator. As he travels, he seeks inspiration for new creations — exploring ruins and ancient temples for lost techniques and sacred designs that might elevate his art. He is a perfectionist who thinks anything worth doing is worth doing right, and he can be quick to assume he's being cheated when work he made is mishandled.

Supernatural Gift — Anvilwroughtθ · Dóron

You were forged in the fires of Purphoros's forge. Your appearance bears a metallic sheen and visible joints.

  • Constructed Resilience:
    • Advantage on saves vs. being poisoned; resistance to poison damage.
    • You don't need to eat, drink, or breathe.
    • Immunity to disease.
    • You don't need to sleep, and magic can't put you to sleep.
  • Sentry's Rest: A long rest requires at least 6 hours in an inactive, motionless state rather than sleep. You appear inert but are not unconscious, and you can see and hear as normal.

The combination of Constructed Resilience and Anvilwrought heritage makes Ilios essentially un-poisonable, undriveable-mad-by-sleep-deprivation, and impossible to ambush while resting. He sees and hears the full party's watch as it happens.

Why Ilios is on the Expeditionπ · Hodós

Crafting Solutions. Ilios, inspired by Purphoros and Keranos, seeks a technological solution that would cut out Karametra entirely. If the goddess of the harvest has fallen silent, then maybe the harvest doesn't need her — maybe it needs a hypocaust, or a clockwork irrigator, or a forge-fed seed protocol that some smith somewhere has not yet built.

Ilios's gods think mortal ingenuity is the point. The Fallow Year is the proof, and the proving ground.
