I. Identity & Standing

Eorlas joined Celenneth and Linnea on the road through Mirkwood in Book III. He had originally set out for Dale to ask for aid and to warn of the plight of the villages around the forest. The chronicle introduced him plainly — a kind old man with herbs — and then established him as something more across the journey: knowledge of poisons, calm authority over Shadow figures with his staff raised, a voice that spoke of the past like one who remembers it. The chronicle deliberately leaves the depth unopened.

He taught Linnea herblore. He gave her athelas. He commanded a Shadow figure to be gone. He named the iron-eye pendant when it activated. He showed them the ancient tree and the pool of clear water in Mirkwood — a memory, a fragment, of Greenwood the Great. He parted with them at the forest's eastern edge on Úrimë 13, 2965, riding on to the eastern villages to prepare them.

Book III · Mirkwood · The Iron-Eye Pendant Activates
The pendant named for what it was.
The pendant Celenneth had carried since Book II, found in the undergrowth, cold and unsettling, almost discarded, carried forward on instinct — now unravelling dark magic. Eorlas, immediately: it carries the strength of its maker, and it is shrouded in shadow.
Book III · Mirkwood · The Ancient Tree and the Pool
A memory, a fragment.
A fragment of Greenwood the Great surviving in Mirkwood's dark — the ancient tree, the pool of clear water. Linnea: how can this exist here? Eorlas's joyous-but-sorrowful tone in answer: a memory, a fragment. Beauty as defiance; the light that refuses to go entirely out. The chronicle noted: he speaks of the past like one who remembers it.
Úrimë 13, 2965 · The Forest's Eastern Edge
The parting.
Eorlas brought up the rear as they emerged from the wood. They paused to rest just beyond the forest's edge, and he turned to them: This is where I leave you. He explained: I originally set out for Dale to ask for aid and to warn of the plight of the villages around the forest, but you are better messengers and rhetoricians. I will continue on to the villages of the eastern forest to prepare them.
Úrimë 13, 2965 · The Blessing
Hand on shoulder; small smile.
To Celenneth, hand on her shoulder: You are a strong leader, Celenneth. You guided us through darkness, and I have no doubt you will continue to do so. To Linnea, a small smile: You've shown great courage, even when the Shadow sought to break you. Hold onto that strength. It will serve you well.
Úrimë 13, 2965 · The Vial and the Message
The elixir given; Elenna named.
From his satchel, the vial of faintly glowing liquid for Celenneth: An elixir brewed from the light of the golden clearing. It will restore strength in your darkest hour. Then, as he began to walk away, the message turned over his shoulder: When you reach Dale, seek out a woman named Elenna, in the market. Tell her that Farnath is save, that Eorlas still walks, and that there is a light against the Shadow.
Úrimë 13, 2965 · Vanished into the Vastness
He turned once more and continued on his way.
A glimmer of warmth in his eyes. Thank you. Farewell. The pair stood in silence for a moment, the weight of his departure settling over them. The road east took him.
