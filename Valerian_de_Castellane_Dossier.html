I. Dossier Overview

Valerian · 1222

Valerian de Castellane is a magus of House Guernicus, formerly of the Rhine Tribunal, now in self-imposed exile from Hermetic office. The name he uses is not his birth name; like many in the Order, he adopted a Hermetic name upon gauntlet. His native language is Frisian, and his deepest study has been the law — both Hermetic and mundane.

The shape of his sheet is unambiguous: he is a Rego/Vim specialist hoplite with a juridical grounding unusual for a magus. His top Art is Rego (15, with Puissant adding +3 in use); his top Form is Vim (15). His highest mundane Ability is Civil & Canon Law (6) in the Holy Roman Empire, with Common Law at 4 and Latin at 6. He has Code of Hermes 3 with Puissant pushing it to effective 5 in use. He is, in operational terms, a magus who can argue a case before any tribunal — Hermetic or mundane — and then enforce its conclusion with Rego suppression and Vim wardwork.

He arrived at Sjórseiðr in the spring of 1222, materialising on the deck of one of the covenant's ships fifty-five days after a sealed letter of his had been opened. He had escaped his imprisonment by delayed teleportation, written into a Watching Ward keyed to the letter's opening. He arrived with books, a trunk, and the request where are my quarters?

On the Reputations

The combination of Hermetic Prestige (positive Order Reputation 4), Infamous (negative Reputation 4), and Judged Unfairly (cannot accrue positive Reputations) generates a coherent picture: he is known and respected within the Order on the strength of something in his background, while simultaneously cursed by name elsewhere, and unable to repair his standing with anyone he does not already have. He is famous and infamous at once, in different rooms.

The Tradeoff Pattern

His virtue/flaw mix tells a particular story. Quiet Magic + Subtle Magic + Fast Caster together make him a magus who can cast quickly, silently, and motionlessly — exactly what one wants in a hoplite who may need to cast while bound, observed, or in disguise. Short-lived Magic + Creative Block limit him as a researcher and durations-caster: he is a field operative, not a builder. Magic Sensitivity aids investigation but reduces his Magic Resistance — a calculated trade for an investigator. The build is exactly what it appears to be: an enforcer who does not advertise his casting.

Rego/Vim Profile

His strongest single combination is Rego + Vim (15 + 15 = 30), with Rego pushed to 18 in use by Puissant. This is the toolkit of a wardsmith and counter-magus: maintaining wards, dispelling enchantments, opening intangible tunnels, holding spells in suspension. Secondary axes — Rego + Corpus (effective 28) and Rego + Terram (effective 30) — give him strong personal transport and earth-control respectively. Creo + Ignem (10 + 5 = 15) supports his single offensive spell. The build favours control over creation, which is the Guernicus operational signature.

The Legal Spine

His skill profile makes plain something the Hermetic name obscures: he is a jurist before he is a hoplite. Civil & Canon Law 6 (Holy Roman Empire) is his highest mundane Ability, with Common Law 4, Latin 6, Theology 2 (Heresy), and Dominion Lore 3 (Angels) supporting it. Combined with Code of Hermes 3 + Puissant (effective 5) and a specialty in Political Intrigue, he can plead a case before any tribunal in the Empire — secular, ecclesiastical, or Hermetic — and read the political subtext of who is really being prosecuted and why. His Penetration specialty (Mentem) and Parma specialty (Terram) speak to a particular professional history of investigations the dossier does not yet record.

VIII. The Adamant & Sanctum

His sanctum, per the sheet, is The Adamant's Laboratory, a 10-point laboratory aboard a ship of the same name now docked at Sjórseiðr. The Adamant is the fifth ship of the covenant's fleet, recently purchased and provided to him with a skeleton crew upon his arrival. He resides on board.

On the Sanctum-Ship

A 10-point laboratory aboard a ship is an unusual choice — the standard Hermetic sanctum is a stone-walled room in a stable covenant. A floating sanctum implies mobility, deniability, and the deliberate avoidance of fixed attachment to Sjórseiðr's other magi. It is consistent with his stated position: he has come here for shelter, not for integration. The Adamant is both his refuge and his exit, should one become necessary.

X. Recent Events

Early Winter 1221
Discovery
A letter is recovered on a captured ship during a naval engagement. It reveals that Valerian de Castellane, a Quaesitor of House Guernicus, is being held in custody at Breda, seat of the Duke of Brabant. The magi of the covenant weigh the strategic case for rescue — political leverage, covenant strength, avoiding a future prosecutor's grudge — against the jurisdictional complications: Brabant is Rhine Tribunal territory, and the covenant has no political cover there.
Late Winter 1221
Forward Party Despatched
Father Lachlan (diplomacy), Stintia (etiquette), Adam Smith (reconnaissance), and Dagmar (background presence) travel by cog to Breda. Dagmar provides a silver brooch for recognition and the message: apologies on behalf of Solving, who was unavoidably delayed. The bishopric is noted to be Liège — the same bishop pursuing Albigensian heresy the covenant has previously encountered.
Late Winter 1221
Prison Reconnaissance
Father Lachlan, presenting himself as a Franciscan ministering to prisoners, gains access to the upper tier of the Breda prison. The upper-tier cells are well-appointed studio quarters with rooms enough for scholarly work. Valerian is found in such a cell, with over thirty books, an extensive desk, and visible signs of ongoing study. Adam Smith senses the Gift and confirms Valerian is a magus.
Late Winter 1221
The Conversation
Father Lachlan presents the brooch. Valerian is initially extremely cautious; he then dismisses the local priest and speaks plainly. He reveals he is imprisoned by the Bishop of Liège rather than the Duke, that the Duke is cooperating with the Church for unstated reasons, and that he cannot trust any clergy under the Bishopric. On hearing of the covenant, his composure breaks; he collapses into a chair and asks, were you not involved in my capture? Told that Solving was unavoidably delayed, he replies with bitter irony: better late than never. He writes a note, casts a spell upon it openly, seals it, and hands it over with a warning: you cannot trust the clergy. He refuses to provide an Arcane Connection.
Winter 1221/1222
Analysis of the Letter
Dagmar's Premonitions roll (an exceptional result) yields two warnings: the letter is magically booby-trapped, and a vision of the Duke of Brabant receiving correspondence from the Pope. The magi convene at a +3 magic aura site on the coast and identify two nested effects in the letter — Rego Vim (outer) and Creo Vim (inner) — with a conditional trigger and an action component. Dustin opens the letter under Parma protection. The letter contains only a cryptic sigil and Valerian's signature. Nothing happens. The covenant is confused, then frustrated.
Spring 1222 — The Scholar's Wake
Arrival by Watching Ward
Fifty-five days after the seal is broken, books materialise on the deck of one of the covenant's ships. A trunk follows. Then Valerian himself — having escaped Breda by delayed teleportation, held in suspense by a Watching Ward keyed to the opening of his sealed letter. His first words on arrival: where are my quarters?
Spring 1222
The Revelations
Through careful questioning (Owen rolling 23 on Order of Hermes Lore), the covenant learns the substance of the matter: a sect of magi has suborned the Bishopric of Liège from within, representing a massive breach of the Code of Hermes — magi exerting control over Church hierarchy. Valerian was investigating this when he was silenced and imprisoned. He still possesses knowledge that, in his own words, could ruin a lot of people's day. He is technically still a Quaesitor but not permitted to practice. His library, he allows, is largely confiscated contraband; he offers it to the covenant's magi in exchange for protection. He is given the covenant's fifth ship, the Adamant, and a skeleton crew.
Autumn 1222
Copying the Folio
First recorded laboratory activity at Sjórseiðr. Valerian copies a folio of five lab texts in Latin: Circular Ward Against Faerie Spirits, Opening the Intangible Tunnel, Wizard's Leap, Wizard's Sidestep, and Watching Ward. A signal he has begun to organise his materials for a longer stay.
The Lotharingian Backdrop

Concurrent with the Valerian crisis: the covenant has intercepted correspondence indicating a Rhine/Normandy secession is brewing — the Lotharingian Tribunal proposal, likely to be raised at the Grand Tribunal in 1228. Florum and Spider's Palace support it; Montverte's position is unstated and would value the intercepted documents. The covenant has decided, for now, on non-intervention. Valdemar opposes giving up the current arrangement; others argue that autonomy may not persist regardless. Valerian's evidence and the Lotharingian documents constitute, together, a politically substantial portfolio. He has not yet committed it.

XI. Current Situation

As of Spring 1222 he is settled, nominally, at Sjórseiðr. The Adamant is his sanctum, mobile and deniable. In Autumn 1222 he copied a folio of lab texts — the first recorded activity since his arrival, and a signal he has begun to organise his materials for a longer stay.

What He Has Said

  • He is a former Quaesitor of the Rhine Tribunal in self-exile; "institutional issues" prevent his return.
  • He is technically still a Quaesitor but is "not allowed to practice."
  • His evidence concerns a sect of magi who have suborned the Bishopric of Liège.
  • He has knowledge that "could ruin a lot of people's day."
  • His library is largely confiscated contraband, offered in exchange for protection.
  • Clergy under the Bishopric of Liège are not to be trusted.

What His Sheet Tells Us

  • His Enemies flaw is major and tied to story — the imprisoning faction, presumed but not confirmed to be the suborners of Liège, remain in play.
  • His Infamous Reputation 4 and Judged Unfairly together indicate his name carries an active stain that he cannot wash out — this is consistent with a Quaesitor whose own Order has set him aside.
  • His Hermetic Prestige 4 simultaneously persists — within the Order, he is known and to some extent respected. He is famous and infamous at once, in different rooms.
  • His Restriction is unspecified and to be defined; its terms will materially shape his deployability.
