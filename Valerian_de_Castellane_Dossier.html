I. Dossier Overview

Valerian · Anno 1222

Valerian de Castellane is, by any practical measure, the most dangerous active Quaesitor in the Normandy Tribunal. He is not the most politically influential — that distinction belongs to magi who have spent their careers in Valnastium's corridors — but influence of that kind requires the cooperation of those being influenced. Valerian does not seek cooperation. He compels compliance, and he has the tools to do it.

Twenty-seven years since his gauntlet, Valerian has built a reputation that operates as a deterrent before he enters a room. The Normandy Tribunal's chief hoplite. Master of Terram, the art of stone and earth that requires patience, precision, and the willingness to be unmovable. Member of the Guernicus Terræ cult, a brotherhood of Quaesitores who believe the Code is bedrock — not to be interpreted, to be enforced. He has conducted eleven successful Wizard's Marches, personally arrested nine magi for Code violations, and his testimony has been pivotal in four Tribunal condemnations.

He is currently stationed at Sjórseiðr, the Norse-Hibernian covenant at the edge of the Rhine Tribunal's northern boundary. His vessel, the Adamant, is docked in the covenant's harbour. He came to investigate Rhine Tribunal irregularities and discovered something considerably worse than irregularities. He has not yet had the opportunity to report what he found.

The Code is not a guideline. It is not a tradition. It is not a courtesy extended by those who have power to those who do not. It is the wall that keeps the wolves from the sheep and the sheep from one another. I am part of that wall. I do not bend. — Attributed to Valerian, Tribunal session of 1215

The Gift's Shadow

Valerian's Blatant Gift is, operationally, a significant constraint. Mundane sources — merchants, servants, priests — react to his presence with immediate and unmistakeable unease, even hostility. He cannot charm his way through a gate or soften a Duke's suspicion with small talk. He moves through the mundane world like a stone falls through water: purposefully, directly, and leaving turbulence behind. He has never attempted to compensate for this. In his view, the Gift is a mark of what he is. Concealing it would be a form of deception he finds beneath him.

Terram Mastery · Stone Focus

Terram 23 (including Puissant +3) with a Major Focus in Stone and Earth means Valerian's Rego Terram combat total rivals that of specialist Flambeau magi. His focus doubles his applicable score for spells directly involving stone, earth, metal, and gem — giving him effective casting totals in the mid-50s for his signature effects. He moves stone as other men move air.

His Vim score of 15 is unusually high for a Terram specialist and reflects his professional obligations: Quaesitores must be able to detect, ward against, and destroy magical effects. His Perdo Vim work is the tool of his enforcement role.

Code of Hermes 5 — Operational Significance

A Code score of 5 is the ceiling of functional expertise — it represents not merely knowledge of the Code but the ability to argue it, find its margins, expose its ambiguities, and bend its interpretations to a desired outcome while remaining technically correct. Valerian uses this not to circumvent the Code but to ensure no one else can circumvent it around him. He has heard every argument for exception, exemption, and special circumstance. He has heard them all twice.

VI. Equipment & Resources

The Adamant

Valerian's vessel, currently docked at Sjórseiðr. A mid-sized trading cog, nominally Flemish-crewed, with a reinforced hold that has been quietly enchanted to resist both magical and physical intrusion. The name is intentional. She is not a fighting ship — she is a mobile laboratory annex, a floating evidence vault, and the fastest way to cross the Rhine Tribunal's jurisdictional boundaries without triggering the Code provisions on covenant-to-covenant transit. The crew know not to open certain doors.

Parma Magica

Parma 6 gives Valerian a Magic Resistance of approximately 36 before form bonuses — climbing to the high 50s when Terram effects are directed at him. Penetrating his Parma with Terram magic is functionally impossible for any magus he is likely to face in the field. His Parma covers his immediate companions when he chooses to extend it, which he does selectively and with explicit stated conditions.

VII. The Current Situation

Active Status — Sjórseiðr · Spring 1222

Valerian is present at Sjórseiðr following his escape from imprisonment at Breda, seat of the Duke of Brabant. He arrived with evidence of a Rhine Tribunal faction's infiltration of Church hierarchy in the Brabant/Rhine region — a potentially catastrophic Code violation that, if the Church discovered Hermetic involvement, would endanger the entire Order.

What He Knows

Rhine magi — associated with the Apple Gild faction and their proposal for a new Lotharingian Tribunal — have placed agents within the Church hierarchy of the Brabant and Rhine region. The goal is to facilitate the kind of open magical-mundane cooperation their proposed Tribunal would legalise. The means include forged documents, compromised mundane officials, and at least one failed apprentice (Magister Reynald de Grimbergen) who serves as a handler within the Duke of Brabant's court.

The trap that imprisoned him was elegant: the Rhine faction forged papal documents ordering the Duke to detain Valerian to "preserve his soul." The Duke, believing he acted on divine authority, sent agents into Flemish territory to seize him — a diplomatic provocation with its own potential for crisis. Valerian was held in Breda until a rescue operation freed him.

The Code of Hermes Paradox

Quod magus ordinis non debet se immiscere in rebus Ecclesiæ nisi in extremis, et quod qui detegit vim magicam hominibus mundanis, eos in periculum ponit et totum ordinem.

"A magus of the Order shall not involve himself in the affairs of the Church save in extremity, and he who reveals magical force to mundane men endangers them and the entire Order."

This is the paradox Valerian cannot solve alone. The Rhine infiltration endangers the Order if the Church discovers it. But exposing the infiltration also endangers the Order by drawing Church investigation. He cannot prosecute without proof. Proving it exists may reveal Hermetic magic to mundane investigators. His own House cannot easily rescue him from "Church charges" without appearing to prove the Rhine faction's claim that he is a dangerous agent.

He has allies, evidence, and the Adamant. He is waiting to see what shape the solution will take.

The Lotharingian Angle

Sjórseiðr holds intercepted documents regarding the Rhine secession plot — the proposal to create a new Lotharingian Tribunal with a "peripheral code" allowing greater mundane involvement. Valerian recognises that his Rhine infiltration evidence and these documents together constitute a comprehensive case against the Apple Gild faction. Separately, each is dangerous. Together, they are potentially ruinous — for the faction, and possibly for the stability of the Rhine Tribunal itself. He is calibrating how to use them.

VIII. Significant Relationships

The Duke of Brabant — Unwitting Instrument Resolved

The Duke acted in good faith on forged documents. He is a victim of the Rhine conspiracy, not a principal. Valerian does not consider him an enemy — he considers him a diplomatic liability who needs to be managed carefully. The Duke's awareness that he violated Flemish sovereignty is its own form of leverage, and Valerian is not above using leverage.

Magister Reynald de Grimbergen — Rhine Agent, Ducal Counsel Active Threat

The failed Guernicus apprentice who lost his Gift in a laboratory accident and was subsequently cultivated as a mundane asset by Rhine handlers. He presented the forged documents to the Duke and has spent a decade feeding Brabant political intelligence to his Hermetic masters. He knows everything about the conspiracy's mundane infrastructure. He is not dangerous by temperament — he prefers quiet resolutions — but he will become actively dangerous if the conspiracy is publicly threatened.

The Rhine Apple Gild Faction — Conspirators Hostile

The magi who ordered his imprisonment. They wanted him silenced before the 1228 Grand Tribunal, where their Lotharingian proposal will be heard. They have not yet made a second move against him, which suggests either confidence that his evidence is insufficient or uncertainty about the political fallout of an open confrontation with the Normandy Tribunal's chief hoplite.

Sjórseiðr Covenant — Current Hosts Cautious Alliance

He arrived here through necessity, not preference. His Blatant Gift makes extended cohabitation with covenfolk difficult. He is keeping his movements on the Adamant as much as possible and limiting his interactions with the covenant's sodales to what the situation requires. The intercepted Lotharingian documents are the reason for the cautious alliance: Sjórseiðr has information he needs, and he has authority they may find useful.

The Normandy Tribunal — Nominal Authority Complicated

His Tribunal would, in principle, support him. In practice: rescuing a Quaesitor from "Church charges" in Imperial territory is diplomatically catastrophic; he has made enemies in his twenty-seven years of enforcement; and some of those enemies would prefer he remain where he is. He is not naive about this. He is not waiting for institutional rescue.

Library Note — Quæsitorial Custody

Materials marked ⚠ are held under active Quaesitorial seal. Handling them without authorisation constitutes a Code violation. Several of the Diedne works are primary evidence in investigations that remain technically open pending a successor Quaesitor's review. The infernal texts have been partially annotated in Valerian's hand — not for use, but for identification, so that he can recognise their effects in the field. The distinction is one he would be prepared to defend before a Tribunal, at length.

X. Operational Assessment

What He Is

Valerian de Castellane is not a subtle man. He has never needed to be. His Blatant Gift makes subtlety structurally difficult; his temperament makes it personally uninteresting. He approaches the Code the way he approaches stone: it is what it is, it yields to force and precision, and anyone who tells you otherwise is selling something.

What he is, operationally, is extraordinary. A Rego Terram total in the mid-50s with a Wizard's Grimoire that covers mobility (Seven-League Stride), reach (Intangible Tunnel), trapping (Watching Ward, Arcane Connection), suppression (Disenchant, Demon's Eternal Oblivion), and non-lethal arrest (Call to Slumber, stone restraints) makes him arguably the most self-sufficient field operative in the Normandy Tribunal. He has never needed to be rescued before. He finds the experience instructive.

What He Wants

To deliver his evidence to a Tribunal that can act on it. To see the Rhine faction answer for the Church infiltration before the 1228 Grand Tribunal gives their Lotharingian proposal any legitimacy. To do this without triggering the very Church crisis he was trying to prevent. He is aware these goals are in tension.

He is also — though he would not express it this way — grateful. Someone came to Breda for him. He has been practicing alone for a very long time.

Stone does not negotiate. Stone does not apologise. Stone simply is, and it remains after everything softer has been worn away. — From Fundamentum Firmum, by Valerian of Guernicus; his own treatise on Terram in combat

Likely Trajectories

  • As Ally: A grateful Quaesitor with his level of authority and resources is a political asset that most covenants would consider generational. He does not forget debts, in either direction.
  • As Enemy: He is patient, he has Arcane Connections, and he knows the Code better than almost anyone in the Order. Making an enemy of him is not a quick problem.
  • As Wild Card: If the Rhine crisis forces him to choose between procedural correctness and preventing a catastrophic outcome, it is not clear which way he breaks. He has spent his career believing those two things are the same. They may not be.
