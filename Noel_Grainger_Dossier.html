I. The Man Who Became Noel

Nathan Grainger spent the first thirty-five years of his life loading furniture trucks. A disqualifying physical condition kept him out of the service his friend Jeremy returned from with a Purple Heart — and instead of letting that close a door, Nathan doubled down: more flag, more country, more grievance. He felt permanently stuck on the bottom rung. He worked, he drank, he watched men he envied get medals and mortgages.

The Embrace cracked the ceiling. For the first time, he could feel possibilities — power, status, a future that wasn't another shift. The trouble is he doesn't yet know what those words cost. He sees in his sire, Helmuth, the same small-time, bottom-rung thinking he spent decades resenting in himself, and he is determined not to be handicapped the same way. What he intends to do with the room he's clearing remains, charitably, undefined.

Helmuth was not a patient teacher. Not everyone on the crew was even turned — Noel was just one of the bodies that got upgraded. Early on, on a job, Noel got excitable, nearly blew the heist, nearly blew the Masquerade in front of mortals and the gang both. The beating he took afterward was educational; it was also the closest thing to instruction he ever received from his sire.

VI. Predator Type · Cleaver

Noel feeds covertly from people who know him as Nathan's surviving relatives: his sister and her two sons, his brother and his daughter, friends like Jeremy from the old neighborhood. He's not violent about it. A late visit, a beer, an arm around the shoulder, a cleanly placed bite, a few minutes of Cloud Memory, and gone before sunrise. They think he's a good uncle who keeps strange hours.

The arrangement is sustainable, deniable, and — by his lights — moral. They love him. He has not, yet, had to look very hard at what it means that they are also his pantry.

XI. Campaign Logbook · "Not the Only Dead"

Session 1 — The Fire, the Jar, the House

Marcus called; bring the truck. Giant fire. A man with a jar. Three Kindred and a fourth man in street clothes — Chenoe, Native American, who laughs and says dragons don't exist in a tone that means but other things do. Locked into a Camarilla house with a murdering monster; the coalition came out and the monster did not. The exposition arrives in pieces: hundreds of years ago, Shoshone and Ute hands sealed prisons across the land — bound things they could not control, weapons they had built and could not unmake. Four such sites; three strongholds; one is already open. "We sealed these things because we can't control them; they are feral and wild."

A century ago, a Kindred opened the first prison. Now someone is opening them on purpose, resurrecting bound things with their own blood. Hundreds, already.

Chenoe leaves his number and the offer of advice on wards. The crew now holds a Rank 2 Haven and a Rank 2 Library — a bookstore. The library window does not stay intact long: three mudbirds come through it. Noel ends the night Tuesday, 15 of 17 rounds left in the M17, and 5 XP earned. (Status: unspent.)

Scene I · The Trunk to Montana

Long haul north. The crew brought the Malkavian along. The Malkavian, in the way of Malkavians, would not stop talking — could not, perhaps; would not, certainly. Noel made the offer once, and again, and then a third time with specificity. The Malkavian did not take the offer.

Noel made good on the words. The Malkavian rode the last several hours to Montana folded into the trunk of the car, undead and silent at last. The crew did not argue. The tagline on this folder is a direct quotation.

Session 3 — The Crow, the Puppeteer, Pointing at Yellowstone

On the Crow reservation. Something rears up and points — north, toward Yellowstone — and the meaning arrives in the gesture, not in words. Birds come on the wind. Three of them, and one is the puppeteer, the others on its strings; the strings snap and the puppeteer runs. Noel hurls two of the rest into the shield they came to break; the shield cracks; they turn to dust. The last bird-thing tries to leap to the river and is denied.

The history surfaces. The bird people were tools, made by Native hands to be turned on their enemies — and then, as such tools do, refused to remain pointed in the intended direction. The Crow elders send the crew south. To the Utes. To Fort Duchesne.

Scene II · Fort Duchesne · The Blood the Ritual Needed

The Ute elders had agreed. They gave what blood they could give without anyone dying — every adult bled to the line and not past it. It should have been enough. It wasn't: one of the crew frenzied, fed from the gathered vessels, and left the ritual short.

The only remaining source on the reservation was the children. The GM framed this as the dilemma: which line does the crew cross. The Ute adults stood firm. They had given everything they were willing to give.

Noel did not cross the line. Noel called the bluff. He turned it back on the elders, quietly and without theatrics: why on earth wouldn't you be willing to die to protect the children? The trucker neonate, the man who never made it past the warehouse floor, the cleaver of his own family — speaking that sentence in that room.

A small number of the Ute adults bled themselves to death and finished the ritual.

Finale — Denver Airport, and What Was Under the Ground

Marcus took the Camarilla. The crew took the shield at Denver Airport — the second prison. Down to where the Nosferatu were first encountered, but the place no longer looked like the place; the geography had become argument, an illusion actively maintained. Derek began reading the earth itself; Noel kept up with him, step for step, deep beneath the airport.

Then his Hunger detonated. Derek slipped his grip. The shield came off Noel's back. The terracotta warriors took a messy critical on the way down. By the end of the descent, Noel was no longer entirely a passenger inside himself, and not entirely the same thing that had loaded the truck on Tuesday.

The campaign closes here. The prisons that remain remain. Helmuth is still teaching the wrong lessons. The Herd still doesn't know what Uncle Noel really is.
