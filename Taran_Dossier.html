♣ Who He Is ♣

Taran · with his Halberd Key

Taran is a Boggart, which means he is made of much the same substance as the house itself — its stubbornness, its weight, its refusal to simply fall over when things get difficult. Boggarts are not graceful creatures, not in the courtly sense. What they are is present — reliably, inconveniently, exactly-where-you-need-them present — whenever something needs holding up or holding back.

He is a Soldier, First Class. The rank was earned; it did not arrive with his folk. He is posted to the Hearth — the warm, lit, central place from which the household takes its heat — and he has served it long enough to be trusted with a Halberd Key, which is both a weapon and an emblem of an older order, when the great gates of the Hearth were kept by soldiers who could fight at the threshold and open the lock if they had to.

He has no coins and a grappling hook. His prologue memory reads like something scratched into the inside cover of a soldier's manual: Being scared is part of being brave. He means it. He earned it from rats.

The War Package, Considered

Fight 3 + Master at Arms (free re-roll on chosen weapon and all combat rolls) + Braveheart (+1 to courage and will) + Endure Pain (remove a Condition) is a coherent argument: he fights well, re-rolls when it matters, holds under adverse conditions, and ignores what the fight has cost him. He is most effective when things are already going badly. This is the deliberate design of a character who has thought about what he is for.

♥ His Contract ♥

The contract is Hereditary. Taran did not negotiate it across a table. It arrived with him, written into whatever Boggarts are made of. The Hearth is both his homeland and his obligation. He is dear to it — an old-fashioned word meaning both beloved and expensive, which is appropriate.

Concession: Juggernaut

Once Per Session

Taran can go Juggernaut. He grows — up to twice his size — and his muscles go hard as numismatic metal (the brass and copper of the household's coins). While Juggernauting: War rolls are Helpful; all other actions are Hindered; +1 to all Action and Reaction Rolls in combat; all Conditions are ignored. Lasts up to 5 turns, or he can stop voluntarily. On ending: he loses one Decorum level (the clothes do not survive the expansion) and gains Tired if he doesn't already have it.

At Decorum 3, the Juggernaut costs him a step he cannot easily recover. It is a last resort and a final word. It is also — this matters for understanding Taran — the fear turned inside out and made into mass. The thing that makes the body want to flee, converted into force. He has done this. He will do it again if the moment requires it.

Counterpart: Word of Honor

If a Boggart makes an explicit promise and does not keep it, Terrible, Terrible Things follow. This is not metaphorical. The household has views on broken oaths, and those views have weight and consequences and a memory that does not clear easily.

He does not say I promise lightly. When he says it, the words cost something. He makes sure they do. He makes sure they should. — On Boggart Word of Honor and its keepers

Watch for the word promise. When it appears in his mouth, so does the full weight of the Hearth behind it.

♣ Traits & Move ♣

Master at Arms
Trait  ·  Free Re-Roll
Choose a melee weapon — certainly the Halberd Key, which he carries in the illustration. All rolls involving that weapon get a free re-roll, and so do all Action and Reaction Rolls in combat. Not dueling weapons or keysketes; those are far too fine for his purposes, and he would bend them. The restriction is not a restriction for someone built the way he is built.
Braveheart
Trait
+1 when courage or strong will is required. This is not lucky. Braveheart is what Will at 3 looks like when it has been made into a deliberate commitment. The bonus applies at precisely the worst moments — which is when a character defined by this axiom is most himself. He is not a different person when things are frightening. He is the same person, acknowledged by the rules.
Endure Pain ♣
Move  ·  War Field  ·  ♣
Remove a single Condition from himself. Not Broken — some damage does not yield to willpower alone. Everything else: Embarrassed, Confused, Hurt, Frightened, Tired, Sick, Poisoned. He takes the Condition, notes it, and puts it somewhere it cannot reach his hands. The move is not dramatic. You notice he did it after the fact, usually because something that should have stopped him didn't.
The Three Together

Master at Arms + Braveheart + Endure Pain = a fighter who is most dangerous when already damaged and already frightened. Most fighters become less effective under those conditions. Taran becomes the reason the others haven't run yet. This is what the selection is describing.

♦ Arms & Effects ♦

He travels practical. Nothing without a use. The Halberd Key is not decorative, even if it looks it from the outside.

Halberd Key
Weapon  ·  Melee  ·  Two-Handed, Heavy, Reach Weapon  ·  Price 3
A polearm with a key-bit at one extremity — long, heavy, a reach weapon that keeps the wielder at the edge of engagements and sweeps a corridor very thoroughly. Once standard issue for the battalions that kept the Hearth's gates, back when those gates were famous and the soldiers who held them were expected to fight and to open the lock. Now carried mostly by honour guards. Taran carries it because it is his weapon under Master at Arms, because it suits the post, and because the history of it seems to suit him.
Grappling Hook & Line
General Item  ·  Price 2
The Hearth has heights, and the household has depths. A grappling hook is the equipment of a soldier who has been somewhere inconveniently vertical and had to get someone else out. His Exploration 2 supports this story: he has thought about the passages between here and there, and has planned for the parts without stairs.
No coins. No wealth noted. The First Class designation is a rank, not a salary. He guards the Hearth because the Hearth needs guarding.

♥ Soul & Disposition ♥

The question one asks of a soldier is not what he can do — the sheet answers that. The question is why he keeps doing it. What sustains a Boggart First Class past the encounter with the rats, past the long night watches, past the session where the thing behind the wall turned out to be bigger than expected and the legs had opinions about the direction of the corridor?

For Taran, the answer is not glory — he has no coins, which means no one is paying him enough for glory to be the point. It is not ideology, precisely. The Hearth is not abstract to him; it is an actual place, warm and lit, full of actual beings who gathered there because it is supposed to be safe. His job is to make it safe. He does not especially enjoy all parts of the job. He also does not leave it.

On Rats and the Education They Provide

He has rescued people from rats. Not metaphorical rats. The household kind — real, numerous, a meaningful problem at this scale. This is where the prologue lesson was distilled. Rats are not the worst thing in the household. They are merely real, and persistent, and what they threaten is often small and without defences of its own. A First Class soldier taking a rat problem seriously is not beneath his dignity. He understood this before he understood much else about the work.

He was frightened during the rat rescues. He has decided to remember this accurately, rather than revising the memory into something more flattering. The fear was there. He moved through it. The acting-anyway is what is worth keeping — and the fear is part of it, because if you remove the fear you have made the courage mean less, and Taran is not interested in meaning less.

How He Speaks

Charm 1. He is not a charmer. He is there to be reliable, which is different from being liked, and he has made his peace with that distinction. He is slow to make explicit commitments, because the Word of Honor shapes the habits of his speech — he checks, before speaking, whether he means what he is about to say. When he has checked, and then says it, it holds.

Grace 3 does more of his communicating than his words do. The body says: I have been here before. I am still here. This is not the worst thing I have stood in front of.

What the Aces Say

He holds the Club and the Diamond — War in reserve, and careful thought in reserve. The absent Heart and Spade are what he has already spent. He is not reckless with his resources. He is prepared for what he does not yet know is coming, which is the only kind of preparation that matters.

♠ The Prologue Memory ♠

Being scared is part of being brave. — Taran's Prologue Memory, as he himself recorded it

The Prologue Memory is the first truth the character chose to carry into play. It is not borrowed. It is the lesson extracted from experience — from the rats, the watches, the moment when the thing was larger than he had planned for — and kept deliberately, because it is accurate and because accurate lessons are worth keeping even when they are uncomfortable.

The lesson is not that fear should be managed or suppressed or overcome. The lesson is that fear is part of the process, inseparable from the act it precedes. You cannot be brave without something to be frightened of. The frightening thing was there. He was frightened. He did the thing anyway. Strip the fear from the account and you have not improved the story — you have falsified it.

This is why Braveheart is not, for Taran, a lucky trait. It is a practice: courage as a repeatable action, taken in the presence of fear, which is the only place courage can actually be taken.

The chapters after the Prologue are blank. He is early. The records have not yet caught up with what comes next.

A Note on What Follows

A character whose central axiom is about the relationship between fear and bravery will be tested on exactly that axis. The household produces things to be frightened of. It always has. The Halberd Key is heavy, the grappling hook is coiled, two aces are still in his sleeve, and the First Class rank was earned and will have to keep being earned. He is ready — or as ready as anyone gets — which is to say: he is frightened, and he is going anyway.
