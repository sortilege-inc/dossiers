Portentous Birth
fire Interpersonal, Spiritual Celestial Realms · p.89

The childbirth assistant carried the silent bundle to the priest outside of the birthing chamber. The child’s grandmother followed with a stony look on her face.

Why isn’t the child crying? the priest thought.

“Show him,” the grandmother said.

The assistant carefully unwrapped the bundle. The unnerving infant stared about, clear-eyed, as the grandmother pointed out the seven moles in a perfect circle on their ear.

The following apply to a character with the Portentous Birth distinction:

  • - You are known among your clan or family group to have been born under blessed circumstances or have had some sort of mark or other indication of greatness bestowed upon you by the Heavens. When you interact with someone who knows of your portentous birth and believes in its power, they treat you favorably and may be inclined to give you special privileges as long as you do not give them a reason to treat you otherwise.
  • - When performing a check for which your fortuitous birth is an asset (such as a Composition check to craft a clever request to someone who believes in the legacy of your birth or a Theology check to theorize about an omen), you may reroll up to two dice.

Generosity
water Interpersonal Core · p.113

During the sixth month of the year after the biannual collection of taxes, the city of Ryokō Owari Toshi celebrates the Days of Generosity. As the festival begins, the head priest of the local temple draws a name in a lottery. The person whose name is drawn becomes “King of Generosity” and “rules” over the festival.

The festival is marked by gift giving and drunkenness. The festival king decides who the best gift giver is on each of the celebration’s three days. That person receives a portion of the “king’s purse,” a collection of coins given to the festival king to pay him for blessing the giver with a prosperous year ahead.

The following apply to a character with the Generosity passion:

  • - You can always identify a proper gift for a particular person that does not risk giving offense or slighting them publicly.
  • - After performing a check to acquire or give a gift (such as a Design check to select an item that complements someone’s wardrobe or a Commerce check to find and select a gift at a market), you remove 3 strife.

人物像 The Person Within Jinbutsu-zō

What sets them apart

While everyone around her recognizes Yukiko as a prodigy, she herself is grounded and practical, treating her intuitive grasp of the elements and the void matter-of-factly and unworthy of comment.

What they had to learn

When Isawa Ujina adopted her, it was mostly to provide safety and structure, and the power of the Isawa family and the Phoenix clan. But custody of Yukiko was immediately handed over to Gensho, an abbot from the Brotherhood of Shinsei. He treated Yukiko as a peer, helping her learn but also holding her accountable.

A defining success

At age six Yukiko performed the "Sight Beyond Existence" inversion for the first time. She did it without difficulty, and explained the method to the astonished shugenja that observed her in terms that appeared to be quoting the Little Teacher; something she was unfamiliar with. It was this action that saw her adopted into the Isawa and formally intitiated in the ishiken school.

A defining difficulty

She was a foundling, and was raised by a woman named Tomoe in a small river village. Tomoe found Yukiko's mannerisms and pronouncements strange but charming. This humble upbringing gave Yukiko a grounded perspective that clashes with Phoenix clan expectations. She doesn't understand why people make such a fuss over her abilities, speaks too casually with superiors, and has peasant habits (like eating with her hands or preferring to sit on the floor) that horrify the clan's more traditionalist members. Some Phoenix nobles see her common upbringing as a stain on her destiny, though Yukiko only remembers Tomoe's kindness and doesn't understand why anyone cares.

What calms them

What truly brings Yukiko joy is the simple act of giving—whether it's sharing her taiyaki with younger students, using her void sight to help someone find a lost item, or teaching others what comes so naturally to her. She doesn't see her gifts as something to hoard or leverage for status; she genuinely wants to help people and feels happiest when she can make someone else's burden lighter. This manifests in both grand gestures (once she spent weeks helping a lesser shugenja understand a technique they struggled with) and tiny ones (she anonymously leaves small paper cranes she's folded in places where sad people sit). Her generosity is so instinctive that she's often confused when people react with suspicion or try to repay her—in her mind, having more than you need means you should share.

What worries them

Despite being constantly surrounded by teachers, minders, and observers, Yukiko is profoundly lonely. Other children are frightened by her abilities or resentful of the attention she receives. Adults treat her either as a sacred relic to be preserved or a phenomenon to be studied—never as a person. She has her doll Hana, but Hana doesn't talk back. She has memories from her past life, but those belong to someone else. Sometimes, late at night, she cries silently because she can't remember the last time someone just... talked to her. Just talked, the way Tomoe used to, about nothing important. This loneliness makes her desperately attached to the few people who show her genuine warmth, and terrified of losing them.

At first sight

Yukiko's eyes are grey, and at moments will flash entirely white.

Under stress

When she is under extreme stress, she will seem to dissociate. At times she will speak, but with words seemingly not her own. She often does not recall what was said during these moments.

Notable relations

Isawa Ujina - Her formal adoptive father; views her as his duty and legacy, though they have little personal warmth between them. He provided a finger of jade to assist with spiritual protection. Abbot Gensho - Her true mentor and guardian from the Brotherhood of Shinsei; treats her as a person first, prodigy second. Tomoe - The peasant woman who raised her from infancy to age six; Yukiko still loves her and doesn't understand why she can't visit.

Standing with their clan

Yukiko sees Righteousness as being what each situation demands; the idea of firm rules for morality are non-sensical to her.

In their parents' eyes

Tomoe saw past the eerie pronouncements to the little girl who needed bedtime stories and who cried when birds fell from their nests. When the Isawa family came to claim Yukiko after her demonstration of void techniques, Tomoe wept but didn't fight it—she'd always known the child was destined for something beyond a river village. She still prays to the Fortunes that someone, somewhere, is making sure her little Yuki-chan is eating properly.

How they will die

Yukiko doesn't concern herself with death. She just hopes that, in the next life, she was go a little longer before discovery.
