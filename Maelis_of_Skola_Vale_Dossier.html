Maelis of Skola Vale

A recluse-witch in a serpent-haunted valley; Pharika visited her there, and the visions came afterward — in a language she did not learn but somehow understands.

Stage of Career

Bioα · Vita

Maelis retreated from society after a life-altering event — she has not said what it was, and her isolation has only deepened her sense that ordinary people are weak, and that meddling in their affairs causes more trouble than it solves. Skola Vale is her home now: a hard valley, dry-grassed and edged with serpent-haunted rocks, where she keeps her own counsel and her own garden. Pharika visited her there. Maelis learned the goddess's secrets from a talking serpent — and ever since, dark thoughts have moved in her like a second pulse. She believes everyone dies. She believes their brief lives may as well be put to use.

The visions came afterward. She did not ask for them. They come anyway, in Celestial — a language she understands without having been taught — and Pharika sometimes speaks through her in a voice that is not quite her own. She is an oracle by nature now, whatever she was before, and Pharika's will moves through her into the world.

Feat: Ritual Caster (Cleric list, Wis-based)

  • Class chosen: Cleric. Spellcasting ability = Wisdom.
  • Starts with a ritual book containing two 1st-level ritual spells from the chosen class's list.
    • Starting rituals: Ceremony, Purify Food and Drink.
  • Can only cast these as rituals (cast time +10 min, no slot). Cannot cast normally.
  • Can add new rituals: Cleric list, ritual tag, max spell level = your level / 2 round up. Cost: 2 hours and 50 gp per spell level.

Supernatural Gift — Oracle (Pharika's voice)θ · Dóron

  • Ears of the Oracle: You speak, read, and write Celestial, the language of the gods. Pharika may deliver a message through you — in your voice, or letting hers come through in any language you speak.
  • Oracle's Insight: When you make an ability check, you can roll a d10 and add it to the total. Wait to decide until after the d20 but commit before the DM declares success/failure. 1/short or long rest.
  • Oracle's Piety: Your oracle traits replace Pharika's normal piety benefits — following the Augur → Seer → Sibyl → Divine Oracle progression. Wisdom is the spellcasting ability.

Oracle's Piety REPLACES Pharika's normal piety benefits. Maelis does NOT have Pharika's standard Devotee ray of sickness trait. At Piety 3+ she casts augury as a ritual instead — see the Patron God panel.

Why Maelis is on the Expeditionο · Hodós

Pharika in the Famine. Maelis's isolation and connection to Pharika could make her privy to alchemical secrets or curses causing the grain's failure. Her fatalistic worldview might lead her to use the crisis as an opportunity to gain influence, even at the expense of others.

The Fallow Year is exactly the kind of cosmic wound Pharika feeds on — every dying field, every desperate alchemical bargain, every person turning to a serpent-cult for answers they can't get from Karametra. Maelis is positioned to thrive in it, and to learn things from it.
