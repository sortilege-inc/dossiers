A.M.O.S. was a player character in Above All Else, Love, a short Exandria-set campaign that concluded after fewer than ten sessions. This dossier records the character as he stood at the campaign's close. Mechanically a warforged with the Duelist class (drawn from Adventures in Rokugan); narratively, a pre-Calamity Aeorian bodyguard reactivated into the world of Critical Role's Campaign 2 era.

I. Concept & Function

A.M.O.S. was built in pre-Calamity Aeor as the dedicated guardian of the scion of a noble family of powerful wizards. His specialization was narrow and specific: when magical threats came for his charge, he was the one who closed the distance and ended them, ideally in a single decisive strike. He is a weapon designed for the moment of intercepted contact, not for prolonged engagement.

At some point before the Calamity, A.M.O.S. went inert. The circumstances are not part of what this dossier records. He was reactivated by the party of Above All Else, Love roughly a thousand years later, in the era contemporaneous with Critical Role's second campaign. The civilization that built him no longer exists. His charge is gone. His operational context is gone. The discipline remains.

He is functioning. He is also confused, in the quiet, professional way of someone whose every assumption about how the world ought to be arranged has turned out to be no longer applicable.

The purpose is unchanged. The threat parameters have expanded considerably. // In-character framing of the displacement

II. Constructed Nature

As an Aeormaton — mechanically resolved as a warforged — A.M.O.S. has a body built for endurance rather than maintenance. He does not eat, drink, breathe, sleep, or require biological upkeep. When he takes a long rest, he enters an inactive, motionless state for at least six hours; he is not unconscious, and he can see and hear normally throughout. He is immune to disease, has advantage on saves against being poisoned, and has resistance to poison damage. Magic cannot put him to sleep.

His Integrated Protection grants a +1 to Armor Class. Armor, when worn, must be incorporated into his body over an hour of contact, and similarly removed over an hour; while he lives, his armor cannot be removed from him against his will. (He is currently not wearing armor — his Duelist defensive style makes use of unarmored Evasive Footwork instead.)

His eyes are mirrored. He is six feet three inches tall and weighs three hundred and twenty pounds. His age is not recorded; the question is harder for him than for most.

III. On the Present World

A.M.O.S. is naive about the era he has woken into, and the naivety has a specific shape. He is not unfamiliar with danger, magic, politics, or violence — he has handled all of those before. What disorients him is the simplicity of present-day infrastructure relative to what he remembers.

The concrete example, returned to in play more than once: in Aeor, buildings did not burn down because someone forgot to account for fire. Automatic magical fire extinguishment was a baseline civic feature. The idea that a structure could exist without such provision strikes him as a kind of administrative oversight that ought to have a responsible party. The fact that no such party exists — that the world simply does not, currently, do this — is something he is still recalibrating around.

He does not voice the disorientation often. When he does, it tends to come out as a question, asked with no particular edge, that nonetheless lands as a critique of the entire civilizational moment. His companions found this funny. He did not intend it to be funny.

IV. On the Lawbearer

A.M.O.S. is a follower of the Lawbearer. His devotion is real — he considers himself genuinely her servant, and he tries to comport himself as one — but it is also, importantly, the kind of devotion that prefers a respectful distance. He does not want to be acknowledged. He does not want to be noticed. The relationship he wants with his god is the relationship a competent professional wants with the founder of their trade: deep reverence, careful observance, and absolutely no personal attention.

The analogy that gets at it: being personally befriended by a billionaire celebrity. There is nothing wrong with the celebrity, and nothing wrong with you, but the scale of the relationship is wrong, and the asymmetry will reshape everything around it. He does not want that. He worships sincerely so that he doesn't have to want that.

Over the course of the campaign, A.M.O.S. did see gods. He avoided direct interaction with them. The party — most of whom found the proximity to divinity thrilling — generally misread his reluctance, or simply did not understand it. The one party member who shared his worship of the Lawbearer was the one he eventually attempted to explain the distinction to: that being devout and wanting acknowledgment are not the same thing, and that in his case they actively oppose each other.

I am devout. That is entirely different from wanting to be noticed. // To the party's other follower of the Lawbearer

VI. Dueling Techniques & Stances

A.M.O.S. fights as a Duelist (Adventures in Rokugan). His combat resolves around focus points — spent to drive decisive blows, generated by his stance and his readiness. Below are his three techniques and three stances as recorded.

Martial Techniques

Dawn's Grace Strike 1 Attack
Focus Cost: 1+  ·  Range: Weapon's range  ·  Weapon: Must be wielding a weapon
Make a weapon attack against a creature in range, adding an amount equal to the focus points spent to the attack roll. On hit, the target suffers damage equal to weapon damage + ability modifier + focus spent. Bonus: If at least 3 focus points were spent, the weapon counts as magical for overcoming resistances and immunities for this attack. If you miss, regain 1 focus point.
Rising Blade 1 Reaction
Focus Cost: 3  ·  Range: Weapon's range  ·  Duration: Instantaneous  ·  Required Weapon: Any sheathed melee weapon
After a creature chooses you as a target for an attack but before it rolls, spend your reaction to draw your weapon and make a melee attack roll against that creature. On a hit, it takes your weapon's damage + 1d4 damage for every 5 feet it has moved this turn. Bonus: If you use a slashing weapon, the creature must make its attack roll with disadvantage.
Iron Grip 1 Attack
Focus Cost: 3  ·  Range: Touch  ·  Mandatory Movement: 10 feet  ·  Required Weapon: Unarmed
Make an unarmed attack with advantage against a creature in range. On hit, deal damage equal to your unarmed damage + ability modifier and subject the creature to the grappled condition. Bonus: Each Large creature you hit suffers the prone condition; each Huge or Gargantuan creature also suffers the maimed condition (−10 feet of speed and disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws, removed after it regains HP) until the end of your next turn.

Combat Stances

Heron Stance
Stance Effects: +1 bonus to Armor Class. After a creature you can perceive misses you with an attack, spend your reaction to have it suffer the marked for death condition (your next successful melee attack against it deals an additional 1d8 force damage) for 1 minute. Focus Generation: At the end of your turn, if you dealt 10 or more damage to any one creature with a single weapon attack, gain 2 additional focus points. If you dealt 20 or more damage, gain 3 instead.
Swallow Stance
Stance Effects: Attacks of opportunity made against you have disadvantage. When attacking a creature that has expended all of its reactions, you gain a bonus to damage equal to your proficiency bonus. Focus Generation: At the end of your turn, gain 1 additional focus point for each opportunity attack that missed you this turn.
Viper Stance
Stance Effects: You have advantage (a +5 bonus) on your passive Perception score and on Wisdom saving throws. When a negative condition you are suffering would impose disadvantage on any check, saving throw, or attack roll, you make that roll without advantage or disadvantage. Focus Generation: At the end of your turn, gain 2 additional focus points if you are suffering at least one negative condition or have at least one danger die assigned to you, and 1 additional focus point per condition or danger die beyond the first.

VII. Class, Racial & Feat Features

Duelist (Class)

FeatureFunction
Evasive Footwork While not wearing armor and not wielding a shield, AC = 10 + Dex modifier + chosen mental ability modifier (A.M.O.S. uses Wisdom). Additionally, while dueling, +2 bonus to AC against any attack made by a creature not participating in the duel.
Lethal Instinct After the staredown at the start of a duel, gain focus points equal to the number of danger dice assigned to you, to a maximum of your duelist level or proficiency bonus (whichever is lower). Once per short or long rest.
Challenge of Steel Once on your turn as a free action, issue a challenge to a creature that can perceive you. The target makes a Wisdom save (DC 8 + proficiency + Charisma modifier). On a success, it chooses whether to enter the duel. On a failure, it must duel you and starts with danger dice equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down). Once per won duel or short/long rest.
Extra Attack Attack twice when taking the Attack action.
Decisive Blade When performing a finishing blow against an opponent and rolling that opponent's assigned danger dice, you may reroll all dice with a result of 1 or 2 and must use the new result, even if it is also a 1 or 2. The additional dice cannot be rerolled.

Aeormaton (Race / Warforged)

FeatureFunction
Constructed Resilience Advantage on saves against being poisoned, resistance to poison damage, no need to eat/drink/breathe, immunity to disease, no need to sleep, immunity to magical sleep.
Sentry's Rest On a long rest, must spend at least six hours in an inactive, motionless state rather than sleeping. Appears inert but is not unconscious — can see and hear normally.

Feats

FeatFunction
Sentinel When you hit a creature with an opportunity attack, the creature's speed becomes 0 for the rest of the turn. Creatures provoke opportunity attacks from you even if they take the Disengage action. When a creature within reach attacks a target other than you (who lacks this feat), you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against the attacker.
Mage Slayer When a creature within 5 feet of you casts a spell, you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against it. When you damage a creature concentrating on a spell, it has disadvantage on its concentration save. Advantage on saving throws against spells cast by creatures within 5 feet of you.

The choice of feats is in keeping with his designed function: closing on magical threats and ending them physically before they can complete their work. Both feats reinforce the original purpose for which he was constructed.

VIII. Equipment & Armament

Retort — Katana, Legendary (Requires Attunement)

Forged pre-Calamity. The weapon was created with and for A.M.O.S.

  • +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon.
  • Defensive. While holding the longsword, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.
  • Iajutsu. When you draw this longsword as part of the first attack you make on your first turn in combat and hit a creature, the katana deals an extra 3d8 damage.
  • You Are Already Dead. When you attack a Large or smaller creature with this katana and roll a 20 on the attack roll, you cut straight through the creature, bisecting and slaying it. A creature is immune to this effect if it is immune to slashing damage, is formless, amorphous, or incorporeal, can regenerate, has legendary actions, or if the DM otherwise decides the creature cannot be killed by bisection. Such a creature instead takes an extra 3d8 slashing damage from the hit.

IX. Notable Relations

The Scion of the Wizard House — his original charge, pre-Calamityhistorical
A.M.O.S. was assigned to protect the scion of a noble family of powerful wizards. He was not a household guard or a generalist sentinel; he was that one person's bodyguard, with a function calibrated specifically to the kind of threats a high-status magical heir attracts. The scion's name, the family's name, and the eventual fate of either are not part of what this dossier records. The relationship is the foundational fact of his design and the absent center of his current operation.
The Lawbearer — patron deity, observed at a deliberate distancedevout · prefers anonymity
His worship of the Lawbearer is genuine. So is his preference that the relationship remain one-directional. He does his work. He keeps his agreements. He honors the compact between guardian and charge — a compact the Lawbearer presides over. He hopes, in the manner of someone who believes hope and observance are connected, that this will be sufficient and will not require further attention.
The Party of Above All Else, Love — companions during the campaign's runactive during campaign
The party found and reactivated A.M.O.S., and for the duration of the campaign he traveled with them. The campaign concluded after fewer than ten sessions; what they were and what they accomplished as a group is preserved in the play of those sessions rather than reduced here. Individual party members are not catalogued in this dossier, with one specific exception.
The Other Lawbearer Worshipper in the Party — shared faith, contrasting orientationco-religious
One member of the party shared A.M.O.S.'s worship of the Lawbearer. The party as a whole was excited when A.M.O.S. saw gods during the campaign — they read this as a kind of distinction, as proximity to importance. He did not, and the conversation in which he attempted to explain why happened with this character. The distinction he was trying to make: devotion and the desire to be acknowledged are different things, and in his case they actively oppose each other.
