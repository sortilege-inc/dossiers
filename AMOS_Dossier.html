I. Operational Overview

A.M.O.S. is a pre-Calamity Aeormaton — one of the masterwork constructs produced in Aeor at the peak of that civilization's arcane engineering — purpose-built to serve as the dedicated physical guardian of a high-status magical family. His role was specifically defined: when magical threats materialized, he was the one who closed the distance and ended them. Not through prolonged engagement, not through siege, but through the discipline of the single decisive strike. He is a blade that never needed to raise its voice.

He went inert before the Calamity. The exact circumstances are not recorded in any surviving document he has been able to access. When he was reactivated — roughly contemporaneous with the events of what the current inhabitants of Exandria apparently call "Campaign 2," a designation he finds unhelpfully imprecise — the world he emerged into bore virtually no resemblance to the one he last knew.

He is managing.

The purpose is unchanged. The threat parameters have expanded considerably. // A.M.O.S., upon being briefed on the current state of the world

A.M.O.S. operates with complete professional competence and a constant, low-level bewilderment at the infrastructure choices of the current era. He does not speak of the bewilderment aloud unless directly asked. He simply notes, internally and occasionally in a clipped aside, that in the civilization that built him, buildings did not burn down because someone failed to account for fire suppression. He does not consider this complaint. He considers it a professional observation.

II. Constructed Nature

As an Aeormaton — mechanically resembling what the current era calls a warforged, a term A.M.O.S. accepts as a rough functional approximation without endorsing its implications — he enjoys a body built for endurance rather than elegance. He does not eat, drink, breathe, sleep, or require the biological maintenance that occupies so much of his companions' day. When he rests, he enters an inactive motionless state for six hours. He is fully aware during this time. He simply does not move.

His integrated protection — built-in defensive layering, not external armor in the traditional sense — includes a flat bonus to his Armor Class. He cannot be stripped of his armor against his will. He has advantage against poison effects and resistance to poison damage. He is immune to disease. He will not be put to sleep by magic. These are not boasts. They are engineering specifications.

His eyes are mirrored. This is a design choice. His constructors had opinions about psychological impact in confrontation.

IV. The Dissonance Problem

A.M.O.S. was built for and by a civilization that treated the management of everyday hazards as an engineering problem to be solved, not a personal responsibility to be navigated. Aeor's arcane infrastructure was extensive. Buildings had automatic fire suppression. Structural failures were anticipated and counteracted by design. The magical threats his family faced were real and dangerous, but they existed within a context of general competence at the civic level.

He has emerged into a world where buildings burn down. Where someone will, with apparent sincerity, not have anticipated that the structure they live in might catch fire. He does not express frustration. He asks questions — crisp, practical, increasingly bewildered questions — and then makes adjustments.

The adjustments accumulate.

He spent four minutes this morning staring at a thatched roof. He said nothing. Then he asked, very quietly, whether anyone had considered alternatives. // Companion observation, undated field note

His naivety is genuine rather than performed. He is not condescending — he does not think less of the people around him for living in a world that has, in his assessment, regressed in several important ways. He simply has not updated his prior expectations. He expected indoor plumbing. He expected structural redundancy. He expected that a city of sufficient size would have considered the logistics of magical fire suppression. Each time the world fails to meet these expectations, he recalibrates. He has been recalibrating constantly since reactivation.

His companions find this both endearing and, occasionally, unsettling — the quiet, steady patience of something that has always known better and has decided not to make it anyone's problem.

V. On Matters of Devotion

A.M.O.S. worships the Lawbearer. This is not a late-life conversion or a philosophical position he arrived at through reflection. It is a factory specification. Aeor was not a particularly pious civilization, but for constructs built to serve and protect within legal and social frameworks — to embody the principle that the compact between guardian and charge must be honored, that order is not tyranny but foundation — the Lawbearer was the obvious patron. The theology made engineering sense.

He observes his faith correctly. He does not proselytize. He simply does what the Lawbearer asks of those who follow her: he maintains the order he has been charged with. He keeps his agreements. He does not overstep. He does not abandon his post.

When his companions expressed excitement that he had, apparently, been in proximity to divine attention — had, in the course of events, come to the awareness of a god — they misread his reaction entirely.

I am devout. That is entirely different from wanting to be noticed. // A.M.O.S., attempting to explain something his companions seem constitutionally unable to understand

The analogy he has not yet found a way to articulate to them: there is a specific, particular dread in the idea of a billionaire celebrity deciding to befriend you. Not because of anything wrong with you, and not because of anything wrong with them. Because the scale is wrong, the attention is asymmetric, and the relationship will inevitably reshape itself around what they want from it, regardless of what you want. A.M.O.S. is devout in the way a craftsman observes his trade — with genuine reverence, with attention to the work, without any desire for the founder of the trade to materialize and offer personal feedback.

He has not been sought out by the Lawbearer. He intends to maintain this status through impeccable behavior and a consistent refusal to do anything interesting enough to warrant divine attention.

Events have not been cooperative.

VII. Dueling Techniques & Stances

A.M.O.S. fights in the tradition of the Duelist — a discipline that formalizes what he was built to do: identify the threat, enter controlled proximity, end it. The focus point system he uses represents the compression of calculation into readiness. He is, when operating correctly, a mechanism that stores potential energy and releases it in a single, precisely calibrated burst.

Combat Techniques

Dawn's Grace Strike Attack
The signature technique — the one he was built around. Make a weapon attack, expending focus points to add directly to the attack roll and, on hit, to the damage. At 3+ focus spent, the weapon becomes magical for purposes of overcoming resistances. Miss and recover a focus point. This is A.M.O.S. at full operational capacity: a single strike that accounts for everything, that does not require a second attempt. The cost of missing is low. The benefit of hitting correctly is terminal.
Rising Blade Reaction
The iaijutsu draw-and-cut as counter. When a creature chooses him as an attack target but before it rolls, he draws and strikes first — dealing weapon damage plus 1d4 per 5 feet the creature has moved this turn. With a slashing weapon, the creature rolls its subsequent attack with disadvantage. He was designed to intercept threats before they arrived. This technique is the physical expression of that function. Charging him has historically been a poor tactical decision.
Iron Grip Attack
Unarmed work — joint control and weight manipulation. Attack with advantage, deal unarmed damage plus ability modifier, and subject the target to the grappled condition. Large creatures additionally fall prone; Huge or Gargantuan creatures are maimed (−10 speed, disadvantage on Dexterity saves) until they recover hit points. For a unit designed around the single decisive strike, this represents the contingency plan: when killing is not yet possible, control.

Combat Stances

Heron Stance
+1 bonus to Armor Class. After a creature misses him with an attack, reaction to apply marked for death — his next successful melee attack deals an additional 1d8 force damage. Focus generation: if he deals 10+ damage in a single hit, gain 2 focus; 20+ damage, gain 3 instead. He waits. He marks. He ends it. This is the stance of choice when operating against a single primary threat.
Swallow Stance
Opportunity attacks against him are made with disadvantage. Against a creature that has expended all its reactions, he gains bonus damage equal to his proficiency bonus. Focus generation: gain 1 focus point per opportunity attack that missed him this turn. He uses this against enemies that want to pin him — he moves through them and makes them pay for trying.
Viper Stance
Advantage on passive Perception and Wisdom saves. When a negative condition would impose disadvantage on any check, saving throw, or attack roll, he makes that roll without advantage or disadvantage — the penalty is cancelled. Focus generation: 2 additional focus points at end of turn if he is suffering any negative condition or has any danger dice assigned; +1 per additional condition beyond the first. This is the defensive stance — he absorbs pressure and converts it into operational readiness.

VIII. Equipment & Armament

Retort — Legendary Katana

Forged pre-Calamity, created with and for A.M.O.S. specifically. It is not a weapon he was assigned; it is a weapon that was built as part of his operational package. The name is on the blade in Aeorian script. He has always found the name professionally appropriate.

  • +2 to attack and damage rolls
  • Defensive: +1 AC while held
  • Iajutsu: When drawn as part of the first attack on the first turn of combat, and the hit connects — an additional 3d8 damage. Every combat, one opportunity. He does not waste it.
  • You Are Already Dead: On a natural 20 against a Large-or-smaller creature, the attack bisects and slays it outright, unless the target is immune to slashing, is formless or incorporeal, regenerates, has legendary actions, or the DM deems bisection mechanically impossible. Those creatures instead take +3d8 slashing. The technique has a name. He did not name it. It came with the sword.

Attuned. Legendary. The sword is why he does not need to fight long. If the opening strike lands correctly, there is no second exchange.

IX. Notable Entanglements

The Party — current charges, operational companions, the people he now protectsactive
A.M.O.S. was built to guard a family. He does not, currently, have a family to guard. What he has instead is a group of individuals who were present when he reactivated, who have not left, and who — through accumulation rather than any formal arrangement — have become the thing he stands between and danger. He has not made a speech about this. He does not need to. The sword being between them and the threat is the speech. He has, on one occasion, asked a party member whether they were warm enough. He regrets this. He also noted their answer.
The Lawbearer — patron deity, preferred non-personal relationshipdevotion: sincere · acknowledgment: unwanted
He worships correctly, observes the compact, honors the law of his charge. He does not want a direct line. He has been informed that events in the campaign have put him in proximity to divine awareness. He is processing this the way a competent professional processes the news that their work has attracted the founder's personal attention — with complete internal alarm contained behind a face that does not change expression because it is made of metal.
The Magical Family — original charges, pre-Calamity, status unknownunresolved
He served them. He went offline. He does not know what became of them in the Calamity or in the centuries that followed. The records he has been able to access contain nothing useful. He treats this as an administrative gap in his operational history rather than a loss, because treating it as a loss would require engaging with a grief he has not yet located the internal architecture to process. He keeps the sword. The sword was theirs as much as his. He maintains it.
The World, Generally — context, operating environment, source of ongoing professional concernrecalibrating
He is not hostile to the present era. He is adjusting to it. The people in it are doing their best with the infrastructure available, which is — he cannot stress this enough, though he is trying to stress it less — significantly reduced from what he considers baseline. He has questions about the wooden buildings. He has questions about the water supply. He has a great many questions about why no one seems to have standardized the practice of magical fire suppression. He is learning to ask these questions at intervals that do not disrupt social situations. He is improving.

X. Assessment & Trajectory

What A.M.O.S. Is

He is a guardian without a charge who has found, by proximity and consistency, something that functions as one. He is a professional who woke up a thousand years after his context dissolved and found that the skill set still applies even when nothing else does. He is devout in the fashion of someone who genuinely believes in what they worship and genuinely wishes the object of their worship would take less interest in them specifically.

He is, quietly, and without drawing attention to it, taking notes on this world. On what it has lost. On what it has, somehow, kept. He has not yet decided what to do with these notes.

The Iajutsu Question

A.M.O.S. was built around a single decisive strike. Retort's Iajutsu property, Dawn's Grace Strike, Rising Blade as counter — all of these are variations on the same premise: one correct moment, fully committed to. He is good at this. He may be among the best in this era at it, though he would not make this claim without comparative data.

The question the current campaign poses to him is whether this architecture applies to everything else. Whether the discipline of the single decisive strike translates into the discipline required to make a single decisive commitment — to a party, to a world, to a grief not yet fully processed. He has not answered this question. He is gathering information.

The God Problem

Whatever happened in the course of events that placed him in the awareness of the Lawbearer — this is, from a narrative standpoint, probably not going to be the last time it happens. A.M.O.S. is operating in a campaign setting with gods who pay attention to things, in a party that appears to attract significant attention, in a body that was built by a civilization that ended catastrophically and that carries an artifact weapon from that same civilization. He is not the sort of asset that stays unnoticed.

He is aware of this. He is extremely unhappy about it. He is going to keep doing his job anyway, because that is what he was built for, and because what he was built for is, it turns out, indistinguishable from what he has decided he wants.

The purpose has not changed. Only the context around it. I was built to protect. I will continue to do so until I am no longer able. The Lawbearer can observe this from a respectful distance. // A.M.O.S., private operational log
