Profile

Moved to the U.S. as a kid for his parents' work. Hated it. Long black hair, extreme metal, the whole shtick. Started bands; every band died on him.

Accepted a temporary role as bassist for the ska band. Said so explicitly. Was clear on this. It wasn't temporary. Too proud to do a shitty job, so he didn't. Wasn't out, then. Just "didn't date."

Now

Went corporate. Ponytail, tattoos under the cuff, marketing speak, elegant Euro-bizgeek vibe. Stock options vested at Hooli.

Married Brock overseas before it was legal in the U.S.; they've been together since around 2007. Brock is a dudebro, gym rat, crypto hodl-er. Currently, massively, into NFTs.

"These are non-fungible tokens (NFTs) like bitcoins. If the spouse who has been buying these digital assets reveals the assets as he or she is required to do according to the law, they can be identified and divided equally between the spouses if they were purchased with community property assets."

The comeback tour is absolutely a ploy for dissipation of marital assets — and a way to stick it to Brock before the divorce finalises. Emil filed right after the options vested. The marriage has been functionally dead for a couple of years.

The Four Petals He Pulls

Emotional Scale

The final number of successes is intensity. +4 is success; +6 triggers benefits.

  1. You feel it enough to influence you.
  2. You feel it intensely. Hard to do anything not related to it.
  3. As intensely as most people ever feel it. Active compulsions.
  4. If we're talking love, more than you love your partner.
  5. All-consuming obsession. The further reaches of madness.
  6. Supernaturally intense. Beyond anything in reality.

Performing With the d4

Emotional Control is a Charisma dice pool; the d4 is added to it, but the d4 is not counted as a die — its number is the count of successes. Each success modifies the result by ±1. Note the result solemnly. Ceremonially pass the d4 to the affected player. Their character is now influenced by his abilities.

To use the power on someone else, he must first reclaim the d4 — which ends the previous compulsion.

A Little Emotional Nudge

When he holds the d4 and subtle manipulation may give him an edge, he may roll the d4 into any pool. It does not count as a die; the number raises one die in the pool by that amount.

Resisting Him

Measured by Willpower (INT + WIS). To permanently remove a target from combat, his successes must match or exceed their Willpower. Anything less and they are influenced, but not broken.

Critical Failure

On a critical failure, the emotional state cannot be removed. It is permanent. He has broken someone. Oh dear.
(The d4 is ignored when calculating whether something is a critical fail.)

The Chorus

A Greek chorus — ghosts of the city Orestes once ruled — backs him up. They do not strike. They do not speak the commands. They supplement the Voice: a tide of sighing, of agreement, of grief in unison. When the words appear in the air, the chorus is the breath that makes them visible.

Chronicle

  1. One show only. They have been told there is one show. The comeback tour is the doorway. Emil, ponytail and tattoos and quarterly forecasts, picks up the bass for the first time in years. On the other side: spectacular eveningwear. A resurrected ruler. A chorus.
  2. A previous game, remembered. They trashed the world the last time they came through — under Gorgoroth the Necromancer. The land remembers. Orestes is the resurrected ruler of the city the previous characters ruled with an iron fist. A revolution killed him. It could not hold him down. Who put him back is a mystery.
  3. Lady Magdalena's wedding. They wrecked it. Evidence was planted. The exact target of the frame is for the next session to remember; what is recorded here is that the wedding does not happen as planned, and someone takes the fall who was not supposed to.
  4. The Factory. Worshippers of the god of the forge laboured there. The party found them — what came of it is still being written.
  5. The alliance, sabotaged. The Monhian Empire and the Brastedian Theocracy were trying to come together. They are no longer trying to come together. Two countries, properly screwed up.

Why He Pulls the Strings

Emil feels that love is not possible. He has material wealth. He has the absence of community and the absence of social connection. He seeks to manipulate others to avoid feelings of inadequacy and loss.

In D&D terms, Dictators are like Bards — if everyone was fucking petrified of Bards.
