I. Concept

The gap between his life as he dreamed it would be, and the state of the world and his life as it was, tormented Rhys — but each time he shoved those feelings under and focused on showing up for his family.

— from the record

An aging punk. A Saskatchewan farm kid who got into hardcore in his teens, toured for a decade with the band Barghest, was crippled at a protest in his twenties, married, moved south, took a software helpdesk job, and raised two children with his wife Gwen. Per his sheet, he has felt incredibly disempowered and overwhelmed; the Garou forms have given him an outlet for the years of pent-up rage at the world and at his impotence.

Burly. Shaved head. Beard. Tattooed. Wheelchair-bound — but only in homid form.

VI. History

Early Life · Mercy, Saskatchewan
Farm kid. School. Hockey.
A fairly normal upbringing. He got into punk rock through a cassette his cousin made for him, and from there devoured anything he could find through mail-order zines and copied cassettes.
College · Regina
Joins Barghest after a few false starts
Moved to Regina for college. After several failed bands, things stuck with Barghest. He played bass and did backup vocals.
~1993–2003
Roughly a decade touring
Toured with bands like I Spy and Dayglo Abortions. Became increasingly politicized over those years.
2003 · Montreal, WTO Protest
Crippled by a tear-gas canister
A tear-gas canister fired into the crowd hit the tail of his spine. He lost the use of his legs. The lawsuit was costly and went nowhere. Medical was costly.
Post-2003 · Regina
Returns to community college; meets Gwen
Gwen was from Laramie, Wyoming. She became pregnant. They moved south so she could have family support. Rhys applied for and got his green card, and found work at a software helpdesk.
2014 · Laramie, Wyoming
Daughter Molly born
2019 · Laramie
Son Miles born
Miles is nonverbal and has inexplicable outbursts and meltdowns.
2020–onward · COVID
Isolation
Deeply isolating. Rhys got used to working from home while Gwen worked long hours as a nurse at the local hospital.
May 24, 2023 · Laramie
T-bone collision · First Change
Mandatory work-from-office day. Gwen and the kids came to pick him up. On the way home they were T-boned in an intersection. Gwen unconscious, the kids terrified. Something in Rhys snapped: he tore off his seatbelt, pulled apart the roof of the car, carried Miles and Molly to safety, then brought out Gwen's unconscious body. The terror in his children's eyes enraged him; he stormed across the highway, tore the other driver out of the truck, and threw him. Only then did he register that he was walking — that his clothing was falling off and his body had become muscular and hairy. Sirens approached. He ran.

VII. Session One — Notable Events

Waking, and the Gorgon

Rhys ran in frenzy, exhausted, falling in and out of consciousness. He woke at sunrise, naked. In what he recognized as a recurring dream, a large creature — his patron, a Gorgon — sniffed at him and walked off, turning back expectantly. He followed across sagebrush and stony soil. He set his intention and his body shifted into glabro: spine strengthening, muscles moving, standing and following. He still believed he was dreaming.

Sereen's Vision & Charity's Approach

A woman approached at a rock formation. She did not regard the Gorgon. She apologized — the accident had accelerated the timing of his First Transformation. She said he had been shown to her in a vision, as someone she would teach and who would come to resist the Wyrm. She offered to guide him in a vision. She pulled out a spider on a thin strand of silk and plucked it like a guitar string until it grew large enough to enter.

Entering the Umbra

A chaotic realm: all kinds of creatures, constant bombardment of how Gaia is being destroyed, banes of destruction, maggots overrunning the umbra. She pointed out the silver footprints Rhys had been leaving — his trail in. Rhys realized his Umbral shape was a Gorgon cub on the cusp of adulthood. He picked up the scents of spirits who had moved through in the past days. He saw the caern — like a lighthouse or a beacon. Defying spatial categories, there was a thick cable, like a power line or steel construction cable. He plucked it with his paw and felt the vibration carry far away.

The Pattern Spiders' Vision

The Vision

A palm-sized pattern spider approached, with unique patterns, looking at Rhys with curiosity. It tapped out a rhythm on the cord; more spiders joined, harmonizing, weaving a screen of silk that played a scene of the area around Laramie. A black cloud descended, devastatingly flooding the area. Rhys cried out in fury. A lightning bolt struck Devil's Playground, and everything was flattened. He swatted at the screen; the silk tore and rebuilt. Three seedlings grew forth into huge oak trees.

Rhys asked the spiders whether the seeds predated the disaster. They showed that the seeds have roots before the caern's destruction — but it was unclear whether destruction is a necessary condition for their growth. Charity, in dire wolf form, was astonished; she had thought these were loose visions and had not realized there was such an ominous threat on the horizon.

Lenny, and a Choice

On the way back, a large blue-collared man jogged up — Lenny. Charity called Rhys "family." Lenny laughed: "Looks like you have the choice of being pretty or mobile. You're going to go Teen Wolf if you want to walk." Rhys had an epiphany — a choice — and shifted into glabro to walk. Lenny howled and shifted into crinos to demonstrate. Rhys felt the instinct to join and resisted.

Brutal Outcome

As they walked, insight landed: I don't think I'm dreaming. Rhys shifted into crinos and shoved Lenny away. Lenny also shifted: "Don't challenge me, young one. I've killed more for less." Rhys turned and ran back toward where he thought the highway was. Charity ran after him, touched his shoulder, and soothed him. He collapsed back into homid, curled up, and wept: "How long has it been? Are my kids ok? Is Gwen ok? Did I kill that man? What the fuck is going on?" Lenny and Charity cradled him.

The Caern & the Pack

They led him to a magically hidden entrance and down into large natural caverns. He met Ruth, and Sereen — an older woman who commanded the others' respect, the pack leader. Keith and Lynn were expected back from Laramie with supplies.

The Botfly Badger

The next morning, after a solid breakfast, Sereen set up a rite. Rhys participated awkwardly. In the Umbra he asked for guidance (Sight from Beyond) and saw Keith and Lynn bringing something important to the caern. He was snapped out as they ran in with a badger covered in pus-filled warts, malformations, and mutations — a hivemind-like infestation, a spirit possession beyond Lynn's ability to undo. Sereen ordered it dispatched. Rhys suggested burning it to make sure none of "those things" got away. In the Umbra, the spirit tried to escape the badger body; Rhys completely failed to react.

The Return Home

Rhys shifted into lupus and snuck back to Laramie. He did not hide his action from the pack, but he did not tell anyone. He approached the alley behind his old house through the bushes. Miles was playing outside. Gwen could be seen through the back window, up and about. Miles saw him, stood, approached, and touched Rhys's snout. The back door opened — Molly called: "Miles, it's time for dinner!" Rhys darted back. Passing the garbage on his way out, he saw a child's drawing of the accident, with Rhys in crinos form, on the ground. It did not look monstrous. It looked matter-of-fact.

VIII. Psychology

From the Sheet

Rhys has felt incredibly disempowered and overwhelmed. The incredible power of the Garou forms has given him an outlet for the years of pent up rage at the world and at his impotence.

Sometimes the gap between his life as he dreamed it would be, and the state of the world and his life as it was, tormented Rhys, but each time he shoved those feelings under and focused on showing up for his family. The First Change handed him action — and physical power — back, and immediately tested what he would do with it.

His own framing of this is the dossier's epigraph: "They were safe from my rage when I lacked power."

IX. Family — Touchstones

Gwen

Wife. From Laramie. Met Rhys in Regina while he was finishing community college after the WTO injury. Nurse at the local hospital; long hours. Unconscious at the accident. When Rhys snuck back in lupus form, she could be seen through the back window, up and about.

Molly

Daughter, born 2014. Called Miles in for dinner from the back door. Drew the picture of the accident — Rhys in crinos form, matter-of-fact rather than monstrous — that he found in the garbage.

Miles

Son, born 2019. Nonverbal. Has inexplicable outbursts and meltdowns. Saw Rhys in lupus form in the yard, stood up, walked over, and touched his snout.

The Other Driver

Drunk. Slurred one question before Rhys tore him from the truck and threw him across the highway. Status unknown at the close of Session One.
