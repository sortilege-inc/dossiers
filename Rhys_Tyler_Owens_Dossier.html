I. Concept

The gap between his life as he dreamed it would be, and the state of the world and his life as it was, tormented Rhys — but each time he shoved those feelings under and focused on showing up for his family.

— from the record

Rhys is a man who has spent two decades being compressed. A punk kid from the Saskatchewan prairies who believed the world could be different. A bassist who toured with bands that meant something. An activist who put his body in front of history — and lost the use of his legs for it. A father, a husband, a helpdesk drone. A man who had learned, methodically and at great cost, to hold down the rage.

He had gotten very good at that. Too good, maybe. And then the car hit, and Gwen went limp, and his children screamed, and something in him that had been coiled for twenty years finally let go.

Rhys is not a man who wanted power. He is a man who had given up on the idea that he was allowed to want anything for himself. The Garou forms have returned to him something he did not even know he had lost: the capacity to act, to matter physically, to be dangerous. That terrifies him in ways he has not finished processing.

VI. History

Early Life · Mercy, Saskatchewan
Farm kid, hockey, cassette tapes
Mercy is a town that doesn't appear on most maps. He was good at hockey and bored out of his mind. A cassette from his cousin cracked something open. He started ordering from zines. Saved up for a mail-order back catalogue. The farm is still there. He has not been back.
Mid-1990s · Regina
College drop-out; joins Barghest
Dropped out of community college the first time. After a few false starts — failed bands, failed roommates — things stuck with Barghest. He played bass. He sang backup. He was not the best musician in the band, but he was the one who held it together when things got bad, which was often.
Late 1990s–Early 2000s
A decade on the road with Barghest
Toured with I Spy and Dayglo Abortions. The shows got better. The politics got sharper. Straight-edge, eco-political, hardcore. He believed every word he sang. That is important: he believed it. He still does.
2003 · Montreal, WTO Protest
Tear gas canister — tail of spine — permanent injury
The canister was fired into the crowd. It hit the tail of his spine. The lawsuit went nowhere and cost more than it recovered. The medical bills took years. He learned to live without the use of his legs. He learned to stop talking about what he had lost. He did not stop feeling it.
Mid-2000s · Regina
Returns to finish college; meets Gwen
Gwen was from Laramie, Wyoming. She was studying nursing. She did not pity him, which was the first thing. She argued with him, which was the second. She became pregnant, and they moved south so she could have her family around her. He applied for and got his green card. He got a helpdesk job at a software company. He did not perform at a show for eleven years.
2014 · Laramie, Wyoming
Molly is born
Seven years old now. She processes the world differently than he expects; she is more organised than either of her parents, which is terrifying.
2019–2022 · Laramie
Miles is born; COVID; isolation
Miles is nonverbal. The outbursts were inexplicable for a long time. COVID compressed everything: Gwen worked long ICU hours, Rhys worked from home, the kids had nowhere to go. He is not sure he would have survived that period without Gwen. He is not sure he was honest with her about how close it was.
May 24th, 2023 · Laramie
First Change — T-bone collision
Mandatory office day. Gwen and the kids came to pick him up. A drunk driver ran the intersection. Gwen went unconscious. The kids screamed. Something in Rhys that had been coiled for twenty years let go. He tore off the seatbelt, pulled the roof apart, carried his children to safety, pulled out his wife's body — and when the terror in their eyes enraged him further, he crossed the highway and tore the driver out of his truck and threw him. And then registered that he was walking. Sirens. He ran.
Session One · Umbra & Caern
First contact with Sereen, Lenny, Charity
Sereen's pack found him. Charity guided him through an Umbral vision — a catastrophic black cloud, Devil's Playground destroyed by lightning, three seedlings becoming oaks. He cried. He raged. He shifted into Crinos and shoved Lenny before Charity calmed him. He ugly cried on the ground. He said sorry and asked to follow them. Then, in lupus form, he snuck back to Laramie and confirmed his family was alive. Miles touched his snout. A child's drawing of the accident — Rhys in crinos form, not monstrous, matter-of-fact — was in the trash.

VII. Psychology

The Compressed Man

The central psychological fact of Rhys is twenty years of managed impotence. He is politically conscious, emotionally intelligent, and has enormous amounts of rage that he has trained himself to suppress. He is excellent at suppressing it. The suppression has been good for his family and costly to himself.

The Garou forms have done two things: they have given him back his legs, and they have given him back the capacity for violence. He does not know yet whether these are gifts or temptations. He suspects the answer is both.

Triggers

  • His children's fear. The terror in Molly's and Miles' eyes at the accident was the direct trigger for his First Change. This is a live wire. Anything that threatens his children or produces that specific expression will push him toward frenzy.
  • Impotence reframed as complicity. The note he carries — They were safe from my rage when I lacked power — is not a boast. It is a fear. He is genuinely uncertain whether his restraint over the last twenty years was virtue or cowardice dressed up as virtue.
  • The world being destroyed while he watches. The Umbral vision of the black cloud and the lightning strike hit him at a pre-rational level. The ecological politics he formed at eighteen are still fully operational. The Wyrm is not an abstraction to him — it is the thing he was already fighting, under a different name.

Relationships within the Pack

  • Sereen — respects her, is slightly wary of her certainty. He is used to being the oldest and most politically seasoned person in the room. He is not used to having a pack leader.
  • Charity — instinctive trust. She was the first person to calm him physically. He noticed that she ran after him, not Lenny. He is grateful and does not quite know how to say it.
  • Lenny / Lenard — complicated. He sees through the cheerful bombast to someone who has probably killed a lot of people and is comfortable with it. He finds Lenny's enthusiasm for the war-form disquieting in the way a mirror can be disquieting.
  • Keith — friction, unresolved. Keith is on his ass about everything. He has not yet decided if Keith is testing him, hates him specifically, or is like this with everyone.
Psychological Note

Rhys has felt incredibly disempowered and overwhelmed. The incredible power of the Garou forms has given him an outlet for the years of pent-up rage at the world and at his impotence. He has not yet resolved whether using that outlet is healing or relapse into something he does not want to be.

VIII. Family — Touchstones

Gwen (wife)

Nurse. From Laramie. Did not pity him. Argued with him. Was unconscious at the accident. Was up and about the next day when Rhys checked on the family in lupus form. He does not know if there has been a funeral. He does not know if they think he survived. He does not know if going back would help or hurt.

Molly (daughter, b. 2014)

Seven years old. She called Miles in from the yard without seeing Rhys in the bushes. She manages the household in small ways that neither parent fully acknowledges. She drew a picture of the accident — Rhys in crinos form — and threw it in the trash. It was not monstrous. It was matter-of-fact.

Miles (son, b. 2019)

Four years old. Nonverbal. Autistic. He saw Rhys in lupus form in the yard, stood up, and walked toward him. He touched Rhys's snout. He was not afraid. This was the one moment in Session One where Rhys felt something other than dread.

The Drunk Driver

Rhys threw him across the highway. He does not know if the man is alive. He has not asked anyone to check. He is aware he has not asked and is aware of what that awareness costs him.

X. Possible Trajectories

The Question of His Family

He cannot go back the way he came. He does not know if he can go back at all. Miles touched his snout. Molly drew the picture. Whether his family would accept what he is — or whether contact with them puts them at greater risk from whatever the Wyrm is building in Laramie — is the thread that will pull at everything else.

The Umbral Vision

A black cloud. Devil's Playground destroyed by lightning. Three seedlings. He asked the spiders whether the seeds predate the disaster. The answer was that the roots precede the caern's destruction — but it's unclear if destruction is a necessary condition for growth. This will haunt him. He is the kind of man who sits with an ambiguous answer for years before acting on it.

The Theurge's Path

He has Sight from Beyond. He was drawn to the pattern-spider in the Umbra without being taught to be. His Insight and Subterfuge scores (both at 4) suggest a man who has always been able to read people and situations more precisely than he lets on. The Theurge's gifts suit the intelligence of someone who spent two decades watching power operate from the outside.

The Rage and What It Costs

He has been extremely careful, for twenty years, not to let the rage turn outward toward people who did not deserve it. He has been careful for good reasons. The Garou forms make that carefulness much harder. The first frenzy he is unable to control is the inflection point everything else bends toward.

What He Will Not Be

He will not be a convert. He does not do certainty. He mistrusts people who find their new community's worldview too immediately convincing, because he has seen what happens to those people. He will remain skeptical, lateral, and slightly sideways to whatever the pack expects of him — which is either the pack's greatest asset or its most persistent headache, depending on the week.
