Class Features
Umbral Corruption Class
As Vaetra accumulates scars, she is slowly consumed by the Umbra. When she deals damage, she deals extra damage equal to her number of scars. When she marks her last Hope slot with a scar, she does not retire — instead she succumbs to the Umbra's influence and charges forever into the dark to join the terrors that stalk lightless spaces.
Make a Scene Hope Feature
Spend 3 Hope to temporarily Distract a target within Close range. The target's attention is seized — they cannot act against Vaetra or her allies until the distraction ends or they take damage.
Rally Bard
Once per session, describe how you rally the party. Give yourself and each ally a Rally Die (d6). A PC can spend their Rally Die to add the result to an action roll, reaction roll, or damage roll — or to clear Stress equal to the result. At the end of each session, all unspent Rally Dice clear. At level 5, the Rally Die increases to a d8.
Rousing Speech Bard
Once per long rest, give a heartfelt, inspiring speech. All allies within Far range clear 2 Stress.
Heart of a Poet Wordsmith
After you make an action roll to impress, persuade, or offend someone, you can spend a Hope to add a d4 to the roll.

Character

Appearance

Vaetra is Shargan — tall, broad-framed, built like the mountains she grew up inside — though she carries herself closer to the ground than her height suggests, as if perpetually ready to move. Her hair is black and braided tight against her skull, practical in the way everything about her is practical. Her eyes are sharp, the kind that find the exits before they find the faces. She dresses in layers she can move in, not layers that impress.

Manner

Short temper — but not undisciplined. She knows exactly where her patience ends and she budgets around it. She reads people the way she reads texts: quickly, for use, without sentiment. She can be warm. She is warm, sometimes, with people who have earned it. The circle earned it. That warmth sits beneath a layer of terse competence she has never bothered to remove.

Purpose

She trained under Renavir as a war-scholar — history as a discipline of survival, language as a weapon, the past as a manual for the present. She never intended to stay in Ulmira's Step. The settlement needed people who understood what was coming, and she was one of them, and so she stayed longer than she meant to. She has not made peace with this. The letter she carries — never opened — is from a posting she turned down three years ago. She has carried it ever since as either ballast or provocation. She has not yet decided which.

On Ygva

When Ygva followed Oshel into the mountains, Vaetra was the one who pressed Kelun into her hands. She did not say why. She told herself it was practical — a Clank companion would be useful on the Griefcleft — but the truth was simpler and she knew it: she was afraid Ygva would be alone. She has not said this. She will not say it.

History

Born in Ulmira's Step to parents who survived the lower valleys — Ridgeborne both, which meant they understood work as a form of devotion — Vaetra was the child who always had a question and seldom waited for the answer. The lector Renavir recognized this early and made it useful: sending her to fetch texts, to index records, to argue back when the arguments needed testing.

She came up alongside Ygva in the same circle, in the same lessons, under the same smoke-dark sky of the Sacred Pyre. They were not alike. Ygva drifted through Renavir's instruction; Vaetra consumed it. Where Ygva seemed to be waiting for something the lessons couldn't give her, Vaetra was already sharpening what she'd learned against whatever came next. They were close in the way that complementary angles are close — meeting at a point, oriented elsewhere.

She stayed after the circle scattered. Mellan went north into the ranges; Sarru took the Pyre; Tamros disappeared. Ygva followed Oshel. Vaetra remained in Ulmira's Step, training, studying, reading accounts from the Amber Reach that described things the settlement's elders refused to name. She has spent five years becoming someone capable of doing something about it. She does not yet know what that something is. The letter in her pack has an answer. She has not opened it.
