I. Identity & the Headless

Pelorajax is a DragonKnight — a wyrmkin warrior, by lineage and bearing, who carries hardened scales (wymplate) and a pair of wings powerful enough to fly. Her ancestry is the most physically formidable on the cell's sheets, and her current condition is the most physically diminished: she has no head. The Lost Your Head complication is not a flourish; she is genuinely without one. She borrows the senses of willing others through a psionic ability called Share Head.

Her class is Summoner, the discipline of binding elementals and commanding them as one's hands and weapons. Her subclass — Circle of Storms — orients her summoning toward elemental keywords of Air, Fire, Earth, and their pairings. The summons do the work she cannot do herself, including the work of having a head. Three signature minions are always available to her; six additional minion-types stand ready in her portfolio.

Her career was Agent. She was a spy. Her cover broke for the oldest reason — she fell for someone on the other side. She is here in Alloy now, in a cell, with a debt she did not sign, summoning storms.

III. Essence — The Heroic Resource

The Summoner's heroic resource is Essence. Essence is the energy by which Pelorajax binds elementals into bodies and commands them in concert. It is spent on the main action Call Forth (the summoning itself), on amplifying Quick Commands, and on the strained or enhanced effects of certain abilities.

A Note on This Sheet

The character sheet does not list explicit start-of-turn essence gain values in the same way the JSON sheets do. The Summoner class operates on its own rhythm — Call Forth costs 1+ per signature minion (or a set cost per non-signature minion type), and Strike for Me costs 0. Specific essence-gain triggers should be confirmed against the Summoner class chapter; this dossier records what is selected and printed on her sheet.

IV. DragonKnight Traits

DragonKnights are wyrmkin — descendants or initiates of a draconic order, who carry hardened scales, wings, and an elemental bond. Pelorajax's traits are oriented toward cold:

Wings

She possesses wings powerful enough to take her airborne. While using her wings to fly, she can stay aloft for a number of rounds equal to her Might score (1 round at +1 Might — minimum 1) before she falls prone. While flying at 1st, 2nd, and 3rd level, she has damage weakness 5. Grants the Fly movement mode.

Wymplate (Cold) & Prismatic Scales (Cold)

Her hardened scales grant damage immunity 1 to one of: acid, cold, corruption, fire, lightning, or poison. She has chosen cold. She can change this type when she finishes a respite. The Prismatic Scales feature additionally grants +1 Cold immunity per level, stacking with the Wymplate to give her Cold immunity 2 at L1.

The Wings Tradeoff

At level 1, flying makes her five points more vulnerable to all damage. She can fly for one round (her Might score) before falling prone. The weakness goes away at level 4; for now, flight is reserved for emergencies, single decisive strikes, or escape. This same tradeoff applies to Lucero d'Albec in the cell, whose Devil ancestry uses the same wings feature.

V. Summoner Class Features

The Summoner brings minions into being and commands them. Pelorajax's class kit consists of a Summoner Strike for direct attack, Call Forth for summoning, three actions for commanding her minions, and several passive features that shape what she does on a turn.

Main Action — Call Forth

Call Forth
Main Action · Magic, Ranged · Summoner's Range · Self · Cost: 1+ essence

Effect: Pelorajax summons one or more minions from her portfolio into unoccupied spaces within distance. She chooses one of the following options:

  • Signature Minions: Summon one signature minion for each essence spent.
  • All Other Minions: Summon the set number of minions listed on the stat block for their essence cost.

Maneuver — Minion Bridge

Minion Bridge
Maneuver · Magic · Melee 1 · One of your minions · Cost: 0

Effect: She shifts into a square adjacent to the target, including vertically.

She can shift into squares that contain one of her minions, even if they occupy difficult terrain. Each time she shifts into a square containing one of her minions during this maneuver, she can shift an additional square.

Spend 1 Essence: An adjacent ally can shift alongside her during this movement. They must end their movement in an unoccupied square adjacent to the last minion she moved through.

Free Triggered Action — Strike for Me

Strike for Me
Free Triggered Action · Magic, Ranged · Summoner's Range · Each of your minions · Cost: 0

Effect: Her minions act in place of her making a free strike or using a signature ability. If she was granted the triggered action against a specific target, her minions must strike the same target. If the triggered action granted her a signature ability, she has an edge on the power roll.

VI. Circle of Storms

Elemental Affinity

Whenever Pelorajax uses Call Forth to summon one or more non-signature elemental minions, she can summon one bonus signature minion at no cost. She chooses between a signature minion that shares an Element keyword with the minions she summoned (Fire, Earth, or Air) or an Elemental Mote.

Heart of Nature

She can sense the presence of creatures with the Elemental or Dragon keywords within 1 mile of her. She can innately feel their emotions or pain, and she can't obtain lower than a tier 2 outcome on any Intuition test made to socially interact with them.

The Circle's Logic

Elemental Affinity creates a recursive economy: spending essence to summon non-signature elementals gets her a free signature minion of related element. Heart of Nature gives her a 1-mile awareness of any elemental or dragon she crosses paths with, and a floor of tier 2 on social tests with them. In Alloy — a city through which every elemental migrant in the timescape passes — this is a sensory radius the other characters cannot match.

The Loadout's Shape

Pelorajax doesn't fight the battle; she composes it.

XII. Complication — Lost Your Head

Pelorajax has no head. She functions by borrowing one.

Share Head

Share Head
You don't have a head but can psionically borrow another.
Maneuver · Psionic, Ranged 10 · One willing creature

Pelorajax can see, hear, and smell as if she were in the target's space. Additionally, she can borrow their mouth to speak when she wishes to do so, speaking in her own voice. This effect ends when she uses Share Head on a different target, when the target moves more than 10 squares away from her, or when the creature is no longer willing to share their head with her.

Lost Your Head Drawback

Having no head, Pelorajax can't see, hear, smell, taste, or verbalize except by using Share Head. Additionally, she can't wear gear that requires a head, such as a helmet or hat.

The Operational Reality

The complication is the character's defining mechanical fact. Without Share Head active, Pelorajax is functionally sensorily inert — she cannot see her surroundings, cannot hear conversation, cannot speak. Maintaining a willing host (an ally, a familiar, a friendly stranger) is a baseline operational requirement, like an oxygen supply for a diver. Her social life depends on someone letting her use their head. The Spies and Lovers incident hands the complication its emotional weight: the last person she trusted to that extent betrayed her, or was betrayed by her, or died for her.

Tactical Implications

In combat, Pelorajax's typical play is: Share Head on an ally (maneuver, no essence) to establish her senses, then Call Forth her minions (main action, essence cost) to do the fighting. If her host falls, moves out of range, or revokes willingness, she goes blind. Her minions are an answer to this — a Flow of Magma or Crux of Ash extends her presence onto the battlefield without requiring she leave the host's perceptual field. The summoning is the strategy and the workaround.

XIII. Inciting Incident — Spies and Lovers

From the Draw Steel inciting incident: "While embedded in an undercover assignment, you fell for someone on the other side. They discovered you were a double-agent and though you insisted your feelings were real, the deceit cut too deep for your love interest to ignore. They exposed you, spurned you, or died because of their closeness to you. You left the espionage business to become a hero with nothing to hide."

What's Established
  • Pelorajax was embedded in an undercover assignment as an Agent.
  • She fell for someone on the other side.
  • That person discovered she was a double agent.
  • She insisted her feelings were real. They were not enough.
  • The love interest exposed her, spurned her, or died because of their closeness to her — one of three (Director's call).
  • She left the espionage business.
  • She is now a hero, with — per the incident's phrase — "nothing to hide."
The Joke and the Wound

The Inciting Incident says she became "a hero with nothing to hide," and the character sheet says she literally has no head — nowhere to hide her face. The complication and the incident rhyme. Whether her headlessness pre-dated the assignment (and the complication is unrelated to the incident) or whether the lost head is a consequence of the cover blowing (a punishment, a price, a sacrifice) is unrecorded. Worth specifying at Session Zero, because the answer changes who she is.

XIV. Position in Collateral draft

Provisional · Drawn from Campaign Concept

The Collateral campaign concept does not list Pelorajax in its primary roster — the character was created after the campaign was first drafted, alongside Brenn & Hroga, to test the new Summoner and Beastheart classes. Connecting her to the campaign's central premise is a Session Zero exercise.

The premise: the player characters wake in the same holding cell in Alloy and are told "The debt has defaulted. You belong to the Saffron Seal now." Pelorajax's position in the cell is the most operationally complex of any prisoner: she cannot perceive the cell until she Share Heads someone in it, and the room is full of strangers. Her first action upon waking will be the question of whose head to ask to borrow.

Role on the Team

Combat: summoner — battlefield composition. Two signature minions on permanent call; five portfolio minions in reserve; Strike for Me allows multi-target free strikes from her squad on a single triggered action; Quick Command: Shield! redirects strikes to adjacent minions. She is the character who occupies the most board space and the least personal space — she is barely on the board at all, and her minions are everywhere.

Out of combat: Heart of Nature radius (1 mile) for elementals and dragons; agent skill suite; Gum up the Works for trap-jamming; Share Head for borrowed reconnaissance. She is the team's natural surveillance specialist — she can use Share Head on a willing ally to extend her senses into a different room while her body stays elsewhere.

Faction Connections

Her past as an Agent implies a former employer. The campaign concept does not yet identify the agency. Candidates from the wider timescape — a draconic order, an intelligence service of one of the Seven Cities or Axiom, the Watch Officers — are all available. Whoever ran her may also be a candidate for who named her as collateral. To be developed.

XV. Character Arc

Three threads pull through Pelorajax's sheet.

The first is trust. Share Head requires a willing target. Lost Your Head says she has nothing else. Spies and Lovers is the story of the last time her trust failed catastrophically. The campaign asks whether she can build the kind of trust the complication mechanically requires — and what happens when she can't.

The second is the storm she has chosen. Circle of Storms is not a defensive subclass. Elemental Affinity rewards aggressive summoning; her portfolio leans on fire and persistent-area effects; Flow of Magma's signature ability is a damage-and-displacement spell her minion casts. She summons violence to a degree that suggests she has decided she is not done fighting, even if she has put down the spy work.

The third is nothing to hide. The Inciting Incident gives her the line; the body she is in delivers it without subtlety. She is the most visibly altered character in the cell, and she is in a city where every public conversation passes through borrowed mouths. The campaign asks whether she can do the work of being a hero — visible, present, named — when her presence and her name are the things that ended the last person she loved.
