Bioα · Vita

Kragar was named for a great minotaur oracle, and bathed in hydra's blood as an infant — the wounds he takes close too quickly to become mortal. He hails from Skophos, the labyrinthine minotaur polis, but found his calling outside its walls: as a charioteer's fighter in the arena, where he won a championship in Iroas's name. Iroas favors him, the story goes, because Kragar's twin brother — still in Skophos — is a champion of Mogis. The god of victory and the god of slaughter share something between them through these two, and Kragar's relationship with both his god and his sibling has been complicated by it ever since.

He is confident, fiercely loyal to allies, and exudes a commanding power that crowds learn to read instantly. He sees himself as a protector of those who can't protect themselves — Iroas's Heroism, taken to the chariot rail — though his pride and his temper push the line on what "protection" looks like more often than he'd admit.

Why Kragar is on the Expeditionο · Hodós

Personal Rivalry. Kragar will be forced to fight his twin brother when the minotaurs of Skophos attack Akros. The campaign frame has Skophos minotaurs raiding Akros during the Fallow Year while Mogis revels in the bloodshed; Kragar's twin leads one of those bands. Iroas favored Kragar partly because his twin is Mogis's champion — the gods set the brothers against each other before the brothers were old enough to choose.

Now choice is overdue, and the Fallow Year is the field on which it gets made. A devotee of Iroas, an Akrosian by adoption, and the brother of the man on the other side of the wall: Kragar is the campaign's hinge for the Akros plotline.
