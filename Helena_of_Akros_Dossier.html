Helena of Akros

A young knight of Heliod, polished sword and polished armor, asking the questions a knight is not supposed to ask.

Stage of Career

Bioα · Vita

Helena's mother was a champion of Heliod, and died in childbirth with her. Her father raised her to be strong — a hero of Akros, a hero of Heliod. When she came of age she joined the knightly order of Heliod, first as a squire and then, after years of training, as a full knight. Her father did not live to see her invested. She faces problems head-on: a simple, direct solution is the best path to success. Her integrity means everything to her, and she would lay her life down for those in her order without thinking twice about it.

Why Helena is on the Expeditionο · Hodós

Divine Questions. Helena struggles to maintain social order, and is confused by Heliod's seeming apathy. The Fallow Year reaches Akros, and the social order Helena has been trained to uphold is fraying. People look to her order for answers; her order looks up to Heliod for them; and Heliod — for the first time in Helena's memory — is not answering.

The questions a knight isn't supposed to ask are starting to ask themselves. Helena is on the expedition because someone with a sword and a vow needs to find out what the gods are doing about this, and her bond to her order is the only frame she has for the work.
