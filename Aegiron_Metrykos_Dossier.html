Aegiron Metrykos

A noble of Odunos who died and came back wearing a beaten-gold mask — honor-bound to debts he can no longer name.

Stage of Career

Bioα · Vita

Aegiron Metrykos was a noble in life. He died at 35 — how, he does not remember. He escaped the Underworld and is one of the Returned, his face surrendered to Erebos's realm, replaced now by a beaten-gold mask worth more than most peasants will see in a year. His skin is pale gray, faintly cracked, the cracks faintly luminescent in the dark. His eyes are black; when something stirs in him — anger, recognition, the rare flicker of an old life — they glow blue.

He is honor-bound and fiercely proud of his accomplishments and his heritage, though most of the accomplishments are now stories someone else tells him about himself, and the heritage is a name on a scroll of pedigree he is not entirely sure is his. He has been told, in the necropolis, that he was a soldier once. Possibly an officer. He does not contradict this. He cannot.

Racial Features — Returned (Human subrace)η · Génos

Returned (Base)

  • Ability Score Increase: +2 Con (already applied).
  • Creature Type — Undead: Affected by spells that target undead (turn undead, sun-blast, etc.). Immune to many things that target humanoids.
  • Returned Nature: Needs water and air; does not need food or sleep. Requires 8 hours of rest to gain the benefits of a long rest.
  • Unreadable Face: Immune to any effect that would sense your emotions or read your thoughts. Wisdom (Insight) checks to ascertain your intentions or sincerity are made with disadvantage.
  • Darkvision (60 ft.): See in dim light as bright; in darkness as dim (grayscale).
  • Age: Returned do not age in the mortal realm; Aegiron appears as he did at 35.
  • Alignment Drift: Returned begin neutral; often drift evil as ennui sets in. Aegiron is currently Neutral.

Returned-Human Subrace

  • Ability Score Increase: +1 to any two ability scores (different stats). Chosen: +1 Str, +1 Con (already applied).
  • Extra Language: One of your choice. Chosen: Sphinx — fitting for an Odunos noble; sphinxes guard the gates between worlds.

The Returned funerary mask itself — beaten gold, worth ~100 gp per the racial doc — is on Aegiron's face at all times and is a flimsy substitute for an identity. Per Returned lore: it is customary, not magically functional.

He Forgetsθ · Lēthē

Supernatural Gift — None. Aegiron has no chosen patron and no supernatural gift. He has no starting Piety, no Devotee trait, no Pious Protection or Heroic Destiny benefits. He is on his own.

What he has instead is the Returned condition itself: a man whose face was surrendered to the Underworld and replaced with beaten gold. His memories are fragmentary. His pedigree scroll says he was a noble of Odunos; the seal on his signet ring matches; the people who handle his diplomatic introductions assume the man wearing this mask is who the documents say he is, because what else can they assume? Aegiron does not always agree.

The draft Feats table listed a "Pious Protection" entry under a "Pious" source. Per the campaign doc, Aegiron's gift is None — that draft entry was treated as an inconsistency and removed.

Why Aegiron is on the Expeditionο · Hodós

Family Crisis. Aegiron will encounter a family crisis, but with the twist that, at least initially, he doesn't know it's his family. The Returned losing all long-term memory ties this hook to the race itself with surgical precision — Aegiron walks among living people who may once have been his wife, his children, his rivals, his commander, and he meets them as strangers.

The Fallow Year is going to surface old grievances and old debts across Theros, and at least one of them will be Aegiron's. He travels because the necropolis is too quiet to remember anything in; the road has more provocations, and provocations are how the mask cracks.
