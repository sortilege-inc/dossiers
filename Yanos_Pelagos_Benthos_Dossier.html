I. Overview

Yanos · Servant of Thassa

Yanos Pelagos Benthos was born with a pearl in his mouth — a sign, his people understood, that Thassa had chosen him. On coming of age he was released to the goddess, to wander the oceans and ensure that the respect owed her was paid, one way or another. He made himself useful to ships, as a number of tritons do. Unlike most tritons, he would occasionally mark a vessel for tribute, calling storm and the oceanic beasts to bring the sacrifice to Thassa's heart.

He did not consider these sacrifices in moral terms. They were, to his mind, the functioning of the world — or, at most, a means of avoiding a more terrible tribute, such as the coastal cities one tsunami from being swept away forever. He treated the matter fatalistically. The sea gives and the sea takes. You cannot fight the tides.

When the story opens, Yanos has recently been picked up by a ship — the lone survivor of a terrible shipwreck.

The Voice in the Shell
Some time ago Yanos found a nautilus shell, through which he sought auguries and divinations of Thassa's will. The oceans spoke back, and Yanos found power and knowledge through it. He believed the voice was Thassa's. It was not. The warlock pact that flows through him is the gift of an entity from the Fathomless. Yanos never recognised the distinction. He thought he was a priest, twice-blessed. He was a priest, and something else.
Balance. The sea gives and takes, great is the sea. Yanos — stated ideal

VIII. Companion — Kana

Through the nautilus shell Yanos met Kana, a giant octopus, who has come and gone over the years and aided him at need. Kana is Yanos's familiar — granted by GM ruling as a giant octopus rather than the small octopus that Find Familiar would normally allow, and present in the world more in the manner of an independent ally than a constantly-summoned familiar.

Note
Kana's form (giant rather than small) and behaviour (independent rather than perpetually at-hand) both override the standard Find Familiar / Pact of the Chain rules. Yanos understood Kana as one of Thassa's emissaries. Like the voice in the shell, the truth of what Kana is and what sent her may be more complicated than that.

IX. Background — Sailor

Yanos sailed on seagoing vessels for years before the campaign opened, facing down storms, monsters of the deep, and those who wanted to sink his craft to the bottom. The horizon was his first love. The shipwreck that left him a lone survivor put him on the deck of the ship the party joined.

Skill ProficienciesAthletics, Perception
Tool ProficienciesNavigator's tools, vehicles (water)
FeatureShip's Passage — when he needs to, he can secure free passage on a sailing ship for himself and his companions, in exchange for assisting the crew during the voyage.

Appearance

Yanos is a triton. His purple-blue skin is webbed and needs to be soaked every few days at minimum. His eyes are foam green. In place of hair, his webs and fronds flatten against him or fan out. If he wears anything at all, it is a simple tunic; he will begrudgingly dress more as a sailor should he need to go in to port. He wears a pearl earring high on his left "ear."

XI. Piety to Thassa

Yanos's relationship to Thassa is governed by piety — accruing it for acts that expand her influence, losing it for acts that diminish it. Piety to Thassa increases through acts such as:

  • Supporting those who would reform or overturn institutions
  • Preventing cataclysmic change
  • Offering a treasure to the sea
  • Defending or maintaining a temple to Thassa

And decreases through acts such as:

  • Trying to keep a secret from Thassa
  • Using magic to calm the sea's fury
  • Upholding an institution not devoted to Thassa
  • Bowing to the desires or demands of another god
An Unresolved Ledger
Yanos believed his piety to Thassa was being measured — accruing for his devotions, his temple-work, his offerings, and (in his framing) for the tribute-sinkings as well. Whether Thassa was the one keeping that ledger, or whether something else was reading it on her behalf, is among the things Yanos never asked about.

XII. Campaign Notes

Across the campaign, Yanos worked mostly as a guide and protector, helping ships make their crossings safely — the work many tritons do. On rare occasions (a small minority of crossings) he marked a ship for tribute and helped sink it instead. He held both of these as expressions of the same devotion to Thassa, the same balance of giving and taking. He did not see them as separate moral categories.

The campaign closed with an encounter with githyanki, whose ship Yanos helped repair. When the work was done, he went with them — into the seas above, the Astral Sea, to carry Thassa's bounty there.

My loyalty is first and foremost to the sea, then to the ship. Yanos — stated bond
What He Never Learned
The campaign ended without Yanos discovering that the voice in his shell was not Thassa, and that his warlock powers came not from her but from an entity of the Fathomless deep. He departed into the Astral Sea content, faithful, and unaware. From the perspective of the goddess he believed himself to serve, what he carried into the seas above may have been Thassa's bounty — or it may have been something else, wearing the shape of her work.
