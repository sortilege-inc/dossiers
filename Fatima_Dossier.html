subject: who i am (the real story) [ pinned post ]
mood: determined music: something only i can hear location: his bedroom window

Everyone at Shoalsburg High thinks they know the story. Poor Fatima, dropped her books one day, got crushed at the Dynacorp site two weeks later, parents suing, very sad. They light candles for like a month and then forget.

They don't know the real story. The real story is that Ashby and I were always going to find each other. The accident wasn't the end of anything. It was the part where the universe finally said okay, now you can stop pretending you have anywhere else to be.

I've been keeping this journal so when we're together for real, he'll be able to read it and understand. Everything I did. Why I did it. He'll see it was all for him.

(He helped me pick up my books once. He bent down and his hair fell into his eyes and he laughed and said here and gave me back my copy of Wuthering Heights. That was the day I knew.)

subject: the town (in case you don't know it) [ setting ]
mood: chilly music: wind across an empty quarry location: the rim, looking down

Shoalsburg is the kind of town where the most important thing about it is gone. The quarry used to be everything — paychecks, gossip, the way the dust got into your hair on a windy day. People stopped using it and the town started dying politely.

Then Dynacorp showed up to industrialize what was left. New site, new fencing, new humming under your feet at night. They built the place I died at.

The Harvest Ball got cancelled this year because Dynacorp pulled the sponsorship. (My parents are suing them. They won't drop it, no matter how much I ask. They think they're doing it for me. They're doing it for themselves.)

( I overheard Ms. Rosewater talking about Dynacorp once when I was where I shouldn't have been. Some of what she said matters. Some of it I'm still working on. )

subject: at the party (i finally got him alone) posted: oct 9 · 03:47 a.m.
mood: ELECTRIC music: the music of the spheres (he could hear it too) location: a dark corner, his lap

I want to remember every second of tonight forever, so I'm writing it all down.

before the party

  • spied on Ms. Rosewater (Dynacorp things — saving for later)
  • Kyle was being a teacher's-pet about the quarry. Gina was making a map from his notes. cute. useful.

the drive over

Ashby and Joey had just had sex. I could tell. They came out of Joey's house with that look. They offered me a ride. I convinced Ashby to spend the first part of the party with just me. Joey didn't like it. Joey doesn't get to have an opinion.

the corner

  • I hinted that Joey was cheating on him with Gina. He flinched. I felt it.
  • I told him I knew his secret. He got flustered. He started to change.
  • I led him to a dark corner. I rubbed his shoulders. I stroked his hair. I helped him come down.
  • It weirded him out that I don't have a scent. And it also calmed him down. Both at once. That's what we are.
  • He turned anyway. I sat on his lap.
and his ears were pointed and his teeth were long and i wasn't afraid because the only thing in the whole world that could hurt me wasn't his teeth, it was him looking away, and he wasn't looking away, he was looking RIGHT at me —

then joey came at us

Ashby and Joey were both under the influence of the music of the spheres (don't ask, I don't fully understand it either, but it was everywhere tonight). Joey came at us pissed. I sucked away his breath. Ashby attacked me — and his claws went right through me. Of course they did. He can't hurt me. Joey ran away.

Vesk was somewhere being a problem with drugs. Manuel was doing something with the slide of his trombone. The "Amazement Zone" was apparently underwhelming. None of it mattered.

subject: a deal posted: ??:?? · ??
mood: finally music: none. perfect quiet. location: somewhere with no clocks

Bill took me to see the Celestials. He took me to see the dolls.

He offered me an arrangement. I gave him Manuel. He gave me a doll of Ashby. Animate. Not independent. Mine.

the player had said, before the campaign even started, that fatima's redemption arc would be her realizing that her actions — or more honestly her inactions — were her own responsibility. that the accident was an accident, that the spurning was a story she'd written, that she could let him go.

that is not the arc that happened.

the arc that happened is the one where every time the door of self-knowledge cracked open she pulled the doorknob off and put it in her pocket and walked further into the house.

I have what I wanted. I always knew I would. People said I was delusional. Look at me now.
