On the Subject

Yakub was born to a Cuman raiding clan on the open steppe — his early childhood was tents, horses, and the constant motion of a band that lived on what they took. At around seven he was given (or sold; he no longer remembers which) to a wealthy Persianate merchant in a city he could not name, where he served as a household servant for the next decade. The household was a comfortable one and he was treated well enough, but he was never of them. He filed and observed and read what books he could.

One stormy night when he was perhaps eighteen, an otherworldly voice spoke to him in a dream. He did not understand what it wanted — he only knew that the world he had been taught to accept was a lie, that something underneath it was speaking. He left service. He found the Mystic Order of Samarkand, scholars and seers who studied the tears in reality and the unpredictable powers that emerged through them, and he gave himself to their study. They named him al-Samarqandi in time. He took to it.

Twenty years have passed since. He has seen things. He fought once in a battle — the only battle of his life — and walked away from it the only man who did. A close friend died in another circumstance, and he holds himself responsible. He watched a djinn level a town from a hillside and was marked by it: a scar across his ribs that does not heal, that aches when the wind shifts. He came to Eris because the order's other masters spoke of balance and pattern and his life had taught him that the world is none of those things. Discord made sense. Discord made him make sense.

He delves into the secrets of chaos along the Silk Road, seeking to uncover the primal forces that move beneath it. His powers are volatile and he is feared and revered by those who know what he is — which is not many.

Class Features

Cleric — Base
Spellcasting
Level 1 · Wis-based

Wisdom is his spellcasting ability. Spell save DC 14 · Spell attack +6. Prepares spells equal to Wis mod + cleric level = 7 spells per day, from the full cleric spell list. Plus domain spells (always prepared, no count). Spellcasting focus: a holy symbol of chaos.

Channel Divinity
Level 2 · 1 use/short or long rest

One option per use. Available options: Turn Undead (standard), Chaotic Displacement, or Disturbance in the Fabric (Chaos Domain).

Ritual Casting
Level 1

He may cast a cleric spell as a ritual if the spell has the ritual tag and is in his prepared list. Combined with the Ritual Master feat below, his ritual casts use the spell's normal casting time rather than the +10-minute ritual surcharge — once per long rest.

Chaos Domain — Empires of the Silk Road
Domain Spells
Always prepared

  • Suggestion 2nd

Domain spells are always prepared and do not count against the 7-spell preparation limit.

Voice of Chaos
Level 1 · Highest-slot trigger

When Yakub expends a spell slot of the highest level he currently has (at L3: 2nd-level slots), he rolls on the Chaos Table (d20). The GM may also trigger rolls in other situations no more than twice per session. At Cleric 20 he ignores the table.

Unpredictable Might
Level 1 · Reaction · 2 uses/long rest (= PB)

When a creature within 30 ft. casts a spell of 1st level or higher, he may use his reaction. First, he makes a Wisdom save (DC 10 + spell's level). On a success, he applies one of the following effects:

  • Change Target — single-target spell to a new target within range.
  • Change Type — acid/cold/fire/lightning/poison/thunder to any of the others.
  • Change Origin — AoE spell with non-caster origin shifts 10 ft.
  • Cause Harm — after a healing spell, deal 1d6 psychic to target or caster within 30 ft.
  • Cause Heal — after a damaging spell, restore 1d6 HP to target or caster (not him).
Channel Divinity · Chaotic Displacement
Level 2 · Choose 4 willing within 120 ft.

Utters a word; he chooses four willing creatures within 120 ft. He does not need to see them but must know their locations. All four swap locations randomly and gain temporary HP equal to 2d4 + 2 (PB), and advantage on the next save they make until the end of his next turn.

Channel Divinity · Disturbance in the Fabric
Level 2 · 30-ft. radius · maintained

He touches enemies' deepest fears. All enemies within 30 ft. make a Cha save (DC 14) or perceive their allies as enemies for the duration. Each turn he makes a DC 15 Wis save to maintain; on failure he takes 1d6 psychic (increases by 1d6 per 2 cleric levels) and the effect ends. Maintained 1 minute = full effect; concentration-like.

Scholarly Tradition

Yakub recognizes through which means to find a specific piece of knowledge, or how such knowledge could be lost to ages. When his player asks "where would I learn about X?", the GM gives a real answer — a specific library, a known scholar, an oral tradition.

Specialty · Occult Scholar of the Mystic Order of Samarkand

The Order studies the tears in reality and the unpredictable powers that emerge through them. Yakub is one of their senior brothers, despite not being born to it — his cell in the Order's house in Samarkand holds two decades of annotated notes.

Connections

  • Nazim ibn Yusuf — They have met twice, both in the Mystic Order's library in Samarkand. Nazim was passing through to consult a fragment of Qara Bitig glossary that the Order had acquired from a Kievan exile decades earlier; Yakub was the senior brother on duty in the reading-room. They spoke for half an hour the first time about the relationship between Old Turkic kurgan-inscriptions and the sealing-circles the Mystic Order kept catalogued in their inner library. They spoke for two hours the second time about the same subject and a margin of related ones, and Yakub asked Nazim if he had ever encountered a particular glyph in his fieldwork. Nazim said he had not. He had.
  • Safiyya bint Tariq al-Qıpçaq — They have not met. If they do, the meeting will be uneven: Yakub is forty-one and twenty years into his bargain with the unseen; Safiyya is twenty-one and three years into hers. He will recognize her almost immediately — the cloud-eyes are visible, and the Mystic Order keeps records of seer-line bloodlines from the Kipchak and Cuman steppes. He may know her grandmother's name. (He almost certainly does.) She will recognize him by his quiet — the particular quiet of a man who has spent a long time listening to a voice that is not his own — and will not know, at first, whether he is an older version of what she is becoming, or a warning about what she might become. He will not tell her which. He may not himself know.
