I. Identity & Role

Secretary Nomos is Zenith's communicator, its interpreter, its second conscience. He is not the unit's muscle — that is Vellpryte. He is not the unit's strategist — that is Keshet. He is the unit's voice, and he is the only member of the team who consistently understands the people on the other side of the table before they speak.

He came to the Office from a world of craftspeople and makers, and he approaches his work with an artisan's standards: the case should be handled not merely correctly but elegantly. A clumsy judgment is an offense to him at a level that has nothing to do with legality. He believes craft is a moral position.

His class is Troubadour, subclass Auteur. He seeks drama from story and recount, using performance to manipulate the sequence of events unfolding before him. His heroic resource is Drama itself — and unlike the other members of Zenith, he does not earn it by being orderly. He earns it when something goes wrong. The first time a hero is winded, the first time someone falls, the first time a villain lands a devastating blow — Nomos gains Drama. He is powered by the story getting worse. This is deeply inconvenient for a Memonek, and he knows it.

A badly-handled case is not merely inefficient. It is ugly. Ugly work is wrong work. I will not produce ugly work. // Nomos, briefing note to the unit prior to first Orden deployment

Cultural Background

Culture: Artisan Guild — Urban, Bureaucratic, Creative. He was shaped by institutions that value craft, formal process, and the production of things worth keeping. His instinct is to make something lasting out of every engagement. He considers improvisation a failure of preparation — except when he does it himself, in which case it is artistry.

Career: Mage's Apprentice. He studied under a more experienced mage for long years — long enough that the absence of that mage now constitutes a wound he does not have the vocabulary to describe. He learned magic, culture, society, and the dead language Mindspeech. He learned to conceal objects, pick pockets, and track. His mentor did not explicitly teach him those things. He is not entirely certain where he acquired them.

Inciting Incident: Missing Mage. His mentor vanished without explanation. The case is open. Nomos carries it everywhere he goes, filed in the same mental architecture as contract disputes.

III. Drama — The Heroic Resource

Nomos's Heroic Resource is Drama. He gains 1d3 at the start of each turn as a baseline. But the more important generation comes from crisis events — the encounter getting dangerous, going wrong, becoming interesting. He is the only member of Zenith who becomes measurably more powerful as things fall apart. This is not coincidence. It is character.

  • 1d3 — at the start of your turn (baseline)
  • +2 — first time three or more heroes use an ability on the same turn
  • +2 — first time any hero is made winded during the encounter
  • +2 — whenever a creature rolls a natural 2 on a power roll (Melodrama)
  • +2 — first time the Director hits a hero with a Villain action or Malice ability (Melodrama)
  • +2 — first time a hero falls 5 or more squares unwillingly (Melodrama)
  • +2 — first time a hero deals damage with 3 surges (Melodrama)
  • +2 — whenever a hero spends their last Recovery (Melodrama)
◈ Design Note

Nomos selected the complete Melodrama suite. A combat where no one is in danger produces minimal Drama. A combat where Vellpryte is winded, Azimuth is falling, and Keshet has just spent their last Recovery is where Nomos becomes the most dangerous person in the room. His peak power and the unit's maximum peril are identical moments.

A lyrical and physical jab that insults an enemy and inspires an ally simultaneously. The gun is the period at the end of the sentence.

Repositioning (High Cost)

Flip the Script
Main Action · Magic, Area · Burst 3 · 5 Drama cost
A different take on events — Nomos justifies new positions for all allies in a burst. When the formation breaks and everyone is in the wrong place, Nomos pays Drama and writes a new scene. This ability costs exactly the kind of Drama he earns when situations collapse — which is the only time it is needed.

Support (Free Maneuver)

It doesn't need to make sense. Just say it with emotion. A rousing statement that gives an ally an edge on their next test, or a withering remark that gives an enemy a bane. Costs nothing. Is a maneuver. Nomos is doing this constantly — commentary running underneath every engagement.

VII. Complication — The Ivory Tower

Nomos studied in an institution. His reading list was wide-ranging, his training thorough — and when he left school, he discovered there were serious gaps in his understanding of how things actually work. Maybe some of those books were a little out of date. Maybe they simply did not cover the world he actually lives in.

The mechanical function: three skills from his complication, one of which the Director will remove permanently and impose a bane on forever. He cannot relearn it. This is the formal expression of a truth he already knows: there is something he was taught that turned out to be wrong.

The narrative function: his Ivory Tower is not just an educational gap. It is the gap between what he was taught and what he encounters. He can read a person with extraordinary precision in one-on-one interaction. He is sometimes blindsided by how things work at scale, in systems, in places where the theoretical models from his mentor's texts do not map to actual human behavior.

◈ The Practical Gap in Play

Nomos knows Mindspeech, a dead language spoken by exiled psions, but occasionally needs a beat to recall the Orden word for a basic mechanical tool. He knows how Synliroi symbolic communication functions in theory. He did not know that most Orden peasants navigate by landmarks rather than cardinal directions until a field incident required it. Both facts are equally true. Neither surprises him for long.

VIII. Worldsickness — His Method

Nomos's strategy is the most unusual in Zenith: he leans in. Where Vellpryte refuses emotion and Keshet suppresses it through contingency planning, Nomos has found a different approach. He takes the worldsickness and turns it into material. His Useful Emotion trait makes this literal — emotion produces surge. His Drama resource makes it structural — the worse things get, the more powerful his performances become.

This does not mean the worldsickness is comfortable. It means he has found a way to make it useful before having to decide what he actually thinks about it. The question his arc will eventually ask: what happens when he runs out of ways to sublimate emotion into work?

IX. The Missing Mage

Nomos's inciting incident is an open wound, not a closed chapter. His mentor — the mage under whom he studied for years — vanished without explanation. No body. No note. No successor arrangement. No professional courtesy of any kind.

He does not describe this as a loss. He describes it as an unresolved case. It is filed in the same mental architecture he uses for contract disputes and procedural irregularities. This is either healthy professional compartmentalization or a spectacular act of denial. Probably both.

◈ Director Hook

The missing mage should surface slowly, through apparent coincidence — an arcane style Nomos recognizes, a document in a handwriting he knows, a contact who mentions an old teacher in passing. The revelation is most powerful if the answer has been visible all along, and Nomos did not see it because he was too close and too far from his own feelings to look clearly.

X. Position Within Zenith

Nomos is the unit's communicator in every register: the translator between the Office's formal mandate and the human realities on the ground. He pairs with Vellpryte for interrogation (she applies pressure, he reads between the lines). He provides cover and repositioning for the perimeter team. He operates at range during combat, firing his sidearm between verbal interventions — a consistent presence who is always doing something, most of it invisible.

◈ The Interrogation Partnership

Vellpryte and Nomos are Zenith's most effective pairing in non-combat encounters. She creates certainty — there is no escape, no evasion, nowhere to go. He creates a different pressure: the sense that someone in the room already knows exactly what is happening and finds it aesthetically inadequate. Together, they close most cases before Keshet needs a plan and before Azimuth needs to move.

XI. Character Arc

The Central Question

His missing mentor, his artisan's need for elegance in a brutal job, and the growing gap between his education and his experience. // Arc summary, Zenith.md

Nomos's arc runs along three parallel tracks that eventually converge. First: the missing mage — a professional puzzle he is treating as grief he cannot yet name. Second: the elegance problem — the steadily increasing pressure of doing morally complex work that resists clean dramatic resolution. Third: the worldsickness — which he has made productive but not processed. He has found a way to use his feelings without sitting with them.

The resolution comes when these tracks intersect. The missing mage connects to something about the Office, or the lower manifolds, or Nomos specifically, that forces a choice between the elegant handling and the honest one. At that point, converting worldsickness into Drama will no longer be enough. He will have to feel something and not turn it into performance first.

XII. Relations

Executor Vellpryte — Senior Field Agent Active
His commanding officer and most useful professional partner. He respects her precision while finding her emotional strategy aesthetically unsound. He knows the worldsickness is reaching her — he can read it in micro-moments she doesn't notice she's showing. He has not told her. He is waiting for the right scene.
Protocol Keshet — Field Coordinator Active
Professional respect, philosophical opposition. Keshet's planning is the architecture within which Nomos's performances take place. Nomos occasionally improvises in ways the plan did not account for. This produces excellent outcomes and notable friction. Both believe the other's worldsickness coping strategy is unsustainable. Both are probably right.
Operator Azimuth — Psionic Specialist Active
The most comfortable working relationship in the unit. Azimuth's mercenary background means she evaluates him on what he does, not what he knows. He finds this simultaneously humbling and clarifying. She is the only member of the unit who treats his improvisation as a feature rather than a deviation.
The Missing Mage — Former Mentor Open Case
Name not on record. Taught Nomos arcane theory, cultural history, society, and Mindspeech. Vanished without explanation. Classified in Nomos's personnel file as "professional antecedent, status unknown." This is inaccurate. This was the most formative relationship he has had. He is not ready to say that yet.
The Artisan Guild — Culture of Origin Background
He still thinks like a guild member. Work should be done well or not done. Outcomes — like objects — should be worth keeping. He applies this standard to case resolutions, interrogation technique, and the composition of a verbal takedown. The guild did not teach him to think this way. He decided their standard was correct and adopted it.
