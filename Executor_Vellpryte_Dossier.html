I. Identity & Role

Executor Vellpryte is Zenith's senior field agent — the unit's commanding officer, primary combatant, and judicial authority. She designates a target through her Judgment ability and systematically dismantles them while the rest of the team manages everything else. Her ancestry traits make her difficult to bypass and impossible to surprise: I Am Law prevents enemies from moving through her space, Unphased means she cannot be made surprised, and Nonstop governs her movement.

Her class is Censor, subclass Paragon, with the Fate domain. She is a divine enforcer who specializes in setting an example for her order. Her domain grants her Oracular Visions — hazy glimpses of what's coming — which she spends to grant edges on tests within range. Combined with her Judgment, this means she is the unit member most able to shape the outcome of any specific moment.

Cultural Background

Culture: Bespoke — Urban, Bureaucratic, Martial. Hand-built. She was raised in cities, inside institutions, by warriors. Career: Watch Officer. She served as an officer of the law before joining the Office of Mediation. The career grants her broad investigative reach — Alertness, Eavesdrop, Search — alongside two practical languages (Anjali and Hyrallic) and a perk for catching falling allies at the last possible moment.

III. Wrath — The Heroic Resource

Vellpryte's Heroic Resource is Wrath, the Censor's measure of righteous escalation. It accrues steadily and accelerates specifically around her judged target — the creature she has formally designated for resolution.

  • 2 — at the start of your turn (baseline)
  • +1 — the first time each round a creature judged by you deals damage to you
  • +1 — the first time each round you deal damage to a creature judged by you
◈ The Judgment Loop

Wrath generation is tied to active engagement with the judged target. The more directly Vellpryte and the target are exchanging damage, the more Wrath she accrues — and the more abilities she can fuel. The mechanic models a process of formal escalation: a target has been judged, the judgment is being executed, the executor's authority grows as the resolution proceeds.

VI. Paragon Subclass & Fate Domain

Paragon — Judgment Order Benefit

Per the Paragon subclass description: "Without a strong example and a firm hand, the weak will be corrupted. You specialize in setting an example for your order."

The subclass grants a Judgment Order Benefit. The first time on a turn Vellpryte uses Judgment to judge a creature, she can vertical pull the judged creature up to twice her Presence score (4 squares). The judgment isn't just designation — it physically draws the target toward her.

Fate Domain — Oracular Visions

Each time Vellpryte earns 1 or more Victories, she earns an equal number of fate points. Whenever she or any creature within 10 squares makes a test, she can spend 1 fate point to tap into a vision of the outcome, granting that creature an edge on the test. Any remaining fate points are lost at the next respite.

Fate points are a separate economy from Wrath — they accrue between encounters via Victories rather than during them, and they support the entire team, not just Vellpryte.

IX. Complication — Fiery Ideal

From the Draw Steel complication: "A spirit beyond your comprehension instilled in you a special purpose, choosing you to be the guardian of a place, a cause, or a philosophy. The flame that now burns in your soul can sear your enemies — or you if you fall short of expectations."

AspectEffect
Fiery Ideal Benefit While fighting on behalf of her special purpose, whenever she obtains a tier 3 outcome with a damage-dealing ability, she deals extra fire damage equal to her highest characteristic score (+2).
Fiery Ideal Drawback Whenever the Director determines that she acts against her purpose or fails to live up to its standards, she takes fire damage equal to 5 + her level (6 at level 1). This damage cannot be reduced in any way.
⚠ Tension Point

The drawback is irreducible. Any time the Director rules that Vellpryte has acted against her purpose, she takes 6 fire damage — no armor, no resistance, no recovery interaction. This makes the question of what her purpose actually requires mechanically significant. Per Zenith.md, the central tension of the campaign is law versus justice: she "must rule regardless, and the team must enforce regardless," and her arc asks whether she can sustain her refusal of emotion indefinitely.

X. Worldsickness Posture

Per Zenith.md, Vellpryte's relationship with worldsickness is refusal. She will not bend, will not be caught off guard, will not slow down. Her ancestry trait selections support this stance mechanically: Unphased prevents surprise, I Am Law prevents bypass, Nonstop regulates her movement. Her posture is structural.

The campaign's central question for her, also per Zenith.md: can she sustain the refusal to feel indefinitely, and what happens to her judgment when she can't?

The specific syndicate, region, and details of the attempted assassination are not recorded in the source file. The mechanical hook — a powerful adversary that wants Vellpryte dead and has the resources to act on it — is established. Specifics are available for the Director to develop in play.

XII. Position Within Zenith

Per Zenith.md: Vellpryte is the unit's senior field agent — the decision-maker in the chain of command. She designates the primary target via Judgment and locks them down in melee; Keshet and Azimuth form the perimeter team managing all secondary threats; Nomos supports from range. The hierarchy is real: "Vellpryte decides, the others execute."

Per Zenith.md, the most charged narrative moments occur when professional obligation and personal conviction diverge — when Keshet's tactical read says withdraw but Vellpryte says finish the job, when Nomos's social instincts say the Office is being manipulated but the Executor decides to proceed.

XIII. Character Arc

From Zenith.md: "Can she sustain the refusal to feel indefinitely, and what happens to her judgment when she can't?"

Two mechanical structures pull against each other across the campaign. The first is the Memonek trait suite (Unphased, I Am Law, Nonstop) — a posture of refusal expressed as ability text. The second is the Fiery Ideal — a complication that imposes irreducible damage whenever the Director rules she acts against her purpose. The arc lives in the space between these: a character mechanically reinforced against external pressure, while subject to an internal standard that can punish her at any time.
