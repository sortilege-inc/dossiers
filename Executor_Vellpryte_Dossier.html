I. Identity & Role

Executor Vellpryte is the senior field agent of Zenith, the Office of Mediation's contractual enforcement unit currently deployed in the lower planes. She is the unit's decisive edge — its designated closer, its final word. When the Office hands down a judgment, Vellpryte is the hand that delivers it.

She is a Memonek: inorganic, porcelain-blue, built for a world of perfect rational order. On Axiom, emotion is a social fashion, worn and discarded by choice. On Orden — this low-energy manifold of sorcery, gods, and mud — emotion arrives unbidden, unwanted, and at full force. Vellpryte's strategy is refusal. She has refused every emotional intrusion since the unit arrived. Whether that discipline is sustainable is the central question of her arc.

Her Censor class defines her operationally. She designates a target through her Judgment ability and then systematically dismantles them. She is not a general combatant. She is a verdict made manifest. Her kit — a reflavored Retiarius, rendered as magnetic net grenade and taser nightstick — gives her the tools to pin, punish, and pursue. She does not need mobility. The target comes to her, or she closes until they have nowhere left to go.

The law is not a suggestion. It is architecture. You don't argue with a wall. // Attributed to Vellpryte, recorded during the Alloy transit, pre-deployment

Cultural Background

Culture: Bespoke — Urban, Bureaucratic, Martial. Vellpryte was not raised in a standard Memonek context. She was hand-assembled for the job. Cities, institutions, martial training — these are the three pillars of her formation. The Office did not recruit her; it built something close to her and then waited for her to choose.

Career: Watch Officer. Before the Office, she policed the upper manifolds as an agent of institutional order. She interrogated, intimidated, tracked, and apprehended. She did not doubt the law she enforced. She has never been asked to enforce a law she disagreed with — not yet.

Inciting Incident: Powerful Enemies. Someone, somewhere in the timescape, wants her specifically neutralized. She does not know who. She does not know why. She finds the uncertainty more irritating than the threat.

III. Combat Profile

Operational Role

Vellpryte's function in Zenith's tactical formation is singular: designate the primary threat and eliminate it. She does not manage secondary targets. That is Keshet and Azimuth's work. She does not support from range. That is Nomos. She closes, she locks, and she does not leave until the target is down.

Her kit produces two instruments: a magnetic net grenade that restrains and slows targets, and a taser nightstick that stuns. Both are reflavored from Retiarius weapons. Together they enforce the principle she embodies — you are not going anywhere until this is resolved.

Judgment (Core Class Feature)

Judgment
Maneuver · Self
Vellpryte designates one creature she can observe as her judged target. While that creature remains judged, she generates Wrath by engaging them directly. The judged target is her professional mandate — everything else is secondary. Wrath drives her higher-tier abilities and increases the precision of her strikes.

Free Strike (Kit)

Taser Nightstick
Free Strike · Melee 1
Reach 1. Damage: Might vs. Stamina. The taser delivers targeted electrical disruption — painful, precise, and debilitating rather than lethal. The weapon is authorized-force only. She doesn't need it to be lethal. She needs them to stop moving.

Tactical Notes

◈ Formation Note

Vellpryte holds the front while Keshet and Azimuth handle perimeter. Nomos supports from range. The formation's success depends on her lockdown of the primary target — if that breaks, pressure cascades to everyone simultaneously. She is the anchor. When the anchor drags, the whole ship goes.

⚠ Failure Mode

If a second major threat emerges, or if the primary evades her net, Vellpryte cannot disengage efficiently. I Am Law prevents enemies from passing through her space, but it does not help her chase a fleeing target. Keshet must identify secondary threats before they become her problem.

◈ Notable Absence

Vellpryte has no art, no crafting, no animal handling. She has no social negotiation skills beyond fear and authority. She is not a diplomat. When the situation calls for softness, she turns to Nomos. When it calls for finality, she handles it herself.

VII. Complication — The Fiery Ideal

Vellpryte's complication is physical. It is not metaphorical. If she acts against her purpose — if she abandons a judgment, if she enforces a rule she believes is unjust, if she betrays the principle that defines her — she takes irreducible fire damage. The law, in this case, is not a value she holds. It is a constraint on her body.

The damage is administrative. I am operating within acceptable parameters. // Vellpryte, post-incident medical note, first deployment month

The Fiery Ideal has two implications for play. First, it means she cannot compromise — not without cost. A morally complex case, an unjust contract, a victim who is also a guilty party — these are not merely philosophical problems. They are hazards. The GM should treat them as such.

Second, it means her discipline is not purely chosen. Her refusal to feel emotion, her insistence on process and order, her iron-rigid application of judgment — these have a mechanical enforcer. She is kept on the rails not just by character, but by physics.

The question the campaign should eventually ask: What happens when the fire is the right answer?

⚑ Director Flag

The complication is most dramatically useful when the case requires Vellpryte to choose between her institutional loyalty and her emerging moral conscience. This will eventually happen. It should not happen in the first session. But it should happen before the campaign ends.

VIII. Worldsickness — Velloparatha

Vellpryte's strategy regarding worldsickness is refusal. She will not bend, will not be caught off guard, will not slow down. She is a creature of Axiom deployed into a manifold of chaos and emotion, and her response is to deny that the chaos is reaching her.

She is wrong. The worldsickness does not care about her strategy. It builds gradually, arrives without warning, and hits at full force when it arrives. The question is not whether it will break through — it will — but what form her first genuine involuntary emotion will take, and what she will do about it afterward.

IX. Position Within Zenith

Vellpryte holds command authority over Zenith. The hierarchy is real, not nominal — she decides, the others execute. This creates the most interesting dramatic tension when professional obligation and personal conviction diverge.

ColleagueDynamicTension Point
Secretary Nomos Interrogation pairing — she applies pressure, he reads between lines His social instincts sometimes say the case is being manipulated. She decides whether to act on that or not.
Protocol Keshet Tactical division of labor — she drives, they plan Keshet's read says withdraw; Vellpryte says finish the job. One of them has to be wrong.
Operator Azimuth She is the throttle; Vellpryte is the direction Azimuth's comfort with collapse concerns Vellpryte, who views every plan deviation as a failure state.

Command Principle

Vellpryte does not lead by inspiration. She leads by certainty. Her team follows her because she is always clear about what needs to happen next. The moment she becomes uncertain — genuinely, visibly uncertain, not merely recalculating — is a crisis for the entire unit.

X. Adversary Notes

Vellpryte's most thematically charged adversaries are those that corrupt or invert her core value: order should serve justice.

Devils — Bureaucratic Corruptors Threat
Devils represent order perverted into self-serving manipulation. A devil offering Vellpryte a "perfectly logical" contract is the most dangerous encounter she can face — not because of combat power, but because she might actually take the deal.
Valok — Ancient Automata Watch
Inorganic intelligences following ancient programming, still executing orders from masters who vanished millennia ago. The question they pose: is blind obedience to old orders the same as lawfulness? Is Vellpryte's devotion to the Office any different?
Synliroi / Lord Syuul Threat
Syuul would find Memoneks fascinating specimens — inorganic beings with ordered minds outside his usual biological purview. A Synliroi fascinated with Vellpryte specifically is a campaign-level threat that also activates her worldsickness in novel ways.
Ajax's Forces — Corrupted Order Threat
Ajax imposes law without justice — a tyrant who uses the mechanisms of order to serve conquest. He is the thing Vellpryte's entire existence is meant to prevent. She will need to confront the possibility that well-intentioned law enforcement can produce Ajaxes.
Unknown Powerful Enemy Threat
Someone wants her neutralized. She doesn't know why. The discovery of who is likely connected to the campaign's central conflict — and to whatever makes Vellpryte specifically dangerous enough to warrant targeting.

XI. Character Arc

The Central Question

Can she sustain the refusal to feel indefinitely — and what happens to her judgment when she can't? // Arc summary, Zenith.md

Vellpryte's arc is not about whether she will experience emotion. She will. The arc is about what she does when her first genuine uncontrolled feeling collides with a decision she must make as Executor. Can she still rule clearly when she is grieving? Can she still enforce a judgment she has begun to doubt? Does the law she upholds require that she be inhuman — and if so, what does it mean that she no longer is?

◈ Player Note

Vellpryte was built top-down, premise first. She is thematically coherent because the player thought hard about what her tensions mean before filling in the mechanical details. The complication is load-bearing. The worldsickness is load-bearing. Don't treat them as obstacles to minimize — they are the character.

XII. Unit Relations

Secretary Nomos — Communications & Support Active
The pairing of Vellpryte's pressure and Nomos's perception produces the unit's best interrogation results. He reads people; she locks them down. What she misses between the lines, he catches. What he needs enforced, she delivers. The relationship functions precisely because they do not overlap.
Protocol Keshet — Field Coordinator Active
Keshet plans; Vellpryte executes. The tension is structural — their roles are complementary but their instincts can diverge at the worst moments. Keshet sees every contingency. Vellpryte sees the target. When the plan requires her to stop pursuing, she finds it difficult. This is not insubordination. It is architecture.
Operator Azimuth — Psionic Specialist Active
Azimuth is the unit's throttle; Vellpryte is its compass. Azimuth's comfort with chaos — her trust in lucky collapses — unsettles a Memonek who believes contingencies are failures of planning. Their worldsickness coping strategies are philosophical opposites: Azimuth absorbs chaos; Vellpryte refuses it.
The Office of Mediation — Principal Authority Institutional
The Office is her employer, her mandate, and her legal sanction. She serves it without doubt — currently. The complication to watch: what happens if the Office's judgment diverges from justice? The Fiery Ideal runs both ways. She cannot act against her purpose. But who defines her purpose?
