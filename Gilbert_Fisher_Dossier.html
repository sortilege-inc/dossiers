Subject Background — In His Own Words

DFPD-31a

Gilbert Fisher is, by every account given by faculty and peers, a popular, handsome, athletic, and excellent student — the kind of boy a small town points to when it wants to feel good about itself. His parents, Anna and Larry Fisher, run a local construction company. He has one sibling, Hannah, ten years his junior. He believes in hard work and dislikes freeloaders; in his own words, "if you believe that someone is a hard worker, you will go to great lengths to help them out and advocate for them." He sees himself as a good, courageous person who tries to make the world a better place. He is not one to back down from a fight. He sees himself as a natural leader.

His greatest joy in life is sports. He is a prominent member of all the Devil's Fork High athletic teams, and fully expects to win a college scholarship — for swimming, in particular, which he describes as his "true gift." Beyond this surface, the subject volunteers no significant information about his interior life. He is fifteen.

Anomalous Physiology — Sealed Addendum

SEALED · NDA

The subject possesses functional gills. They are normally invisible and visible only when underwater or when the subject deliberately concentrates on them. As a result he can breathe underwater indefinitely and cannot drown. He is not aware of the cause. The only other living person to whom he has disclosed this is his mother, Anna Fisher, who has advised him to be thankful for the trait and not to draw attention to it.

SPECIAL MODIFIER  //  +1 to all swimming-related skill rolls. Cannot drown in water.

Note added during investigation: the subject's grandmother, Shelly Fisher, vanished from this town in 1963 at the age of 17 (see Pattern Index, item b). She was never found. No connection is presently being drawn in the open file.

Pattern Index — Disappearances, Devil's Fork

DFPD-9 · Research Handout

Through diligent research, the investigators have identified a pattern of young people who have gone missing every twenty years in Devil's Fork. The pattern extends back through the history of the town for more than a century.

  • 1983— 20 yrs Peter Foote — Patricia's brother, age 6, presumed drowned.
  • 1963— 40 yrs Shelly Fisher — Gilbert's grandmother, age 17. Photographed with her husband (Gilbert's grandfather) in front of the old gas station on Maple Road (closed some years ago). Shelly went missing, abandoning her two young children. Never found.
  • 1943— 60 yrs Finnegan Black — a young man who went missing, presumed runaway, at age 16. No photo on record.
  • 1923— 80 yrs Prudence Shore — black-and-white photo of the girl, missing at age 14. In the photo she wears jewelry similar to the kind Jesse's aunt sells to tourists at the Devil's Fork Jewelry & Crafts shop.
  • 1903— 100 yrs Jonathan Sharpe — a simple notice posted in a 1920s paper requested information about Jonathan Sharpe, age 10, missing.
  • 1883— 120 yrs Simon Bass — obituary for Simon Bass, age 6, presumed drowned in the lake. Body never recovered.
  • 1863— 140 yrs Coral Woodhall — an ancestor of Alex's, whose obituary appeared in a church bulletin printed not long after the founding of the town. Coral was 15; an avid swimmer who loved to swim in the lake early in the morning. Presumed drowned. Body never recovered.

Investigator's note (Fisher): the twenty-year interval is exact within the margin of error of small-town record-keeping. Two of the names on this list — Shelly Fisher and Coral Woodhall — are blood-kin to two of the involved parties. The current year falls on the cycle.

Incident Report — Recovery of A. Woodhall

DFPD-5 · Incident
Status ★ Resolved / Withheld Filed by: Subject & Associated Witnesses

Per the subject's testimony, the missing girl Alexis Woodhall was located in an underground cave system connected to the river, in the company of a number of townspeople who were in the process of offering her up to creatures the subject describes as "fish-people." Alex was found drugged and restrained, and gave no indication of voluntary participation.

The other investigators — Patty Foote, Jesse Shore, Lynda Sharpe, and Gordon Black — were, by the subject's account, engaged independently elsewhere in the system, fighting fish-people and causing chaos. The subject did not coordinate his actions with them at the moment of recovery.

The subject took hold of Alex and entered the water. He swam for the shore by way of the river, using his gills openly. A storm was over the valley. Despite the weather and the pursuit, he reached the shore. He and Alex thereafter left Devil's Fork.

Out-of-Character & System Notes

DFPD-99

System: Fear Itself (2nd Edition) — a GUMSHOE horror game by Pelgrane Press in which ordinary modern people stumble into supernatural horror and rarely come out the same. Investigative abilities (Academic / Interpersonal / Technical) always succeed when their use is declared; General abilities (Athletics, Fleeing, Hiding, Scuffling, Shooting, Stability, &c.) are spent from depleting pools against opposed tests or thresholds. Stability and Health are the pools that measure surviving the experience.

Campaign: Alexis is Missing — a one-shot. Five teenage classmates from Devil's Fork High investigate the disappearance of one of their own, in a small town whose twenty-year cycle of disappearances turns out not to be a coincidence. The scenario concluded with the rescue of Alex and the subject's flight from town.

Player Character: Gilbert is a player character. The dossier is presented in the voice of the Devil's Fork P.D. case file because that is the genre furniture the story put on the table — a missing girl, a small town, and what was always underneath both.
