I. The Subject in Brief

Malek is a mutant halfling of the burned lands — a field medic of unusual constitution, employed in turn by many crews across his career. Some of those contracts were free arrangements. Others were forms of indenture. He moved between them because he was useful: he could close a wound, he could set a bone, and he could endure desert conditions that broke other healers. He was, by most reckonings, a competent and reliable hand in the wastes.

He also, on more than one occasion, was required to leave a place in some haste. The reason was consistent. Malek had eaten a baby, and he had not asked permission first.

What follows compiles his confirmed traits, capabilities, the contents of his pack, and one recorded field action drawn from his time with a party of giants and halflings in the eastern wastes.

IV. Professional Pattern

A Useful Hand

Malek's professional record across his career consisted of crew after crew. Some of the contracts were free arrangements between consenting parties. Others were forms of indenture. The terms varied; the reason for hiring did not. He could treat wounds, and he could keep functioning in heat that put other healers down. For a field medic in the wastes, that combination is worth a great deal.

Hardiness

His mutation made him suited to the sun in a way ordinary halflings are not. He could work where others rested. He could march where others stopped. The same mutation made him poorly suited to cold, but the cold rarely came up.

The Recurring Departure

Across multiple postings, Malek's tenure ended in the same fashion: he was found to have eaten a baby, and he had not asked permission. He did not consider the matter as serious as those around him did. Other parties consistently disagreed. The pattern was sufficiently established that it is the single most documented feature of his employment history.

V. Recorded Field Action

Session of 30 June 2024 · Eastern Wastes Expedition

The Oasis Settlement

Malek spent time at a settlement built around an oasis, in the company of half-giants, halflings, and itinerant workers. Water access at the oasis cost one chit per week. The local economy ran on chits and on the wealth represented by domesticated sheep and kinks — the large insect-like creatures used as beasts of burden.

To establish himself with the Giant community, Malek offered his services as a bone-setter. A sheep belonging to a Giant shepherd named Brook had injured its leg after a near-encounter with one of the oasis amphibians. Malek used his psionic Lift to raise the animal off the ground, soothed it while it panicked at being airborne, and set the sprained ankle. The work was good enough that the Giants saw no need to put the sheep down. This earned Malek a degree of standing with Brook and access, at the standard rate, to the oasis water.

The March

The party then set out across the wastes toward an old bandit tower — roughly thirty-six miles, six days of expected travel, across oasis terrain, sand dunes both easy and difficult, and a region of stony badlands. Malek tolerated the journey well; his Heat Adapted mutation gave him advantage against the heat of the sun, though it did not reduce his standard water requirements. During the second day he took temporary Constitution damage from the conditions, which faded with rest.

The Encounter

Around three in the morning, the party spotted a humanoid creature roughly the size of a half-giant, hunting alone. Malek opened by casting Weaken on it from range — the power manifested in the target as a sudden headache and an evident drop in coordination, imposing a penalty to its attacks and defense. The creature engaged anyway.

During the fight, Malek used Grasp to seize one of the creature's massive fists in mid-swing, neutralizing one of its attacks for a round.

The Save

A member of the party — Hugo — was struck down, taken to zero hit points by a punch and a fall of roughly ten feet, with internal bleeding. Malek, still concealed by his once-per-day Stealthy, ran into hand-reach of Hugo and cast Mend with advantage. The casting succeeded. The first round of focused mending healed one hit point, which was enough to stabilize and rouse Hugo. Malek then spent five further rounds maintaining the focus and healing Hugo back up. Stealthy held throughout — though the GM ruled it would have broken had Malek been the one prone and dying.

After

With the creature dead, Malek produced his bone saw and removed one of its arms, processing it for rations from the corpse.

VII. Campaign House Rules (For Reference)

Inferior Materials. Weapons not made of metal — bone, stone, obsidian — have a chance to break on a maximum damage roll, a critical hit, or a critical fail. Bone, fortunately, is cheap to replace.

Psionic Manifestation. Psionic powers resolve as a d20 plus bonus, attempting to beat DC 10 + the power's level. Failure carries the risk of mishap. Powers manifest in the target as a headache; psionics are common enough in this world that a target may suspect the cause, or may blame food or water as an animal would.

Encumbrance & Travel. Average travel is roughly eight hours per six miles. Forced marches beyond eight hours require Constitution rolls; failure imposes exertion (a −1 penalty to everything) until 24 hours of rest in a comfortable place.

XP. The campaign uses "Puncher Mode" — half of a monster's level is awarded as XP for defeating it, in addition to standard Shadowdark XP sources.

Critical Wound Effects. Critical hits with edged weapons force a Constitution save against ongoing bleed (1 damage per round). Critical hits with bludgeoning weapons force a save against being stunned for a round.
