I. Chronicle of Origin

The desert does not care what you are. It cares only what you can endure. Malek, by all accounts, can endure a great many things — and has an appetite for a great many more. — Overheard at the Tyr water-market, attributed to a caravan master who no longer employs him

No living record names the halfling warren that produced Malek, and those who once knew have mostly ceased to be. He surfaces first in a House Shom caravan ledger — a minor notation describing a "small, grey-skinned healer of unusual constitution, useful in the wastes." That entry is crossed out. The reason is listed as administrative, which in Athasian commerce typically means a body was involved.

He is a mutant, as Athasian halflings sometimes are: skin the colour of storm-shadow on basalt, eyes burning amber-gold that cast faint light in full darkness, teeth overcrowded and serviceable. He is small even by halfling measure, and he moves through tall grass and crowded markets alike with the low patience of something that has learned to wait.

He carries no family name and has given three different birthplaces to three different employers. Investigators have found no record corroborating any of them. What is consistent across every account: he heals well, he withstands the killing sun without complaint, and sooner or later he must be removed from proximity to infants.

IV. Operating Character

The Healer Calculus

Malek is worth more than he costs — and he knows it, and so does every crew that has employed him. The desert kills efficiently: dehydration, wyvern-spine, green-glass fever, sword-cuts that go septic in the heat. He treats all of these without ideology about who deserves treatment. Templars, escaped slaves, sorcerers, and caravan thieves have all been stitched back together by his hands. Payment is the only moral framework he publicly acknowledges.

Hardiness in the Field

He does not complain about the sun. He does not require shade. He does not panic at water rationing. Field commanders consistently note his usefulness in the deep waste, where most healers cease to function after two days. He has been known to operate on a wounded man in direct noon-sun without visible discomfort. This is not bravery. He simply is not affected.

The Appetite

Malek has, on multiple recorded occasions, consumed infants without the consent of parents, guardians, or supervising crew leadership. He does not regard this as morally distinct from eating any other desert creature. He has learned, pragmatically, that others do not share this view. He will not do it in front of witnesses if it can be avoided. Crews employing him are advised to brief leadership staff accordingly and restrict his unsupervised access to crèches, nurseries, and settlement perimeters when his dietary needs are otherwise unmet.

Mobility Pattern

He moves laterally — from crew to crew across the same quadrant of wasteland — rather than fleeing into unknown territory. He understands the desert better than he understands cities, and he is not stupid about which risks are manageable. His longest gap between documented incidents is eleven months, during the Red Tether Company period, suggesting adequate provisioning suppresses rather than eliminates the behavior.

He looked at my son the way I look at rations on a long march. Not with malice. With hunger. That is, if anything, worse. — Testimony, Salt Road caravan master, Balic city arbitration
