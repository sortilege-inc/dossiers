I. The Subject in Brief

Malek is a mutant halfling of the burned lands — a field medic of unusual constitution, employed in turn by many crews across his career. Some of those contracts were free arrangements. Others were forms of indenture. He moved between them because he was useful: he could close a wound, he could set a bone, and he could endure desert conditions that broke other healers. He was, by most reckonings, a competent and reliable hand in the wastes.

He also, on more than one occasion, was required to leave a place in some haste. The reason was consistent. Malek had eaten a baby, and he had not asked permission first.

The dossier that follows compiles his confirmed traits, capabilities, and the contents of his pack at the close of his recorded career.

IV. Professional Pattern

A Useful Hand

Malek's professional record across his career consisted of crew after crew. Some of the contracts were free arrangements between consenting parties. Others were forms of indenture. The terms varied; the reason for hiring did not. He could treat wounds, and he could keep functioning in heat that put other healers down. For a field medic in the wastes, that combination is worth a great deal.

Hardiness

His mutation made him suited to the sun in a way ordinary halflings are not. He could work where others rested. He could march where others stopped. The same mutation made him poorly suited to cold, but the cold rarely came up.

The Recurring Departure

Across multiple postings, Malek's tenure ended in the same fashion: he was found to have eaten a baby, and he had not asked permission. He did not consider the matter as serious as those around him did. Other parties consistently disagreed. The pattern was sufficiently established that it is the single most documented feature of his employment history.
