I. Portrait & Appearance

Aurora Adelphi Uluru
Moon Elf · Age 33

Aurora is slender, fair-skinned, with blue eyes and dark brown hair. She is 5'11" and weighs 141 lbs. Her most distinctive feature is a set of Pict-like facial tattoos — fine, angular marks that suggest a tradition of body inscription uncommon among city elves, more at home in deep-forest lineages or isolated hermitic orders.

She carries herself with the stillness of someone accustomed to long silence. Years of solitary hermitage have made her unhurried — not slow, but deliberate, as though she is always half-listening for something beyond the immediate room. Those who watch her closely note that her stillness sometimes crosses over into absence: there are moments when she is, for all observable purposes, somewhere else entirely.

Her companion is Olor — a boreal owl whose name translates from colloquial Elvish as dream-vision. The name was not chosen idly.

V. Class & Race Features

Moon Elf

Fey Ancestry

Advantage on saving throws against being charmed. Magic cannot put her to sleep.

Trance Elf

Elves do not sleep. Aurora meditates for 4 hours a day — the Common word for this state is 'trance.' During meditation she can dream after a fashion; such dreams are mental exercises that have become reflexive through years of practice. After 4 hours of trance she gains the benefits of 8 hours of sleep. What constitutes a dream for Aurora has grown considerably harder to define.

Darkvision Elf

Accustomed to twilit forests and the night sky. Superior vision in dark and dim conditions: she can see in dim light within 60 feet as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. She cannot discern colour in darkness — only shades of grey.

Druidic Elf Language

She knows Druidic, the secret language of druids, and can use it to leave hidden messages. Others who know the language automatically spot such a message. A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check will spot its presence but not decipher it.

Bathed in Moonlight Moon Elf

She can attempt to hide when she is only obscured by dim light.

Lunar Magic Moon Elf

She knows the minor illusion cantrip. When she reaches 3rd level, she can cast sleep as a 2nd-level spell once with this trait, recovering the ability on a long rest. (Not yet reached at current level.)

Druid (Level 2)

Wild Shape Druid 2

As an action, she can magically assume the shape of a beast she has seen before. This feature is usable twice between short or long rests. Her druid level determines the beasts she can transform into: at 2nd level, any beast with a challenge rating of 1/4 or lower that has no flying or swimming speed.

Available beast forms (CR 1/4 or lower, no fly/swim): Baboon, Badger, Cat, Deer, Giant Fire Beetle, Goat, Hyena, Jackal, Lizard, Rat, Scorpion, Spider, Weasel; Camel, Giant Rat, Giant Weasel, Mastiff, Mule, Pony; Axe Beak, Boar, Draft Horse, Elk, Giant Badger, Giant Centipede, Giant Goat, Giant Lizard, Giant Wolf Spider, Panther, Riding Horse, Swarm of Rats, Wolf.

Balm of the Summer Court Moon Druid 2

She is a font of fey energy that offers respite from injury. She has a pool of fey energy represented by a number of d6s equal to her druid level (currently 2d6). As a bonus action, she can choose one creature within 120 feet and spend any number of those dice up to half her druid level. The target regains hit points equal to the total rolled, and also gains 1 temporary hit point per die spent. She regains the expended dice on a long rest.

Discovery: Dream Hermit Background

She can cast Dream once per day without materials, preparation, or a spell slot. If she casts it upon herself, she can communicate with her mentor.

Sorcerer (Level 1) — Wild Magic Origin

Wild Magic Surge Sorcerer 1

Immediately after she casts a sorcerer spell of 1st level or higher, the DM can have her roll a d20. On a roll of 1, she rolls on the Wild Magic Surge table, creating a random magical effect. A surge can happen once per turn. If a surge effect is itself a spell requiring concentration, it does not require concentration.

Tides of Chaos Sorcerer 1

Once per long rest, she can gain advantage on one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. Before she regains the use of this feature, the DM can have her roll on the Wild Magic Surge table immediately after she casts a sorcerer spell of 1st level or higher — at which point she regains this feature's use.

VII. Personality & Character

Personality Traits

I needed to commune with nature, far from civilisation. I am utterly serene, even in the face of disaster.

Ideal

Live and let live; meddling in the affairs of others causes only trouble.

Bond

Nothing is more important than my relationship with my mentor and order.

Flaw

I keep my own counsel, and do not readily share insights.

The contradiction at the centre of her character is precise: she values non-interference as an ideal and yet the thing she holds most important — her relationship with her mentor — is a relationship of total dependence. The mentor has refused explanation for what is happening to her. She has accepted this without confrontation. Her flaw and her bond are, in practice, two names for the same disposition.

VIII. Backstory

The Relic and the Forest

In early adulthood, Aurora became heir to a peculiar relic: a glass orb, perforated, housing a single living Tillandsia ionantha — 'vilyolva' in colloquial Elvish. This is her druidic focus. The previous guardian had died of old age, and nothing prepared her for what was to come. That first night, meditation turned to sleep, and she encountered a person in her dream who advised her on a journey deep into the forest, and promised wisdom and strength. Upon waking, she resolved to commit to the endeavour, and spent the following decade in seclusion.

Every night upon meditating, the voice returned — teaching herbal lore, the secrets of animals, the cruel and compassionate ways of nature. For a decade, this was the whole shape of her life.

The Disturbance

A few months before the campaign begins, her dreams grew strange and troubled. This continued until she awoke suddenly to find herself surrounded by animals fallen into an enchanted sleep. When they awoke, she spoke to them and discovered that she had called them to her through her dreams. As the days passed, she occasionally found herself dropping into sleep and performing magical actions. Her mentor refused explanation — refused even to acknowledge responsibility. Increasingly she found herself acting both in sleep and whilst awake.

Disturbed, she journeyed out of the woods and began seeking other means to understand what was happening to her. The campaign begins here: at the edge of the forest, carrying the relic she did not ask for and the magic she did not choose, in search of an answer her mentor will not provide.

On the Nature of Her Magic

The meeting point of Wild Magic and oneiromancy is unusual. Her druidic magic is learned, deliberate, earned through a decade of patient study. Her sorcery is not. It is older, stranger, and — if the surges are any indication — not entirely under her direction. The dreams began the disturbance. The magic followed the dreams. Whether the magic is the cause of the dreams, or the dreams are the cause of the magic, remains unresolved.

IX. Notable Events of Play

Event Log · I In Combat · The Fireball

She Fell Asleep Mid-Battle, Cast Fireball on Herself, and Survived

During a combat encounter, Aurora fell asleep. This was not a tactical decision. It was not a spell effect from an enemy. She simply went under — her oneiromantic affliction asserting itself at the worst possible moment, pulling her out of waking action in the middle of a fight.

While asleep, she cast Fireball — centred on herself.

Wild Magic Surge

Fireball is not on the Druid spell list, and she had no access to it as a Sorcerer 1. The most consistent reading is that this was a Wild Magic Surge — the chaotic sorcerous element asserting itself through the dream-state, selecting a spell from beyond her known repertoire. The mechanics do not cleanly accommodate this. The fiction does.

She survived. At 23 hit points with Constitution +2 and the fortitude that a decade of forest hermitage builds, she was able to absorb the blast. That she survived is the remarkable fact. That she cast it at all is the disturbing one.

What This Moment Tells Us

She is not in control. Not of the dreams, not of the magic that follows the dreams, and not — when the two overlap — of what her sleeping self will do in the world. The gap between her waking self (serene, deliberate, a decade of cultivated stillness) and her sleeping self (capable of casting offensive area magic at point-blank range on her own position) is the essential tension of the character. The moment is absurd and it is terrifying, and she survived it, which means she had to wake up afterwards and understand what had happened. What she made of that understanding is not recorded.
