On the Subject

Khalid was raised in the shadow of Alamut. His father — or the man who told him he was his father — was Sinan, a name spoken with reverence in the order and silence outside it. Whether the lineage is true blood or true allegiance, no one in Alamut bothered to clarify, and Khalid learned not to ask. He was put to the order's schooling at six. He learned the Qur'an in Arabic, the Ginans of the Nizari tradition in Persian, and the order's quieter texts — the ones that name angels Ismaili scholars do not name aloud — in an Esoteric script that exists in perhaps two hundred copies in the world. He learned the blade at twelve. He learned what to do with it at sixteen.

The order does not call its work killing. It calls it consequence. A Nizari blade, the masters say, is the conscience of the Caliphate made physical — a way of moving political weight that a small mountain state cannot move by armies, fielding nothing it cannot afford to lose. Khalid does not entirely believe this. He has killed three men under formal contract and two more in field-improvisations; one of the latter still surfaces in dreams. He has not yet asked the Imam for guidance about it.

He left Alamut on assignment a year ago. The assignment is not finished. He has not gone back, but he has not gone rogue either; he reports through the order's cell-network at the longer caravanserais and takes new contracts when they find him. He moves under a half-dozen working names along the Silk Road and has answered to Khalid for perhaps three of his twenty-four years.

He does not know that Alamut has perhaps a year and a half left before the Mongols arrive. The order's seers do; they have not told the field-agents. Whether the orders he receives in the coming season are real contracts or evacuation-preparations dressed as contracts is something he will have to learn to read for himself.

Class Features

Rogue — Base
Expertise
Level 1

Doubles proficiency bonus on two skill proficiencies. Chosen: Stealth and Sleight of Hand. Reflected in the +7 bonuses above.

Sneak Attack
Level 1 · Once per turn

Once per turn against one creature he hits with an attack, if he has advantage on the attack roll or if another enemy of the target is within 5 ft. of the target (not incapacitated) and he doesn't have disadvantage. Must use a finesse or ranged weapon.

Sneak Attack 2d6 extra damage at Rogue L3 — finesse or ranged weapon required
Thieves' Cant
Level 1

Knows the secret rogue argot — a code blended into normal conversation. He can hide a message in apparently-normal speech that other Thieves' Cant speakers will understand.

Cunning Action
Level 2 · Bonus action

On each of his turns he may take a bonus action specifically to Dash, Disengage, or Hide. Combined with Skulker and dim light, this is the engine of his style.

Hashashin — Empires of the Silk Road
Esoteric
Level 3 · Subclass core · Cha-based

Order-training grants Proficiency in Religion, one extra language (taken: Esoteric / Hashashin cant), and the Ibrahamic-lore expertise listed in Skills above. Once per long rest he may choose one cleric cantrip and one cleric 1st-level spell:

  • Guidance cantrip
  • Inflict Wounds 1st

The cantrip he casts at will at a cost of 1 psychic damage per casting; the 1st-level spell he casts once per long rest without a spell slot at a cost of 1d4 psychic damage. This damage cannot be prevented by any means. Charisma is his spellcasting ability for these spells. A holy symbol or "blessed blade" (melee weapon, metal, holy carvings, ≥ 25 gp) serves as his spellcasting focus — his engraved shamshir qualifies.

Dreadful Arts
Level 3 · Reaction · 2/long rest (= PB)

When a creature he can see within 60 ft. targets him or an ally within 10 ft. of him with a spell, he may use his reaction to force the creature to make a Wisdom save DC 12. On a failed save, the creature must choose a new target or lose the spell, and is frightened of him until the end of his next turn.

Alternative trigger: if the targeting spell has an area of effect, he may instead use his reaction to grant himself or a creature within 10 ft. advantage on the saving throw. 1/long rest, separate from the PB-based uses above.

Sneak Attack via Blessed Blade · Universe Note
House-ruling flag · GM discretion

Khalid's peak burst combines Inflict Wounds (3d10 necrotic, melee spell attack) with Sneak Attack (2d6) — averaging ~24 damage on a hit. Strict RAW reads Sneak Attack as "finesse or ranged weapon attack," and a spell attack is not a weapon attack. The charitable reading — that the spell is channeled through the finesse blessed blade — would allow the combo. The GM may rule either way; this dossier flags the question rather than answering it.

Feat

Skulker
Level 1 · Granted feat (no ASI applied)

Alamut's training-houses are built into the Elburz mountainsides — corridors of dim oil-lamp light, half-buried in mountain shadow. Khalid learned to read faces and watch corners by candlelight before he learned to fight, and his eyes have not lost the habit.

  • He can try to hide when lightly obscured from the creature from which he is hiding.
  • When hidden from a creature and missing it with a ranged weapon attack, the attack does not reveal his position.
  • Dim light does not impose disadvantage on his Wisdom (Perception) checks relying on sight.

Background — Outcast

Guiding Winds

He can find fresh water sources, fruitful hunting grounds, and safe camping spots in the wilderness, away from natural hazards. Useful for travel between caravanserais on long stretches between safe houses.

Specialty · A Man Who Keeps to Wild Places

In Khalid's case, "outcast" is half a true description and half a working cover — he travels alone, dresses plainly, sleeps outside the walls when discretion calls for it, and is comfortable both in wilderness and in the disreputable margins of cities. His order tells him to be no one's neighbor; he is good at it.

Your Journey

Lifepath · rolled and chosen entries from the Silk Road book

Birthplace
A Nizari Ismaili daʿwa-house in the Elburz mountains — an outlying chapter-house of Alamut, or a small village mosque attached to the order's network. Born under the order's care.
Family
Taken as servant of the order itself. He is not certain whether Sinan, his father-of-record, is biological or adoptive. The order does not say.
Siblings
None. He is alone in his cohort.
Lifestyle
Poor. The order does not pay its agents in gold; it pays them in shelter, training, and the certainty of their place in the cosmos. On assignment he eats well — operational expenses are reimbursed. Off-assignment he lives lean.
Strings to Arcanum
His community knows the arcane is real. Alamut keeps texts on djinn-binding, on the Names of God, on the older esoteric arts of the Fatimid Caliphate, and on the practical psychic-cost magic the order teaches its assassins. Most outsiders see superstition; he sees the seam of the cloth.
Past Education
Educated through unorthodox ways — the order's inner schooling at Alamut and outlying chapter-houses. Order-internal masters teaching scripture, languages, esoteric texts, statecraft, and the blade. Grants the Dragonchess / Chess set proficiency — the order plays it for tactical training.
Event I · d20 = 13 · Witnessed something horrible

A field-improvisation killing at age twenty-two.

One of his two non-contract kills. The man surfaces in dreams. Recorded as second flaw — see Personality below.

Event II · d20 = 11 · Lost something important

A wax-sealed letter delivered to Tabriz.

He carried a small wax-sealed letter from the order's senior master at Alamut to a chapter-house in Tabriz. He delivered it. He has not been told what was in it. He suspects, given subsequent events, that the letter named someone who has since died; he is not certain it was not him doing the killing. Open thread.

Event III · d20 = 19 · Religious faith changed completely

He privately suspects he serves a faction, not the Imam.

Not in the obvious direction — he is still a Nizari Ismaili, will be one when he dies. But his understanding of what the order is has shifted. He believed, until recently, that he served the Imam. He now suspects he serves a faction within the order that may not have the Imam's full sanction. He does not know what to do with this suspicion. He has told no one.

Event IV · d20 = 6 · Met someone important — hostile attitude

A Persianate-court official knows his face.

A vizier or qadi who knows Khalid's face from a contract that was not supposed to leave witnesses. The official has not yet moved on him. He is waiting for the right moment, or the right protection. GM-assigned figure; not yet named in chronicle.

Profession

Silk Road PEX system · Apprentice rank

Smuggler
Apprentice · Rank 1 PEX 0

Right Tools for the Job (free): proficiency in Disguise Kit and Forgery Kit (Thieves' tools already from Rogue — no duplicate grant). Never Tell Me the Odds (free, Cha-floor): when making any Charisma-related ability check, he cannot roll lower than 7. At Cha 15 / +2 mod, his social-roll floor is 9.

Khalid's "smuggling" is not commercial in the usual sense — he does not move spices for profit. But the supplement's Smuggler profession captures the practical mechanics of his order's work better than any other: moving people, messages, and small objects past gates and inspections; concealing identity; reading underworld contacts. The Apprentice abilities cleanly support the Hashashin operative-on-the-road life.

Personality

Trait

"I have answered to six names in twenty-four years. Khalid is one of them. The others are not your business."

Ideal — Order · Lawful

"The world is a series of accountings. My order keeps a ledger most people do not see. I am one entry in it. So is everyone I have killed. So, eventually, is everyone."

Bond

"My order taught me to read, to think, to kill. I owe it everything I am. I am beginning to suspect I do not owe it everything I do."

Flaw

"I do not know whether Sinan was my father. The order has not told me. I have not asked. I am afraid of both possible answers."

Second Flaw — Event I

"I dream of a man I killed in Tabriz two years ago. He was not a contract. He was in the way. I cannot tell, even now, whether I killed him because I had to or because I was afraid."

Connections

  • Yasmina al-Khorasani — They have crossed paths three times in two years, at three different caravanserais between Tabriz and Merv. The first time Yasmina noticed him because he was the only other guest in the courtyard who refused the wine and watched the doorways instead of the dancers. The second time he noticed her because she was the only other guest in the courtyard reading a small folded paper she did not want anyone to see. The third time they nodded at each other across a fire and did not speak. They have a wary, unspoken professional respect — both work in margins where seeing-clearly matters, and both have learned not to be the first one to ask the other's name. Neither yet knows the other's order. Both suspect.
  • Aftab Singh the Arbiter — They have not met. If they do, they will be opposite ends of the same axis — Aftab's Karma (Reason, Responsibility, Consequence, Duty) against Alamut's Order (Concealment, Patience, Killing-as-Accounting, Loyalty to the Imam). Aftab will recognize Khalid for what he is within an hour of conversation; whether he names him is a separate question. Khalid will recognize Aftab for what he is in roughly the same span; the question for Khalid is whether the recognition makes Aftab an asset, a threat, or — uniquely difficult — neither.
