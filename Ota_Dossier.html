I. Identity & Standing

Ikoma no Hosokawa Ota was born Yogo, of the Scorpion clan, and was placed within the Yogo Penitent Order — a monastic order of the Brotherhood of Shinsei, attached to the Scorpion. He was released from the Order into the service of Ikoma Kenji, and Kenji had the Hosokawa formally adopt him and bring him into the Lion clan legally. His sheet records his family as Yogo and his clan as Lion: the legal change and the formation that preceded it both remain visible on the file. His role is bushi; his school is the Penitent Order. He has not yet been played.

Physical Description (from the portrait)

The portrait shows a broad-shouldered man with warm-toned, weathered skin and dark eyes. His long dark hair is bound up high in the samurai topknot, with strands escaping at the temples; he wears a short moustache and a small chin-beard. He is dressed in a plain white kimono with a red sash about the waist and a bold golden sash worked with fine red thread crossing one shoulder; he holds a yari in one hand. His expression is composed and watchful rather than expressive.

Manner of Presence (from Step 4)

The sheet records what stands out about him: he is grounded and responds to provocations; he does not react to them. Responses are always considered, although sometimes that consideration occurs in fractions of a second. Step 15 adds that he refuses to be touched by others while disregulated or in physiological distress. His default stance is Earth.

Current Obligation & Drive

Ikoma Kenji sought out the Yogo monastery. He has felt cornered by his beloved brother, who refuses to recognize reality, and Kenji needs an ally who can help him overcome his aversion to conflict; and if needed, initiate that conflict. Current giri
Ota longs to be a peacemaker, but does not have the liberty to contest his lord's framing nor leave his lord's service to serve others more in keeping with his desire. Current ninjō

On Bushidō

Ota believes that bushidō is something that must be strived for, and must be failed at. Step 8

On Death

Ota hopes that his death is fated before he is forced into doing something irreversible and terrible on behalf of his lord. Step 20

III. Disposition & Bushidō

Step 4 sets his standing posture: grounded, responsive rather than reactive, deliberate even when fast. Step 15 sets the boundary: he refuses to be touched by others while disregulated or in physiological distress. Step 8 sets his philosophy: bushidō is something that must be strived for and must be failed at. Honor is paramount; Compassion is the less significant of the two named tenets.

Reading: the tension between the two tenets

Honor and Compassion are both named on the sheet, with Honor placed paramount. The ninjō identifies what he wants — to be a peacemaker — and explicitly notes that he is not at liberty to act on it under his current obligation. A reasonable inference: the gap between Honor (which his current service satisfies) and Compassion (which his ninjō reaches for) is the engine of his interior, but the dossier does not state this directly.

Skills Read Against Role

His sheet carries the bushi role but the skill distribution is closer to a monk-in-training than a frontline fighter. Martial skills sit uniformly at 1 (Fitness, Melee, Unarmed, Meditation). Theology is 1, Skulduggery is 1, Performance is 1, Labor is 1, Composition is 1, Culture is 1. Social-courtly skills (Courtesy, Command, Games, Sentiment, Government) are at 0. Reading: the build supports defensive martial action and ritual work but not court manoeuvring; Ardent Leader is the only social lever on the sheet, and it does not depend on a courtly skill rank.

Stance & Technique Read

Earth is his stance and his Fitness-keyed kata (Snapping Branch Strike) is Earth-rooted. Void powers the school ability (Oath of Sacrifice) and both rituals (Divination, Threshold Barrier). Veiled Menace Style is also Void. Reading: the technique loadout couples a defensive martial trigger (Oath of Sacrifice) with two ritual capabilities; the build appears designed to absorb harm and convert it into kihō output, rather than to initiate combat.

IV. Backstory & Interior

The twenty-questions answers describe a particular shape, recorded verbatim below in the language of the sheet:

Clan Relations (Step 7)

Ota no longer has a relationship with the Scorpion clan; when the monastery released him into Ikoma Kenji's service, he was released fully. That doesn't mean the Scorpion won't try to reinsert themselves someday, however. But Kenji had the Hosokawa formally adopt Ota and bring him into the Lion clan legally.

His Greatest Success (Step 9)

Upon grieving his greatest humiliation, Ota dedicated himself to the monastic arts, and found excellence in the practice of air kihō; even though he is no longer bound to the Yogo, he knows that should he ever have need, he need but ask.

His Greatest Difficulty (Step 10)

The other Hosokawa do not know who Ota was, and the reasons for adoption were made opaque to them, but they cannot help but believe there is a darkness there, and sometimes they can see that darkness in Ota's eyes.

What Calms Him (Step 11)

Ota delights in selecting and preparing a gift that is a perfect match for the recipient.

What Worries Him (Step 12)

Ota remembers burying his sister alive. He tries not to. But part of him cannot shake the horror, and he refuses to allow himself to be similarly trapped.

What He Has Learned Most From (Step 13)

Yogo Kitohime never asked what Ota had done. Never judged him. She knew his burden intimately, like he knew hers. She focused him on the present, not the past.

First Impression (Step 14)

The bold golden sash with fine details in red thread that is tied to Ota's yari matches the sash he wears, and is unusual for the Hosokawa.

Stress Behaviour (Step 15)

Ota refuses to be touched by others while disregulated or in physiological distress.

Parent & Childhood (Step 17)

As a child, Yogo Ota didn't get to see much theater, but what little he witnessed sparked his imagination and he and his sister made many of their own productions around their home.

Heritage — Uncle Ota (Step 18)

Uncle Ota was an accomplished polearm master, who was delighted to teach what he could to his young namesake. He left on his lord's business when Ota was a young teen, and never returned.

Reading: the heritage roll attached a -5 honor modifier and gave Ota the Snapping Branch Strike kata as a heritage item. The honor modifier is recorded; the cause of the uncle's non-return is not on the sheet.

VI. Flags & Open Doors

Items on the sheet that the GM and player have left flagged for play:

  • Strife at 5 of 6 from creation. The mechanical signal of a character arriving close to his composure limit.
  • Step 20 death wish. Ota hopes his death is fated before he is forced into doing something irreversible and terrible on behalf of his lord. A player flag for the campaign.
  • Step 7’s Scorpion door. That doesn't mean the Scorpion won't try to reinsert themselves someday, however. The dossier records this explicitly as an unresolved future.
  • Step 14’s golden sash. First-sight item, deliberately flagged as unusual for the Hosokawa. Provenance is not stated on the sheet.
  • Step 10’s darkness. The Hosokawa can sometimes see that darkness in Ota's eyes. Mechanically expressed in Whispers of Doom; narratively, the door behind it is the Dark Secret anxiety.
  • The Dark Secret itself. Anxiety on the sheet; the underlying fact (Step 12) is that Ota buried his sister alive. The sheet does not record the circumstances, motivation, or aftermath; these are open.
  • Uncle Ota’s non-return. Heritage gave Ota a kata and a -5 honor modifier; the file does not explain the modifier’s cause or the uncle’s fate.
  • Kenji’s brother. Named only in the giri text; not described beyond “refuses to recognize reality.” The conflict Ota was brought in to enable is unwritten.
  • Step 16’s deferred relations. The rest we can figure out in the story. Most of his social field outside the three families is open by design.

VII. Operational Environment

The campaign is set in the City of the Rich Frog (Toshi sano Kanemochi Kaeru). The following situates Ota in that setting; the JSON itself does not specify a location.

The city is a river-trade hub on contested ground, held in a three-clan Imperial neutrality (Lion, Dragon, Unicorn) and run on the ground by the Kaeru-otokodate. The Lion are its longest-investing presence; the Dragon hold the Shrine district; the Unicorn district has grown steadily. The Scorpion are not formally garrisoned in force, but the appointment of a Scorpion as Emerald Magistrate of the city is recent and grumbled-about among the other clans. Ryoko Owari Toshi, the chief Scorpion city, lies downriver, and trade between the two cities is described as fairly brisk.

Where Ota sits

His sheet places him within the Lion clan via the Hosokawa, in Kenji’s service. The lion helm indicates his association with the Ikoma family specifically. Akodo Minami, a young samurai-ko, is named in the campaign lore as the highest-ranking Lion in the city; Ota’s sheet does not connect him to her, only to Kenji and the Hosokawa.

The Scorpion presence

Step 7 leaves the Scorpion door explicitly open. The city’s ambient Scorpion presence — Emerald Magistrate, river trade with Ryoko Owari, occasional opium-den enforcement, fines on traders — provides the surface against which any future Scorpion approach to Ota could be staged. Whether and when this happens is a campaign question.

VIII. Possible Trajectories

He has not been played yet. What follows is not prediction; it is a sketch of lanes the sheet leaves open. The campaign will choose among them or invent others.

Threads the Sheet Names

  • The conflict Kenji wants initiated. The giri identifies this directly. The brother is not named; the shape of the conflict is not specified.
  • A Scorpion reinsertion attempt. Step 7 raises the possibility explicitly. Timing and form are open.
  • The Dark Secret surfacing. Mechanically tracked from day one. The sheet attaches glory and status consequences to exposure.
  • Whispers of Doom encountering its first counter-evidence. The adversity says people who do not know him personally will assume the worst. Knowing him personally is the path through it.
  • The Step 20 outcome. Either resolution — the hoped-for early death, or surviving past the irreversible thing — is on the table.

Mechanical Pressure Points the GM Can Use

  • Confined-space scenes — Claustrophobia: 3 strife per scene that requires entering one; first occurrence per scene yields a Void point.
  • Will-assertion checks — Dark Secret: 3 strife after any such check; first occurrence per scene yields a Void point.
  • Being touched while disregulated — not a strife trigger on the sheet but a Step-15 stress behaviour the campaign can engage.
  • Strife already at 5 / 6 — a single trigger of either Anxiety in an early scene is likely to push him to his Composure limit.

Reading: What the Build Supports

The Air-Void-heavy ring layout, the ritual loadout, and the defensive school ability point toward a character who functions best as Kenji’s steady, watchful second — useful in mixed scenes that involve both martial readiness and ritual or spiritual work, less suited to courtly maneuver or to initiating violence. Ardent Leader gives him a way to land a sentence above his station; the rest of his social skills do not currently support sustained court work. None of this is stated on the sheet directly; it is a reading of the build.
