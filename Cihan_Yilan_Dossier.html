I. Overview

Cihan Yilan is a vizier in the service of the Serref of Ughardi, currently operating far beyond his nation's formal borders in the city of Skudderby and its surrounding territories. He is a pragmatist of the first order — a man who measures every person, institution, and obstacle as a resource to be leveraged, circumvented, or simply ignored when its resistance is too expensive. He is not cruel for its own sake; he is, if anything, indifferent. The harm he causes is incidental to his objectives, and he finds the idea that his goals should be limited by the concerns of people who have no leverage over him genuinely puzzling.

His current mission involves advancing Ughardi interests in Greater Skai through a bridge-construction project over the Lani tributary near Ul-Tanat, a scheme that required forged documents, diplomatic manipulation, secured labor from foreign guilds, and the quiet neutralization of Ughardi officials who refused to cooperate. He has accomplished all of this and considers the setbacks he has encountered to be ordinary friction, not failure.

The party has encountered him and, by all accounts, does not like him. One player-character has already taken an opportunity to do something actively harmful to his interests. He is aware that he has made enemies in Skudderby; he does not consider this a serious problem.

Water flows where it must. — Cihan's reported philosophy on elemental resistance to his construction project

II. Known Particulars

True Nature & Disguise

Cihan is a naga. In public — within Skudderby, at the construction site, and in all diplomatic settings — he presents as a noble human on horseback. The disguise is apparently maintained through magic or naga shapeshifting ability. His saddlebags (which cause no encumbrance) are consistent with a character who genuinely travels mounted; his letter of introduction and calligraphy set are the toolkit of the human nobleman persona. The horse, and what it conceals, should be treated as a permanent layer of the deception. Very few people in Skai Holt know what Cihan actually looks like.

Threat Assessment

Do not underestimate him in a direct confrontation. His Overwhelming Assault stun in round one, followed by Merciless Strike compounding on that stunned condition, followed by a shuriken Distracted debuff feeding further Merciless Strike damage, is a meaningful damage chain. Move as Your Own Piece can force an enemy to attack their own ally. His Serpentine Charming means the party may not know they are in range until it is too late. Latent Potence makes him harder to neutralize at low HP — and more corrupted each time. His cold vulnerability is the clearest tactical opening.

His real danger remains political. His contacts journal, the Pavish correspondence network, Align Interests, and his Unimpeachable flaw mean that even a decisive physical victory against him will likely result in someone else — probably someone the party cares about — bearing the consequences.

IV. Psychology & Operating Method

Cihan respects power and firm obstacles, but his first instinct when encountering either is not confrontation — it is circumvention. He will find the clever way around. He places a high value on leverage and very little value on sentiment. He is not easily rattled by complications because he expects complications; they are the texture of the work, not evidence that something has gone wrong.

He has no apparent ideology beyond Ughardian interest and his own advancement within that system. He does not hate Greater Skai; he simply treats it as a space to operate in. Local populations, their customs, their treaties with elemental spirits, their spiritual claims on land — these are variables to manage, not obligations to honor. He will make concessions to workers' superstitions when the cost of doing so is low, but he will not let them become impediments.

His most characteristic move under pressure is the threat that demonstrates superior resolve: when water spirits warned his workers off the Lani tributary construction site, his instinct was not to withdraw but to suggest building an upstream dam. The message is clear — if you want this tributary to remain, leave us alone. Whether he would have followed through is an open question; what matters is that he was willing to say it and mean it.

Cihan is not involved [in the quarry dispute] except in the initial arrangement, and as the velvet-gloved fist should pressure be necessary to maintain production targets or protect the industry/property. — GM Notes, October 2023

On Territorial Disputes

When operating in legally contested territory — particularly the disputed quarry believed to fall under Skai Holt claim — his diplomatic position is elegant in its cynicism: Ughardi citizens are merely employing Skai Holt citizens and improving their economy. It is not a diplomatic incident unless Skai Holt authorities attempt to physically remove property or shut down operations. This is not an argument he believes; it is an argument he uses because it is hard to counter without escalation.

On Manipulation of Officials

He is skilled at maneuvering people into positions where they have already done the thing, and all that remains is the consequences. The former head of Ul-Tanat — a Skai Holt contact of the party's thief — was manipulated into issuing official orders that were deliberately crafted to be interpretable as treasonous. She did not know this at the time. Cihan co-designed the situation with the Serref's permission, and then helped execute it. The result: she is wanted for treason, there is an extradition order across Greater Skai, and Ughardi is now pursuing her. This is not extraordinary cruelty to him. It is effective work.

V. Current Operations & Known Schemes

The Lani Span Project

Cihan's primary operation is the construction of a bridge across the Lani tributary near Ul-Tanat, within what are generally recognized as Ughardi borders. The project was stalled by the refusal of Nizaj Amut, the disgruntled satrap of Ul-Tanat, to provide official authorization. Cihan resolved this by forging Nizaj's signature and sending letters of confirmation to the Serref — meaning Nizaj is now publicly on record as having approved a project he opposed. He cannot easily repudiate it without implicating himself.

To secure the additional labor and materials the project requires, Cihan found influence within the Pickling Guild in Skudderby. He supplied the guild — specifically Elder Beorn, and through him Beorn's protégé Ikemba — with kruthik brains in exchange for support in finding workers. The documentation for all worker hirings bears Nizaj Amut's (forged) signature.

Pay rates for workers sourced through Skudderby are reportedly better than what Nizaj is known to offer from his own resources, which has helped secure labor and creates a favorable public impression of Ughardi management practices in the region.

Post-Elemental Apocalypse Status

While Cihan was away on official Serref diplomatic business, the elemental forces whose ancient compact with the land had been violated by the construction project acted. The party had delivered Cihan's ultimatum to them; they took the party's side and gave three days' warning before action. That warning was honored. All Skai personnel evacuated. Then the elementals destroyed everything — every building thrown down, every worker not previously departed dead, all materials scattered or ruined.

On his return, Cihan received this news along with an official statement from the Pickling Guild withdrawing support until the safety hazard is resolved, and billing him for lost materials and labor hours. The satrap of Ul-Tanat seized on the catastrophe to publicly blame Cihan for the deaths of Ughardi citizens and to claim this proved the project was unjustifiable from the start.

The GM's summary of Cihan's likely reaction: "Well you know that this means war."

The Disputed Quarry

Cihan identified a quarry optimal for the bridge-building project on land with a legally ambiguous claim: technically within grounds that could be interpreted as Ughardi-usable, but historically claimed by Skai Holt, which had discontinued quarrying there due to what it views as superstitious concerns about something disturbed below. Cihan has moved to exploit this. His strategy: employ Skai Holt locals at favorable wages, making their economic wellbeing dependent on Ughardi continuing to operate there. This makes Skai Holt enforcement politically costly.

Extradition of Urreek

The former head of Ul-Tanat — known as Urreek, contact of the party's thief and cousin of the party's ranger — is wanted across all of Greater Skai on an extradition order that Ughardi has secured broad agreement on. Ughardi soldiers and spies operating in Skudderby in connection with Cihan's project are actively seeking her. She has gone to ground. The party has been asked to find her a way out. If Ughardians find and capture her and the party intervenes, the consequences could escalate into an incident between Ughardi and Skudderby guild leadership.

X. Note on Ferrosis

Cihan was exposed to ferrosis before meeting Fade. The exposure appears small; there are no reports of symptoms. His response to Xeric Umqun's direct pressure to curtail the disease was inadequate by Xeric's measure, though what he said specifically is not recorded.

A tainted shipment of some substance, traced to Ughardi, is believed to be part of a larger spread of the disease — potentially originating not from Ul-Tanat but from further inside Ughardi territory. Cihan's operations brought Ughardi presence to Skudderby; there is a question of whether that presence also brought ferrosis vectors, intentionally or otherwise.

The Pickling Guild is aware of ferrosis in the Brine Coves and is in internal conflict about how to respond. Cihan's party-supplied kruthik brains — which Zalia had a vision about during her time in the hive — are connected to this research. He supplied something that now sits at the center of a spreading health crisis he appears uninterested in resolving.
