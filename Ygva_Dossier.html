Class Features
Umbral Corruption Class
As Ygva gains scars, she is slowly corrupted by the Umbra. When she deals damage, she deals extra damage equal to her number of scars. When she marks her last Hope slot with a scar, she does not retire — instead she succumbs to the Umbra's influence and charges forever into the dark to join the terrors that stalk lightless spaces.
Witch's Charm Hope Feature
When Ygva or an ally within Far range rolls a failure on an action roll, she can spend 3 Hope to change it into a success with Fear instead.
Hex Class Feature
When a creature causes Ygva or an ally within Close range to mark any number of Hit Points, she can mark a Stress to Hex them. Action and damage rolls against a Hexed creature gain a bonus equal to her tier. Only one target may be Hexed at a time. The GM may spend Fear equal to Ygva's Spellcast trait to remove the Hex; otherwise it drops at scene's end.
Commune Class Feature · Once per Long Rest
During a long rest, Ygva communes with an entity — an ancestor, deity, spirit, aspect of nature, or other. She asks a question, then rolls a number of d6s equal to her Spellcast trait and chooses one result. If any dice share a value, she gains multiple effects: 1–2 a taste, scent, or sensation; 3 a feeling relevant to the answer; 4 sounds; 5 a vision; 6 she psychically experiences a relevant scene as if she were there.
Nightwatcher Moon Subclass · Passive
While in moonlight, Ygva gains a +1 bonus to all Spellcast Rolls.
Night's Glamour Moon · Foundation
Mark a Stress to envelop herself in a magical glamour lasting until she marks a Hit Point, makes an attack, or takes a rest. While Glamoured she can disguise herself to look like any creature of her approximate size that she has seen, or enhance her own appearance to gain advantage on Presence Rolls that leverage the change.
Ire of Pale Light Moon · Specialization
When a Hexed creature within Far range fails an attack roll, they must mark a Stress.
Moonbeam Moon · Specialization · Once per Session
Ygva conjures a column of moonlight illuminating the area within Close range until the end of the scene. While bathed in this light, she and any allies gain a +1 bonus to Spellcast Rolls and advantage on rolls to see through illusions.
Lunar Phases Moon · Mastery
At the start of each session, roll a d4 and gain the matching effect until session end. 1 — New: spend a Hope to reduce Minor damage to None. 2 — Waxing: +2 bonus to damage rolls. 3 — Full: +2 bonus to damage thresholds. 4 — Waning: +1 bonus to Evasion.
Endurance Ancestry · Giant
Gain an additional Hit Point slot at character creation.
Reach Ancestry · Giant
Treat any weapon, ability, spell, or feature with Melee range as though it has Very Close range instead.
Steady Community · Ridgeborne
Advantage on rolls to traverse dangerous cliffs and ledges, navigate harsh environments, and use survival knowledge.

Character
Appearance

Ygva stands seven and a half feet tall. She dresses in layered wraps of dark fabric that catch the wind as Oshel's once did. Unlike her mentor, she wears no mask, leaving her face exposed to the elements.

Manner

Her tone is severe and she does not suffer fools. The people of Ulmira's Step seek her out on occasion — she listens and works to their benefit — but her severity has kept the asks limited.

Purpose

Ygva has learned the truth of their predicament: there are worlds above and below this one, and the gods are hatching outward, leaving this world behind as a rotting caul. She has been finding talismans and objects that still retain a hint of the splendour of the gods. She is determined to pull them back and tether them — to bind the gods to a shared fate with the world.

Kelun

Ygva's companion. A stone figure recovered from the mountainside, reactivated after decades of silence. He accompanied her into isolation with Oshel and has returned with her to the mountains above Ulmira's Step. Of late he has been learning, and even seems able to master some rudimentary magic.

History

Ygva was born and raised in Ulmira's Step, a Ridgeborne Shargan settlement high in the mountains of the Halcyon Domain. Her father Arlen is a pyrehauler. Her mother Tirva — whose name in Lammat means wind-marked — is remembered more in lullabies than in stories. Her older sibling Nereth left when Ygva was ten, thoughtful and intense, always looking to the horizon — possibly drawn to the Celsians or the wild glades. Her younger sibling Sela died at three.

Her childhood circle drifted apart one by one. Mellan became a ranger in his mother's footsteps at thirteen, pressing a smooth black river-stone with a white ring into Ygva's hand the night he left, then turning without waiting for anything in return. Sarru began training under a Pyrekeeper guardian named Maedra. Tamros vanished without explanation, returned weeks later claiming to have found a way inside the mountain and to have seen more of the titan at the mountain's base — then was gone again by dawn. When the lector Renavir arrived from the Celsian Athenaeum in Amber Reach, Ygva and Vaetra attended his lessons; the elders did not permit Kelun to join. Each night Ygva recounted everything to Kelun — not just what Renavir taught, but how he said it.

When Ygva was nearly fifteen, the witch Oshel came down from her ledge high above the settlement, sang in old Kurethik, and pointed at Ygva. Vaetra tried to stop her leaving and urged her to take Kelun. Ygva followed Oshel. Kelun came with her. They lived in isolation for five years, studying under Oshel. Oshel did not die — she chose to fade into the Umbra willingly rather than risk returning as a revenant. No rites were spoken; no remains were left.

Ygva and Kelun made their way back to Ulmira's Step. She is now twenty, and has since retreated to the mountains above the settlement.
