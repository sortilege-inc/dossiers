Class Features
Umbral Corruption Class
As Ygva accumulates scars, she is slowly consumed by the Umbra. When she deals damage, she deals extra damage equal to her number of scars. When she marks her last Hope slot with a scar, she does not retire — instead she succumbs to the Umbra's influence and charges forever into the dark to join the terrors that stalk lightless spaces.
Witch's Charm Hope Feature
When Ygva or an ally within Far range rolls a failure on an action roll, she can spend 3 Hope to change it into a success with Fear instead.
Hex Class Feature
When a creature causes Ygva or a Close ally to mark Hit Points, she can mark a Stress to Hex them. Action and damage rolls against a Hexed creature gain a bonus equal to her tier. Only one target may be Hexed at a time; the GM may spend Fear equal to Ygva's Spellcast trait to remove the Hex, or it drops at scene's end.
Commune Class Feature · Once per Long Rest
During a moment of calm, Ygva communes with an ancestor, deity, spirit, or otherworldly being. She asks a question, then rolls a number of d6s equal to her Spellcast trait and chooses one result: 1–3 a taste, scent, or sensation; 4–5 sounds or a vision; 6 she psychically experiences the scene as if she were there.
Nightwatcher Moon Subclass · Passive
While in moonlight, Ygva gains a +1 bonus to all Spellcast Rolls.
Night's Glamour Moon · Foundation
Mark a Stress to envelop herself in a magical glamour that persists until she marks a Hit Point, makes an attack, or rests. While Glamoured she can disguise herself as any similarly-sized creature she has seen, or enhance her own appearance to gain advantage on Presence Rolls that leverage the change.
Ire of Pale Light Moon · Specialization
When a Hexed creature within Far range fails an attack roll, they must mark a Stress.
Moonbeam Moon · Specialization · Once per Session
Ygva conjures a column of moonlight that illuminates the area within Close range until the end of the scene. While bathed in this light, she and her allies gain a +1 bonus to Spellcast Rolls and advantage on rolls to see through illusions.
Lunar Phases Moon · Mastery
At the start of each session, roll a d4. 1 — New Moon: spend Hope to reduce Minor damage to None. 2 — Waxing: +2 bonus to all damage rolls. 3 — Full Moon: +2 bonus to damage thresholds. 4 — Waning: +1 bonus to Evasion. The effect persists until session end.
Ancestry — Endurance Giant
Gain an additional Hit Point slot at character creation.
Ancestry — Reach Giant

Community — Steady Ridgeborne

Character

Appearance

Ygva stands seven and a half feet tall, her Shargan frame hardened into something close to stone by ten years on the Griefcleft peaks. She dresses in layered wraps of dark fabric — heavy, wind-worn, the hems fraying like Oshel's once did — though unlike her mentor she wears no mask, leaving her face open to the cold. Her eyes carry the particular flatness of someone who has spent years measuring distance and silence in equal measure.

Manner

Severe. Not cruel — but she has no patience for wasted words, and the people of Ulmira's Step have learned this. They come to her with real problems now, having burned through the trivial ones early. She listens fully. She judges quickly. She acts without hedging. The mountain taught her that hesitation is just a slower kind of failure.

Purpose

She knows now that this world is a shed skin — that the gods are hatching outward into other places, leaving behind a caul: a membrane of dying splendor, rotting slow. She does not accept this. She hunts talismans and god-touched objects, scraps of divine residue still carrying warmth. Her aim is to tether them, to bind the gods back to a shared fate with the world they made and abandoned. Most who hear this call it madness. She calls it the only honest use of her remaining time.

Kelun

Her companion since childhood — a Clank, one of the stone vessels the gods once spoke through. Kelun traveled with her into Oshel's isolation and came out changed. They have mastered rudimentary sorcery now, seemingly by accident, by way of the words Ygva recited to them over years of dark nights. She has never quite decided if that terrifies her or not. It doesn't stop her from going forward.

History

Born in Ulmira's Step to Tirva and Arlen — wind-marked and the silent pyrehauler — Ygva grew up among the Sacred Pyre's smoke and the carved stone steps of the forgotten pilgrimage. Her older sibling Nereth left when she was ten, drawn to the horizon. Her younger sibling Sela died at three. Grief was the household's second language.

Her childhood circle — Mellan the scout-in-training, Sarru the future Pyrekeeper, Kelun the reawakened Clank, Vaetra the war-hungry reader, Tamros who found something inside the mountain and never explained it — scattered one by one into their callings. Ygva drifted through Renavir the lector's lessons without igniting, watching Vaetra consume history like fuel while something in herself stayed dark and quiet.

Then Oshel came down from her ledge. The witch walked out of the mist, sang in old Kurethik, and pointed at Ygva. No explanation. Ygva followed. Kelun came too. They lived for five years in the Griefcleft peaks, learning the weight of silence, the grammar of Umbra-touched magic, and the shape of a world where the divine was draining away. Oshel did not die. She chose the Umbra over the risk of becoming a revenant, and simply faded into it.

Ygva and Kelun came back down the mountain. Twenty years old. Everything already carved.
