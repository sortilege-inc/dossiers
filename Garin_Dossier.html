I. Identity & Standing

Garin is a Rohirrim woman of an éored riding the plains east of Edoras. She and her fellow riders found Celenneth on the open plains in the long ride home after Fangorn — gone since Súlimë 5, returned at Cermië 1, nearly four months absent. Garin's mare carried Celenneth back to Edoras. Across the chapters of the ride, Garin fell for her quietly, and the chronicle has been clear: her interest is apparently obvious to everyone except its object. Celenneth did not see it. Nothing physical passed between them. Linnea later went to the stables to meet Garin before the next patrol, and the friendship that formed was named by the chronicle: Linnea built friendships with Eadwyn and Garin that are genuine and mutual.

IV. Chronicle of the Encounter

Book VI/VII · The Plains · Cermië, late summer
The éored found Celenneth on the open road.
Celenneth had been gone from Edoras since Súlimë 5; it was now Cermië 1 — nearly four months. The plains after Fangorn: open sky, golden grass. The chronicle: open sky, golden plains, the warmth of human company after weeks of ancient indifference. Garin's mare carried Celenneth home.
Book VI/VII · The Ride to Edoras
Teasing; the riders' glances; the line said without thinking.
You're as sharp as an arrow when it comes to spotting danger, but when someone pays you a compliment, you're as dull as a blunted sword. The other riders noticing and exchanging knowing glances. Celenneth, somewhere along the road: I never stay in a place more than a season. The old reflex speaking before the new reality caught up.
Book VII · Edoras · The Telling
Celenneth told Linnea everything.
By the fire afterward, Linnea asking hold me while you tell me everything. Linnea's response: I didn't want you holding back. The chronicle: not jealousy, not performance of acceptance, but genuine interest and warmth.
Book VII · Edoras · Linnea at the Stables
Linnea introduced herself to Garin before the next patrol.
Proactive rather than defensive. I know what we have. I don't need to fear anyone else. The act of someone who has done the internal work of understanding what their relationship actually is and choosing to honour it on its own terms.
