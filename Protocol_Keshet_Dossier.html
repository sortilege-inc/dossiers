I. Identity & Role

Per Zenith.md, Protocol Keshet is the unit's field coordinator. Per the role description: "Tactical planning, perimeter coordination, battlefield control. Keshet designs the operational approach and coordinates the team's positioning. They work alongside Azimuth as a handler-operator pair managing every threat that isn't Vellpryte's designated target."

Per Zenith.md narrative profile: "Keshet is defined by a failure they refuse to repeat. … Their relationship with worldsickness is suppression through discipline and planning. Every contingency accounted for is one less variable that can make them feel something unexpected."

Cultural Background

Culture: Memonek — Nomadic, Communal, Academic. Per Zenith.md: "The most traditionally Axiomatic member of the team. They didn't acculturate to the lower planes the way the others did."

Career: Warden. From the career description: "You protected a wild region from those who sought to harm it, such as poachers and cultists bent on the destruction of the natural world. Knowing your land well, you could also serve as a guide or the leader of a rescue party for those wandering the wilds." The career grants Nature, Gymnastics, and Sabotage skills, the Voll language, 120 Project Points, and the Danger Sense exploration perk.

III. Focus — The Heroic Resource

The exact gain triggers, from the source:

  • 2 — at the start of your turn
  • +1 — the first time each round that you or an ally damages a creature you have marked
  • +1 — the first time in a round that an ally within 10 squares of you uses a heroic ability

VI. Key Abilities

Core Mechanic — Auto-Granted

Mark
Maneuver · Ranged 10 · One creature · No Focus cost
"You draw your allies' attention to a specific foe — with devastating effect." The target is marked by Keshet until the end of the encounter, until she uses the ability again, until she willingly ends the effect, or until another tactician marks the target. When a creature marked by Keshet is reduced to 0 Stamina, Keshet can use a free triggered action to mark a new target within distance.
Mark: Trigger
Free Triggered Action · Trigger: you or any ally uses an ability to deal rolled damage to a creature marked by you · 1 Focus
Keshet gains one of the following benefits (cannot gain more than one from the same trigger):
  • The ability deals extra damage equal to twice Keshet's Reason score (+4).
  • The creature dealing the damage can spend a Recovery.
  • The creature dealing the damage can shift up to Keshet's Reason score in squares (2).
  • If Keshet damages a creature marked by her with a melee ability, the creature is taunted by her until the end of their next turn.

Career Perk — Warden

Danger Sense
Exploration Perk · Passive in natural environments
Whenever Keshet is in a natural environment (but not in a settlement in that environment), she gains an edge on tests made using the Alertness skill, and she can't be surprised. Additionally, she has a connection to nature that warns her if any natural disaster is imminent within the next 72 hours, though she doesn't know exactly what it will entail (an earthquake, a wildfire, and so forth).

VII. Mastermind — Subclass Features

From the subclass description: "You have an encyclopedic knowledge of warfare, viewing the battlefield as a game board and seeking victory by thinking steps ahead of your opponents."

Studied Commander

Studied Commander
Class Feature · Two distinct effects
Effect 1 (party-wide): While Keshet is present, each hero with her treats the Discover Lore project related to a war or battle as one category cheaper. Projects seeking common lore become free (but still require a respite activity to complete).

Effect 2 (pre-encounter Reason test): If Keshet has 24 hours or more before a combat encounter or negotiation, and she has one or more clues or rumors regarding it, she can make a Reason test as a respite activity. She can make this test only once for any given encounter or negotiation.
Combat encounter results:
  • ≤ 11 — Director tells the number of creatures in the encounter.
  • 12–16 — Director tells the number and level of the creatures.
  • ≥ 17 — Director tells the tier 2 outcome information, and when the encounter begins, all enemies are surprised.
Negotiation results:
  • ≤ 11 — Director gives three motivations, one of which belongs to an NPC in the negotiation.
  • 12–16 — Director gives one motivation for an NPC in the negotiation.
  • ≥ 17 — Director gives tier 2 outcome information; Keshet and all allies gain an edge on tests made to influence NPCs during the negotiation.

Overwatch

Overwatch
Triggered Action · Ranged 10 · One creature · No Focus cost · Trigger: the target moves
"Under your direction, an ally waits for just the right moment to strike." At any time during the target's movement, one ally can make a free strike against them. Spend 1: if the target has R<[average], they are slowed (EoT).

IX. Complication — Self Taught

From the complication description (verbatim, including the typo in source): "While your peers were learning their trades in fancy schools, you whoned your capabilities on the mean streets with nothing but your own instinct as a guide. What you lost in polish and tactical acumen, you now make up for in raw power."

EffectMechanic (verbatim)
Self Taught At the start of each of your turns during combat, you can forgo gaining your Heroic Resource until the start of your next turn. If you do, your strikes gain a damage bonus equal to your highest characteristic score until the start of your next turn.
◈ Mechanical Note

This complication is mechanically benefit-only — there is no separate listed drawback. The trade is per-turn: forgo Focus gain → +2 damage on strikes that turn (Keshet's highest characteristic is 2). On any turn Keshet takes this trade, she is choosing personal damage over the team-economy that her Focus would otherwise fuel.

From Zenith.md: "The fallen heroes they couldn't save, the self-taught methods that work but lack polish, and the question of whether any plan can truly account for chaos."

The three threads correspond to specific features. The fallen heroes are the Honor the Fallen inciting incident. The self-taught methods are the Self Taught complication. The "question of whether any plan can truly account for chaos" is interpretive but anchored in Zenith.md's framing of Keshet's worldsickness posture as suppression-through-planning.
