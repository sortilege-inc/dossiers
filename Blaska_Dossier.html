I. Identity & Role

Blaska is an orc, raised wild and communal, taught crafts and the care of animals, eventually settled to a farm of her own. She gave up the warrior reputation orcs carry the way a person sets down a coat they never wanted. What she kept was the discipline — the slow, daily work of regulating the body, of pushing back against the noise of the world. That discipline is now her class: she is a Null, an unarmed psionic warrior who dampens magic and absorbs the supernatural. Where she goes, the impossible becomes harder.

Her tradition is Metakinetic — she has learned to see through the illusions of the universe to her own body's potential. Her power is not loud. It is the deliberate refusal of someone else's spell to land, the small space of stillness she carries around her, and — when she chooses — a hand placed precisely on the joint or the throat or the chest of someone making trouble.

In the Free City of Brass, Blaska is the most magical city in the timescape's least welcome guest. She doesn't want to be here. Someone made sure she was.

III. Discipline — The Heroic Resource

The Null's heroic resource is Discipline: the measure of focused, hard-won composure that lets her bend the supernatural away. Discipline builds at a steady tick, and accelerates whenever an enemy inside her Null Field tries to take meaningful action — the more they push against her quiet, the more she has to spend.

How Discipline Builds

TriggerGain
Start of your turn+2
First time each combat round that an enemy in the area of your Null Field uses a main action+1
First time each combat round that the Director uses an ability that costs Malice+1
◈ The Null's Logic

Discipline rewards Blaska for being where the trouble is. The more loudly the supernatural asserts itself within her aura, the more Discipline she gets to push back with. It is the opposite of avoidance — her job is to walk into the spell, not around it.

IV. Orc Traits

Orcs carry an anger that cannot be hidden and a fury that drives them in battle — reputations that the peace-loving orcs (Blaska among them) find distasteful. She has the racial baseline plus three trait selections, all of which fit a person who would rather build than break.

Two of three picks (Passionate Artisan and Grounded) are non-combat or defensive. Bloodfire Rush is reactive — she runs faster after being hit. Blaska is not the orc the books warn you about. She is built to stay put, weather the blow, and out-craft and out-last the people who keep dragging her into fights.

V. Null Class Features

The Null requires no weapons and no tools. Blaska's body and her aura are the kit.

Always Active — The Aura

Null Field
Maneuver · Area, Psionic · Aura 1 · All enemies

Each target reduces their potencies by 1. Once as a free maneuver on each of her turns, Blaska can spend 1 Discipline and give her Null Field one of the following additional effects until the start of her next turn:

  • Gravitic Disruption — The first time on a turn that a target takes damage, she can slide them up to 2 squares.
  • Inertial Anchor — Any target who starts their turn in the area can't shift.
  • Synaptic Break — Whenever she or any ally uses an ability against a target that has a potency effect, the potency is increased by 1.

The aura remains active even after an encounter ends. It ends only if she is dying or if she willingly ends it (no action required).

Always Available — The Damper

Inertial Shield
Triggered Action · Psionic · Self · Trigger: You take damage

Blaska halves the damage. Spend 1 Discipline: the potency of one effect associated with the damage is reduced by 1 for her.

Built In — Movement & Form

Psionic Martial ArtsWhenever Blaska uses the Knockback or Grab maneuver, she uses Intuition instead of Might for the power roll and for determining if she can target creatures larger than her. With Knockback, she can choose to slide the target instead of pushing them.

VI. Metakinetic Tradition

Of the three Null traditions, Blaska follows the Metakinetic: "You learn to see through the illusions of the universe to more fully understand your body and its psionic potential."

At level 1, the tradition grants an additional Exploration skill (Swim) and access to the Metakinetic Mastery table — a scaling table that pays out as her Discipline pool grows in size. At low echelons the relevant entries are:

Discipline ThresholdBenefit
2Whenever she uses the Knockback maneuver, the forced movement distance gains a bonus equal to her Intuition score (+2).
4The first time in a combat round that she takes damage or is force-moved, she gains 1 surge — even if she resists the effect.
6She gains an edge on the Grab and Knockback maneuvers.
◈ How This Reads in Play

The tradition rewards Blaska for putting hands on things — for grappling, knocking back, and being struck. Combined with Psionic Martial Arts (Intuition replaces Might), her unarmed control game scales sharply once Discipline starts flowing.

◈ The Loadout's Shape

Two signatures cover the two ranges of combat Blaska cares about: Faster than the Eye when two enemies are adjacent, Inertial Step when she needs to close or reposition. Psychic Pulse trades a maneuver for an area damage hit that also grows her Null Field for a turn. Arcane Disruptor is the anti-mage finisher — built specifically to punish creatures that try to spend Malice within her reach. The whole loadout assumes she will be in melee, in the thick of the spell, doing the unfashionable work of putting hands on the supernatural.

◈ A City-Free Perk in the City

Danger Sense works in natural environments specifically. In Alloy — the densest urban location in the timescape — Blaska does not get the perk. The mechanical inversion is the lived reality: she is at her most useless precisely where someone has chosen to put her.

X. Complication — Loner

From the Draw Steel complication: "You've always been a lone wolf. With no one else to lean on, you've picked up a million survival tricks. Which made it all the more surprising when you joined your current adventuring group and found the family you'd never known you needed."

AspectEffect
Loner BenefitWhen she finishes a respite, she chooses a skill she doesn't have. She has that skill until the end of her next respite.
Loner DrawbackNow that she finally has people who care about her, she won't let anyone take them away. Whenever a creature reduces one of her allies to 0 Stamina, she is taunted by that creature until her ally's Stamina is higher than 0, another creature makes her taunted, or the end of the encounter.
⚠ Tension Point

The complication assumes the lone wolf has already let people in. In Collateral, Blaska wakes up in a cell with four strangers — the complication frontloads its own emotional question: how long until they count? And what happens to her position on the field when they do? The drawback drives her toward whoever has just dropped an ally, often pulling her away from where she'd tactically prefer to be.

XI. Inciting Incident — Blight

From the Draw Steel inciting incident: "A horrible blight swept over your homeland, sickening the livestock and causing crops to rot. No one knows whether the blight is of natural origin or something more malevolent, but you set out in search of a way to cleanse the land of this affliction."

◈ How the Farm Was Lost

The campaign roster's hook — that Blaska "lost her farm" — runs through Blight. She did not lose it to a raid or a foreclosure but to something slower and more insidious: animals dying, fields rotting, no one with a clean answer for why. She left not because she gave up on the land but because she went looking for a way to cleanse it. Whether the blight is natural or "something more malevolent" is the open question the inciting incident hands to play.

◇ Tie-In to Collateral draft

A Null whose homeland is dying of a possibly-supernatural plague has obvious resonance with a campaign about a debt held by a creditor whose nature is unclear. The Saffron Seal naming her as collateral while she is mid-quest to find a cure is a configuration with at least two readings — the Seal wants her sidelined, or the Seal is the thing that needs the cure to fail. Either is available to the Director.

XII. Position in Collateral draft

◇ Provisional · Drawn from Campaign Concept

The following is drawn from the Collateral campaign summary, which is explicitly marked as draft/provisional. Details are subject to change in Session Zero.

The premise: the player characters wake in the same holding cell in Alloy, the Free City of Brass, and are told "The debt has defaulted. You belong to the Saffron Seal now." None of them know who named them as collateral. Blaska's place in this is the strangest, because she had stopped doing this work. She had a farm. She is here because someone reached across worlds to pull her here, and the question of who, and why her, is the campaign's central mystery.

Role on the Team

Combat: melee anchor and anti-mage. Blaska's Null Field reduces enemy potencies, and her abilities punish anything trying to spend Malice within reach. She is built to stand inside the spell.

Out of combat: practical hands, animal handler, crafter, plain-spoken read of people. The bookish, faceman, and rumor-hunter roles belong to others. Blaska is the one who can build, mend, ride, swim, climb, cook the food, and tell whether someone is lying without needing to explain how she knows.

Suspected Tension with the City

Alloy is described in the campaign summary as the most magical city in the timescape. A Null in Alloy is, mechanically and thematically, a counter-pressure to the place itself. Whether the Saffron Seal sees her as a threat, a tool, or a problem to be parked is unresolved.

XIII. Character Arc

Two mechanics pull against each other across the campaign:

  • The Null wants her to be alone in the center of the storm — discipline, composure, her body as a closed system that the supernatural can't enter.
  • The Loner complication tells her she has already found the family she didn't know she needed — and punishes her for losing them, mechanically taunting her toward whoever drops one of her people.

The character was a peaceful orc who became a disciplined warrior who became a quiet farmer. The campaign asks her to become something fourth: a person whose stillness has to make room for other people inside it. The Saffron Seal may break her cover. The strangers in her cell may not stay strangers.
