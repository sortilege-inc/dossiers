Linnea of Bree

Bound to Celenneth by the Aegis · Warden · Of Bree-Blood

Men of Bree Calling: Warden Patron: Radagast the Brown Safe Haven: The Angle Standard of Living: Common

A Bree-folk woman, long dark hair, grey-eyed. The granddaughter of a Bree herbalist who taught her that there wasn't a problem that couldn't be eased with a good cup of chamomile tea. The daughter of a man who was killed; held captive afterward by men who treated her as property; cut free by a Ranger of the North near the Anduin and not put down at the next village.

I. Identity & Standing

Linnea was raised in Bree by her grandmother, who taught her plants. Her father was killed; afterward she was held by bandits near the Anduin. Celenneth cut her free and was meant to drop her at the next village. She rode on instead. Across the books that followed she was named Linnea of Bree by Thalion in his pavilion; learned riding, swordwork, and archery from Eadwyn of Rohan; midwifery from Freowyn; took up the bow and rode Eorlin, the foal she watched born; bound herself to Celenneth by the Aegis at Yule; mothered Brynja with her; midwifed Hallas into the world. She has been admitted to the great halls and addressed by name there; she has also carried a solo book of the chronicle, alone with Gandalf and Bilbo and the Marish and a branded man and the road home.

The Hobbiton Dream

Whoever brands the men whose clasps are wolves spoke to her in dream: you carry what is ours, you will return or be consumed. The cursed dagger Celenneth carries from Fangorn appeared in the dream though Linnea had never touched it. Gandalf, hearing of it, named the principle: dark objects call out to each other.

VI. Chronicle of Her Road

Before the Chronicle Begins · Bree
A Bree childhood; the grandmother's chamomile.
Plants learned in the grandmother's kitchen. There wasn't a problem that couldn't be eased with a good cup of chamomile tea.
Pre-Book II
The father killed; taken by bandits.
Linnea's father was killed. She was held afterward by men who treated her as property. The road brought her, eventually, near the Anduin.
Book II · The Wagon
Cut free by Celenneth.
Celenneth circled the camp, cut the ropes, whispered stay quiet, and was gone before the bandits knew. She gave Linnea her cloak unprompted, and shared it in the cold.
Book II · Cermië 2965 · The Campfire
The wall came down.
I am more liable to regret inaction. The kiss across the firelight. Celenneth: I don't want to. But yes.
Book II · Radagast's Forest
The Brown Wizard's prophecy.
Your fates are intertwined, though the path may not be what either of you expects... there will come a time when you must choose between your duty and your heart. Linnea's response, after: he didn't exactly give us much to go on, did he.
Book III · Eorlas; Bain in Dale
Herblore begun; the orc-map laid before a king.
Eorlas the wandering herbalist met and invited to join. Athelas among his gifts. The orc-stolen map laid before Bain in his hall; Linnea asking rest before the audience and being respected. Dol Guldur named for the first time in a hall of state.
Book IV · The Woodland Realm
Thalion named her.
In his pavilion: Celenneth of the North, Linnea of Bree. The feast of Mirkwood with Eryndor and Arannis; the elven glade. Under stars, Celenneth's first I love you, breath against hair.
Book V · Rhovanion · The Silver Ring
A wandering spirit, near the Anduin.
The spirit pressed the silver ring into her hand. Carried close for four story-years, its nature unknown.
Book V · Rohan · Yule, the Binding, Brynja
The Aegis sworn; Brynja adopted; Eorlin foaled.
The Binding of Aegis: Linnea repeating the words back, voice trembling, gaze never leaving Celenneth's. Love. We could have offered her love, the night before. The mare's foal taken under Freowyn's hand. This is the best part of my day, in the kitchen after Eadwyn's lessons. Celenneth riding back hard: I missed you both.
Books V–VII · Rohan · The Apprenticeships
Riding, swordwork, archery; midwifery.
Under Eadwyn: I just want to be strong enough to protect the people I love. — That's exactly why people would follow you. Under Freowyn: a foal is no different from a soldier. The green cloak of Rohan accepted: I am honored. I will strive to be worthy of it. Eorlin grown and given. Celenneth declining her own cloak: to bear your cloak would be to claim a bond I cannot fully uphold.
Book VII · The Trollshaws Bath
The chain-scars given air.
Linnea tracing the scars: you're beautiful. You don't always have to be strong. Not with me.
Book VIII · Bree · The Family Dinner
The homecoming refused; the home named.
Miss Calder's scandals and wonders in the market. Merla, Harvin, Jocelyn at the dinner. Emily's anger: you could've written, you wanted this. Celenneth: she chose to survive. Merla: she belongs here, she could have a better life. Linnea: you don't get to choose. Loran in the frost: I should've tried harder. Stars guide you. Pelle and Mareth at the gate with the traveling blanket and the carved wooden horse. The hillside afterward: I'm scared. — You'll always belong, with me, with Brynja. Bitterroot found for Brynja's fever.
Book IX · The Solo Book
Sent alone to Bilbo; the Shire; the Marish.
Celenneth wanted to take the road in her stead; Gandalf refused. I'll be back soon. Always. The brigands on the road: strong men such as yourselves have no need to waylay weary riders. Silver pennies paid; Gandalf's quiet well played. The wolf's-head clasp first seen in a Hobbiton garden, later in the Green Dragon. Bilbo's tea at Bag End: the silver ring named: a memory ring, a longing that never settled. It chose you. Keep it close. The letter to Celenneth written before breakfast and not sent; tucked in her pack to deliver in person. Gandalf gone at dawn without waking her. The branded man with the wolf-charm: this isn't just a desperate bandit, this is someone trapped in something cruel. The Hobbiton nightmare: you carry what is ours. You will return or be consumed. The Marish; the Wyrmscale Sigil taken on instinct; the villagers warned on her way out. Orlen and Maren met. Home to Brynja and Celenneth: always.
Book X · The Frost Maw
At Celenneth's shoulder for the underground work.
The warg in the snow; the overhang Linnea spotted in the blizzard. Damrod and Hallas met. The binding-curse chant in the wraith chamber, her voice hesitating, growing. Your lore saved us, love. Flaming arrows giving the wraiths substance enough to be broken. Orlec's death, mid-warning; the Guardians at the altar; Brandor's last gift of the Warden Blade fragment. Brandor's chosen herbs the next morning. Buried in the frozen hills with the woodcarving of the old runes. In the depths, Hallas found the iron-bound chest with three relics: the Lorebook of Arnenir, the ward scroll, and the Frostward Key — not yet named.
Book XI · The Glade, the Watchtower, the Spring Festival
The chalice received; the spring festival; the parting.
The glade spirit: we've never shied from duty, or from wonder. The pendant at her throat. The chalice given by the glade, used at a Dúnedain holy spring and left: may it refresh their spirits as it did ours. The pregnancy reveal: I've missed my cycle. — This child will have love no matter whose blood runs in their veins. The important thing is that they are ours. Lothair the wandering scholar met on the road. Brynja's it smells like home again at homecoming.
Book XI · The Angle · Hallas Born
The birth Linnea midwifed.
Linnea's hand at Celenneth's back; the lower-back pressure learnt under Freowyn. Celenneth in the worst of it: I can't, Linnea, I can't, it's tearing me apart. The thin wavering cry. Our little Hallas. Welcome to our family.
Book XII · The Angle · The Grief Named
The wound she could not carry.
Pregnancy was Celenneth's; the body's transformation Linnea's to witness rather than to know. I feel like I'm on the outside of something I've always wanted. I just want to be enough. Celenneth's answer: you're more than enough, you're everything. The ache held without being solved. The Warden Blade reforged at Eadric's forge, with Niril his master's hand on the magic: you bear a blade with an old name, and one worth honouring.
Book XIII · Eryndil · The Sea
West aboard the Eärlindë.
Eryndil arrived in the Angle bearing Círdan's errand. The Barrow-downs passed; Eryndil and the bear on the road: we see you, we're merely passing through. Almedra and Berthor named the three relics from the Frost Maw chest: the Lorebook of Arnenir, the ward scroll, the Frostward Key. The lorebook was left with Almedra: its knowledge is safe, and that's what matters. What we carry now will serve us — and those who come after. The ward scroll and the Frostward Key continued west with the family. The Númenórean ruin; Brynja's pottery shards: treasure can be knowledge, too. The ambush; Celenneth's ribs taking a hit; the prisoner released; Eryndil's arrow at the next bend. Martha and Luthan's farmhouse; the brooch left on the mantel.
