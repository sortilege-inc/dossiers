I. Identity & Standing

Kaia kW is the vocalist and longest-tenured member of Homeschool Dropouts, a Night City hardcore band that has burned through fifteen-odd musicians in five years and shed at least five names before settling on this one. She is in her mid-twenties, trans, Pacific Islander, Māori-speaking by inheritance and street-slang fluent by necessity, born on a commandeered supercontainer ship that no longer exists. She lives in a stacked cargo container in the Old Combat Zone, owes nobody (anymore), and as of the back half of last quarter she has begun — quietly, against most of her own instincts — running with a crew of edgerunners called the Preem Chooms.

Physical Description

Mid-twenties, small, wiry, dense. Short curly hair, dark; nose piercings, plural; gang colors of the SNCZ (South Night City Zone) worn head-to-toe in the underground rocker idiom — bottoms, top, footwear, jewelry, even contact lenses tinted to the gang's palette. The signature laser-light street jacket flickers across her body when she moves, advertising her as a target and a sign at once. Her hands are calloused from instrument and amp-cable both. The voice, untreated, is rough; treated through her AudioVox it becomes a thing you cannot easily get out of your head.

Manner of Presence

She does not perform charm. She performs presence — the steady, refusing-to-be-moved-from-here weight of someone who has spent four years holding a band together while every other instrument changed hands around her. Off-stage she is moody, rash, headstrong; she hates almost everyone she meets on principle and revises that assessment slowly. On-stage she is a different animal entirely — the dance comes out, the AudioVox engages, the KillStrom amp finds the room's resonant frequency, and she will scream a crowd into a single shared heartbeat for forty-five minutes at a stretch.

IV. Interior Architecture

The structural fact about Kaia's interior is endurance under conditions designed to scatter her. Her father's people are scattered. Her mother is gone. Her brothers are gone — two presumably dead with the ship, one ghosted somewhere in Night City. The Reclaimer community she was raised inside no longer exists as a community. The band she joined has been disassembled and reassembled around her four or five times. The city she walked into eats people like her on principle.

She is still here. That is the architecture.

The Hardening

"Hates almost everyone" is not a default emotional state — it is a discipline. It is the wall she has put up since the Ryūjin went down, rebuilt after every band member walked, reinforced every time the city took someone. She is suspicious before she is anything else, and the people who get past the suspicion (Cain on the wrong end of a shotgun the morning he found her sleeping in the Dust Devil; Candy in the middle of a firefight that was nominally about Candy's past; McKracken whose floor she is currently crashing on) do so by virtue of being there when they did not have to be.

The Two Voices

There is the off-stage Kaia — small, watchful, conserving words. And there is the on-stage Kaia — the AudioVox engaged, the body unlocked into Dance 6, screaming a room of strangers into a single beating muscle. The second one is the truth; the first one is the way she lives the rest of the time so the second one is still available when she needs it. The stage is where she is most herself. The cargo container is where she rests.

What She Carries Forward

The Māori is her mother's. The trading-stall hustle is her father's. The Reclaimer ethic — salvage, rebuild, refuse to be erased — is the whole community's, and it is the through-line from a sinking sea-fortress to a stacked cargo container in the Combat Zone to a DIY pop-up concert that doubled as cover for a corporate hit. She has been reclaiming her own life from saltwater since she was a teenager. The instinct does not switch off because the water has changed.

What She Is Not

Not a sellout. The lifepath has a clear shape for what that would look like — Marcus "Crash" Chen, the original vocalist who took a corp media deal, is the cautionary tale she carries in her bones. The line between getting off the street and being bought off the street is the central moral question of her arc, and she has not yet had to walk the harder side of it. Jessica Donuts's birthday booking is the first time the question has had a real face.

Not safe. EMP 6/7 is the start of a slope, not the bottom of one. The throat implant she is shopping for will take another point. Then the next thing will. The rate at which she keeps her Humanity will determine the rate at which she remains the person her brother could recognise if he ever surfaced.

V. Family Before the Sinking

The Arasaka Ryūjin was a supercontainer ship commandeered and turned into a megacomplex and trading hub in the Pacific, hosting refugees and displaced people from across the region. The Vailea family — Rangi, Keani, and four children — arrived early in the ship's reconfiguration. They ran a modestly successful trading stall for most of Kaia's childhood. The Ryūjin was eventually caught in the crossfire of corporate interests and sunk. Kaia escaped with one brother. Two younger brothers and both parents did not surface.

Hardcore punk with metallic edges. Screamed and shouted vocals, processed through her AudioVox into something both more bearable and more dangerous. Lyrics about displacement, corporate violence, Pacific Islander identity in a city that pretends she is invisible. The stage show is built around the KillStrom Sonic Boom Amp — an actual sonic weapon that, when patched into her rig, can force a crowd to make Resist Torture/Drugs checks. IX. What She Carries Hidden

Kaia is not a liar — she does not have the patience for sustained deceit — but she is private by long practice. A partial inventory of what the crew, the band, the scene, and Night City do not yet know about her:

  • That she was Kai before she was Kaia, AMAB, and the transition happened somewhere between the Ryūjin and Night City — the chronology compressed by the chaos of survival. Her gang colors and her stage persona were forged in parallel with the rest. The dossier is the first place this is written down.
  • That she has three brothers, not "a brother." Two of them did not get off the ship. She does not name them out loud.
  • That Mikey is in Night City and has stopped responding. The crew assume her family is "scattered to the winds" in the lifepath-vague sense. The specific fact — that one brother is alive and within reach and not answering — she has kept to herself.
  • That she actually likes McKracken. She would not phrase it that way. She is sleeping on his floor and has not yet looked for somewhere else to be.
  • That she paid Lou the 550 eb in full this period, which closes a debt she had been carrying since before the Preem Chooms existed. She has not announced it. It matters to her.
  • That the Reputation 3 "Maelstrom choom" tag worries her. Boomer and Dozer are not friends, and the kind of street rep that earns the Maelstrom tag is not the kind she wants reaching the band.
  • That she has not yet decided what to say to Jessica Donuts about the birthday gig. The money would matter. The implication would matter more. She is waiting to see Jessica in person before she decides.
  • That she counts the days since Mikey's last reply. She does not show anyone the count. She has it.

XI. Operational Environment

Kaia's working geography is a narrow set of overlapping circuits in Night City — the Combat Zone where she lives and where the Biotechnica job happened, Old Japantown where the Lily Pond and Mort's are, the underground venues of the SNCZ, the corporate downtown she only enters on jobs, and McKracken's apartment which is where she sleeps now.

The Combat Zone

Where her cargo-container apartment sits. Where the Biotechnica lab was, before they took it down. Where the broken van and the Maelstrom encounter happened. Where the building collapse buried her. She knows it well enough — Local Expert (Home) 4 — to navigate the alleys and the venues and the way back from a bad scene, and badly enough that any given night here can still kill her.

Old Japantown

The Lily Pond. Mort's. The kind of small bars and venues where a band the size of Homeschool Dropouts plays without a contract and gets paid in drinks if at all. Where she met Candy Unicorn and where Mikey, last she heard, lives somewhere.

The Blackout Bar · Lou's place

The bar where Lou runs sound. Where she went to try to reconnect after the Combat Zone job and was rebuffed. Where she paid him off. The relationship survives; the welcome is provisional.

Downtown · The Delirium · the corporate world

Where the meet with Mr. Purvis happened. Where Jessica Donuts presumably lives and parties. The world Kaia does not enter in gang colors unless she is being paid to. The world that will, eventually, decide whether her band gets to exist on its own terms or not.

The Wider Threats

  • The Red Chrome Legion — stormed the Lily Pond the night she met Candy. The retaliation was nominally about Candy. Kaia killed at least one of them. Whether they remember her face is an open question.
  • Biotechnica — paid the crew. Denied the sniper. Both can be true. Both can be false.
  • The Scarecrows and the Tin Men — the gang that ran the Emerald City lab. Most of the principals on site died in the basement or under the collapse. Whoever runs the franchise above them has a reason to ask who did it.
  • Maelstrom — not enemies, not friends. The street-rep tag may be worse than no tag at all in certain rooms.
  • Netwatch — tangentially — the censorship hit on Candy's music video drew their eye to the scene Kaia plays in. Kira is on the other side of it. The lines blur.
  • The critic / influencer — rumour-level. Someone in the scene who wants to break or co-opt the underground. Not yet a named opponent on the file.

XII. Possible Trajectories

The sheet is rich but unfinished — 215 IP unspent, three empty cyberware slot containers, a brother somewhere in the city, a corporate gig pending, a band stable for the first time in years. What follows is not prediction but a sketch of the lanes the character can grow into, given her sheet and her present situation.

The Quiet Threads

  • The throat implant & the cyber path. The next mechanical inflection point. The cost in Humanity is real (she is 60/66 now) and the cost in money is high. Whether she stops at the throat or keeps going — Tech Hair, internal weapons, a second cyberaudio piece — will define the next stretch of her sheet.
  • Demolitions 1 → 2 → 3. The skill she took on the Biotechnica job and used to bury herself. Whether she leans into it or treats the Combat Zone collapse as the lesson it was is a player choice with a real arc behind it.
  • The band stable. The current Homeschool Dropouts lineup has held for two years. Every previous lineup eventually broke. The arc of this band — does it record, does it tour, does it survive a real test — is the slow thread under everything.

The Loud Threads

  • The Jessica Donuts gig. The first concrete sellout-or-not decision. A corporate-adjacent patron offering a substantial fee. Kaia's life goal is get off the street, no matter what — the no matter what is the test. The session that resolves this booking is one of the major beats of her arc.
  • Mikey. He is in Night City. He has stopped answering. The campaign can leave him as an absence or it can produce him — alive, dead, addicted, corp-owned, or simply tired. Each option rewrites a different part of her interior.
  • The Red Chrome Legion remembers. Or doesn't. If they do, the Lily Pond night comes back — with weight.
  • Biotechnica's denial. The sniper had a reason. The denial is on the record. The truth is not. If the sniper traces back to Purvis — or to whoever Purvis answers to — the Preem Chooms have a job to settle and Kaia is going to be in the middle of it.

The Tested Edges

  • The KillStrom used as a weapon proper. She has it. She has not yet had to point it at a room as a weapon and pull the trigger. The first time will not be theatre.
  • The day "hates almost everyone" stops being a discipline and starts being a problem. She is letting people in — Cain, McKracken, Candy, Lennox, the band. The discipline that kept her alive may not be the disposition that lets her keep what she has built. The campaign will press.
  • The day someone offers her the Marcus "Crash" Chen deal. Not Jessica's birthday. The real one. The genuine corporate offer to take Homeschool Dropouts as a product and grind the band down into something marketable. She knows what her answer would be. The cost of that answer is the question.
  • The day she sees Mikey. Whatever state he is in. The interior reorganisation that follows will not be optional.

What She Will Not Be

She will not be a smooth-talking face-of-the-scene Rockerboy. INT 3 and Conversation 2 do not support it. Her instrument is intensity, not articulation; her medium is the body and the amp, not the interview.

She will not be a corporate signing. Not in any sense that recognises the words she would still call her own.

She will not survive every member of the crew. Night City does not work that way. The question is which losses she absorbs and which ones reorganise her, and what the version of her that comes out the other side of the next Talia-shaped loss looks like.

She is still here. That has been true for a long time now. The campaign is going to ask, repeatedly, what it costs.
