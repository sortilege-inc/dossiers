Class Features
Umbral Corruption Class
As Kelun accumulates scars, they are slowly consumed by the Umbra. When they deal damage, they deal extra damage equal to their number of scars. When they mark their last Hope slot with a scar, they do not retire — instead they succumb to the Umbra's influence and charge forever into the dark to join the terrors that stalk lightless spaces. For a Clank that was already a vessel for something vast, this possibility is not abstract.
Volatile Magic Hope Feature
Spend 3 Hope to reroll any number of your damage dice on an attack. The magic that runs through Kelun is not trained — it surges. When it surges correctly, the results are significant. When it surges otherwise, they try again.
Arcane Sense Sorcerer
Kelun can sense the presence of magical people and objects within Close range. They do not need to concentrate or roll — the awareness is constant, ambient, indistinguishable from the ordinary texture of their perception. They were a vessel for divine speech. The residue of that makes everything magical faintly legible to them, the way the smell of smoke is legible to someone who spent years near a fire.
Minor Illusion Sorcerer
Make a Spellcast Roll (DC 10). On a success, Kelun creates a minor visual illusion no larger than themselves within Close range. The illusion is convincing to anyone at Close range or farther — it has depth, shadow, appropriate texture. It does not move unless Kelun concentrates. It makes no sound. It is, they have found, most useful for creating the impression that something is not there.
Channel Raw Power Primal Origin
Once per long rest, Kelun can place a domain card from their loadout into their vault and choose one effect:
  • Surge: Gain Hope equal to the card's level — raw power converted to possibility
  • Enhance: Add a bonus equal to twice the card's level to a damage roll on a spell — the discharged magic pouring directly into the strike
The magic in Kelun does not come from study. It comes from accumulation — decades of divine residue, five years of Ygva's incantations, and something older than both that has no name yet.

Character

Appearance

Kelun is a Clank — one of the stone-and-metal figures the gods once used as voices, now walking under their own direction. Their surface is weathered pale grey, the texture of old limestone, with seams that occasionally catch light in ways that suggest something interior. They are roughly human-shaped, broad in the torso, seated or standing with the unhurried solidity of something that has no particular relationship with urgency. They wear wrappings of dark cloth over their frame, practical against the cold. Their face, if you can call it that, is a smooth ovoid. Expressions happen — you learn to read them in the angle of the head, the set of the shoulders.

Manner

Unhurried. Not slow — unhurried. There is a difference. They process at a pace that suggests whatever they are considering has been given its full weight. When they speak it is rarely at length, but it lands — there is no wasted word, and the Presence score of +1 is the result of a thing that has existed long enough to understand that attention is its own form of speech. They are, in Ygva's experience, the most reliable creature she has ever known. This is not a small thing to be.

The Sorcery

No one taught Kelun to work magic. Ygva recited incantations during the long dark of the Griefcleft years — to keep her own mind ordered, to practice Oshel's forms, to fill the silence. Kelun listened. At some point, somewhere in the fourth year, they began to replicate. Ygva noticed it the third time a fire responded to Kelun's proximity rather than her own. She did not say anything immediately. When she did, she said: keep going. She has never quite decided if what she feels about this is pride or fear. It has not stopped her from going forward.

The Orb

The whispering orb came from the upper Griefcleft, found in a crevasse on the descent from the mountain. It whispers in a language Kelun partially recognizes — it is not Kurethik, not any mortal tongue, but it has the cadence of something the gods once used. Kelun has not translated it. They are not certain translation is the right approach. They listen to it the way you listen to weather.

History

Kelun was reactivated in Ulmira's Step decades before Ygva was born — the circumstances of their original purpose and the nature of their deactivation are not recorded anywhere accessible, which is itself a form of information. They were present in the settlement as a kind of fixture: known, trusted in the way of something that has always been there, unclear in the way of something no one has thought to ask the right questions about.

Ygva found them early. The child with the flat eyes and the too-careful way of watching things — she asked Kelun questions that other children didn't ask, and Kelun answered with the same deliberate honesty they brought to everything. The friendship that formed was not explained by either of them and was not questioned much by anyone else. When Ygva was given to Renavir's lessons, Kelun attended in the loose sense of being present nearby. When Oshel came and pointed at Ygva, Kelun came too. No one asked Kelun if they wanted to. They did not feel the omission as an injury.

Five years on the Griefcleft. The witch's lessons. Ygva's incantations filling the dark. Oshel fading into the Umbra. The descent. And somewhere in the middle of all of it, Kelun began to work magic — not the learned kind, not the studied kind, but something that grew in the space where decades of divine residue and five years of close contact with a witch-in-training had left conditions suitable for something to grow. They came down the mountain changed. They are still determining in what direction.
