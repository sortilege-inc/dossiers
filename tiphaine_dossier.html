I. Identity & Station

Tiphaine merc'h Riwall is a newly-made squire about to join the eschille of the Wolves of Vagon, in the western marches of Salisbury. She is fourteen years old, of dual Breton-Roman parentage, devoutly Roman Christian, and the orphaned daughter of Sir Riwall ap Hoel of the Three Oaks, who fell at the Battle of Cambridge last year. Her placement among the Wolves was arranged by her uncle in Armorica through correspondence with the late Earl of Salisbury, who made the assignments of squires to knights before his own death at the Night of the Long Knives. The specific Wolf to whom she will be squired has not yet been revealed.

Physical Description

She is small for her age — petite, finely-boned, with long flaxen hair gone wavy at the temples and plaited loose over one shoulder, grey eyes set wide and watchful, and a fine scatter of freckles across the bridge of her nose. Her mouth rarely settles into a smile. Her bearing is self-contained, almost preternaturally still; she does not waste motion. She wears practical wool and leather: a dark blue cloak (her father's), a clean linen shift, a small silver cross on a leather thong at her throat, her father's hunting knife at her belt, and a heavy leather gauntlet for her hawk.

Manner of Presence

She speaks rarely and listens often. Her Cymric is fluent but unmistakably foreign — vowels rounded where they should be flat — and people are always telling her it is charming, which she finds irritating. She has the courtly graces her mother trained into her; whether she will deploy any of them voluntarily among the Wolves remains to be seen.

Two observations on the sheet

Honour 19 (Famous). Her Honour is the defining mechanical fact about her: at Famous, it is known and is itself an instrument of play. Combined with Modest 15, her honour will not present as priggishness.

Literacy (Latin) 9. In 464 A.D. Britain, most knights cannot read, and most squires cannot read. Reading and writing in Latin is a churchman's skill. Tiphaine has it, by virtue of her mother's project completed before her death.

III. Interior Architecture

The structural fact of Tiphaine's interior is a tension between formation and defences.

The Formation

Her mother's project, completed before her death: a devoutly Christian, courtly-trained, Latin-literate young woman. The religious virtues took. All six Christian virtues (Chaste, Forgiving, Merciful, Modest, Spiritual, Temperate) are dominant on her trait sheet, and her Modest 15 is her single strongest trait. She is, at the level of her formation, the girl her mother prayed she would be.

The Defences

Selfish 12, Arbitrary 12, Suspicious 13 are dominant. She watches the world warily. She keeps what is hers. She has her own sense of fairness, not always patient with rules she finds unjust.

The Tension

Both registers are real and both are dominant. She is devout and watchful, Forgiving and Suspicious, Modest and Selfish. Which side surfaces in any given scene is a question for play.

What She Is Not

Not bent toward revenge. Her father was killed at Cambridge last year. Her trait of Forgiving (13) is dominant over Vengeful (7). Hate Saxons (14) is a passion — a hot feeling she can be triggered into — but it sits against her deeper traits of Forgiving and Merciful.

Where Her Face Feels Like Her Own

In the forest. She does not weep where anyone can see her. (From the player's character introduction.)

IV. Family & Household

Sir Riwall ap Hoel — father, deceased last year at CambridgeBreton
A Breton knight of Armorican descent. Came to Logres as a young man and won his small manor from the previous Earl of Salisbury through good service in the field. He kept hawks and taught his daughter to ride, to track, to shoot a bow, and to fly her goshawk Argantia. Died at the Battle of Cambridge alongside his Earl, in the rising against Vortigern's tyranny. Tiphaine wears his hunting knife at her belt.
Lady Marina — mother, deceased five years agoRomano-British
Of a Romano-British family, devoutly Roman Christian, and tirelessly committed to making her daughter the polished young lady she required. From her, Tiphaine has her Latin — both spoken and written, an exceptional gift her mother completed teaching before she died of a fever — her saints, her prayers, her courtly graces, and her habit of careful hands and clean nails.
Donatien — younger brother, age sixFostered with his mother's family
Heir to the Three Oaks, such as it remains. Currently fostered with their mother's family. Tiphaine has not seen him since their father's death. She loves him beyond measure — Love Family 18, near-Exalted, the second-strongest passion in her soul, just below her honour. He is too young to write.
Lord Konan — paternal uncleArmorica
Her father's elder brother, still in Armorica. Arranged her squireship by correspondence with the late Earl of Salisbury, a formality he completed at distance.
Her mother's family — maternal kinRomano-British, fading lineage
A Romano-British family. Christian, Latin-speaking. They have Donatien. Whether and how they enter Tiphaine's life again is a question for the campaign.
Argantia — goshawkTrained by Tiphaine herself
Her hawk. Female goshawk, named with a Breton word meaning silver-bright. A gift from her father, trained under his eye and Ronan's. She rides on Tiphaine's gauntleted left fist when they travel, hooded.

A small manor in the western marches of Salisbury, named for the three ancient oaks at the entrance to the home wood. Burned by the Saxons in the raids of last summer. Whether anything of it can be restored is unknown.

The specific Wolf to whom Tiphaine is to be squired has not yet been revealed; that revelation will set her Homage (Lord) passion.

VI. At the Threshold

Tiphaine has not yet been played. What follows is not narrative claim about her behaviour — she has none yet — but observations about the situation she is walking into, and the lanes the sheet supports.

What the Wolves Will Encounter

A small foreign girl with a charming accent, a hawk on her fist, and a courtly bearing she may or may not deploy. She does not yet know any of them; they do not yet know her. The Wolf to whom she is squired has not yet been revealed when she arrives.

What the Sheet Supports

  • Honour as engine. At Famous, her honour will quickly become legible to the people around her, whatever her personal reserve.
  • Latin literacy as resource. The first time a letter, a roll, a deed, or a charter needs reading, she can do it. Whether and when she reveals this is a choice.
  • Bow and hawk as practical contribution. The lean winter and ongoing need for game make her hunting capabilities directly useful to the eschille.
  • The courtly skill cluster as a held weapon. Courtesy, Flirting, Orate, Fashion, First Aid are all at 9. She can deploy any of them.
  • The Forgiving / Hate Saxons tension will surface when a Saxon enters the picture in a position that invites either cruelty or mercy.

Open Questions for Play

  • Which Wolf is her knight? (Sets her Homage / Fealty passion, currently 0.)
  • Does the Three Oaks remain something she — or her brother — can return to?
  • How does her formation hold (or not) under the pressures of a tyrannous reign and a hungry winter?
  • What does she do the first time she has to choose between her trait of Forgiving and her passion of Hate?
  • Does the campaign's Red Dragon dream come to her as well as the knights?

A Note for the Gamemaster

Resist playing her as a grief-driven avenger; her trait sheet does not support it. Resist treating her courtly skills as decoration; they are real. Resist underestimating Honour 19; it is the loudest number on her sheet. She is fourteen, untested, and the right kindness from the right Wolf at the right moment will affect her more than she will let show.
