Grey skin, red eyes, white hair — unmistakably drow at any pub on the front lines. In the cities of the heartland, where the war is rumour and the propaganda is caricature, she passes for a particularly strange wood elf. No one has thought to look closer.

VIII. Gameplay Events

Arrival in the Bar (Session 12)

Senna entered the campaign as a stranger in a bar already in chaos — cultists dead, the place fogged over, patrons being stabilised, the barkeep's assistant caught picking pockets and confessing to a 200gp offer for a glass orb. She presented herself directly to Lokar, asking him to help stop the crusade against the drow that his people were being drawn into. The party was skeptical. They didn't know what a drow looked like; she could have been any number of strange elves.

The Crater & the Plane of Ice (Sessions 12–13)

The party went on to the experiment site to destroy the dark orb. The crater was warm in the snow and trees grew around it. A drill was driven in; lightning flashed and froze a horse solid; the orb cracked open into a tunnel onto the Plane of Ice, with a distant castle and circling dragons. Senna's role in this sequence is not detailed in the available notes; the heavy lifting on the orb was done by Ursimi (with the Ring of the Ram) and Dmitri, who was momentarily replaced by an ice clone after looking into the heart.

Casting Off from the Docks

At sunrise, with smoke in the direction of the harbour, the party scrambled aboard Captain Famir's ship. Senna mistimed the jump and missed the boat; Ursimi (transformed into an octopus) caught her and pulled her aboard. The escape ran through a ship jam, past the Butterfly Knights demanding port-closure inspections, and through the Teeth of the Kraken — where the party's pursuer did not follow.

Counsel on the Voyage

Senna recommended the party seek a priest of Ackhar the Severe before approaching Deathbeggar's Isles, the haunted Drow tombs whose ghosts could occupy a living body. She advocated raising the dead in advance — not to harm them but to deny the ghosts a vessel. Ursimi was unconvinced and remained suspicious of her motives. The party noted: Senna is probably a necromancer.

Senna's actual recommendation is mechanically straightforward. Circle of Spores druids prepare Animate Dead, Gentle Repose, and Blindness/Deafness automatically. She was telling them what her kit is good for. They heard necromancer. — reading between the lines

The Sea of 10,000 Stars

The party crossed the Sea of 10,000 Stars, where first-time travellers can catch a star and pull it down. Senna jumped first and hit water; the starscape recovered around her. Ursimi, polymorphed into a bird, succeeded where she had not, and brought up a single star worth 400gp.

Mythathlenor & Kensu

In the white-towered city of Mythathlenor, the party found Cheshire cultists arguing internally and got a lead on the cleric Kensu Zendak. At the temple of Artur, Kensu raced Ursimi (and lost), but contributed his winnings to the orphans-and-war-heroes fund regardless. He joined the expedition to Deathbeggar's Isle.

Deathbeggar's Isle

Kensu broke the barrier around the isle with a thrown skull. Senna transformed into a bird, carried a rope up the cliff, and secured the party's climb. Inside was an ancient Drow temple to Lolth — an extremely old object of worship. A skeleton with a cup asked for an offering; Senna stepped forward first, after the blood offering, and walked deeper into the tomb. She was the first to discover the giant spider-thing in the next room.

The Tomb's Inner Chambers

The party fought a twelve-foot skeleton crawling with spiders, casting Hold Person and pleading “I am not a mean face” as it died. Inside the tomb: a dead drow in gold-thick halfplate; a spider-pile with a flute, robes, skull mask, and a belt-pouch; an inscription on the flute — “blessed mother i've led my heart astray in moment of weakness, clear my heart of foolheartiness, remove my self-inflicted wounds, that i might spread your glory and influence with others.”

The Top of the Tomb

At the top of the stairs, a smooth wooden door with the word “don't” carved in a Drow-like font. Beyond it: a vast chamber draped in purple satin, a giant spider crushed against the 25-foot ceiling with eight unchained chains around its legs, and an elven woman's face between its eyes — drooling, eyes closed. Robed figures wearing the same warding symbol from the floor below were feeding it. Ursimi, looking at Lolth, felt something tighten around her mind. The chains-not-attached are the signature of the Chainpusher, one of the three great evils, bondage without bonds. The party voted to leave and not speak of it.

Senna's reaction inside the upper chamber of the Lolth temple is not recorded in the available notes. Whether she recognised what she was looking at, whether she contributed to the vote to leave, and whether she carried anything away from it — open. — gap in the record

Ordgate (Senna offscreen)

Senna does not appear in the Ordgate sessions described in the available notes — those follow Lokar to the fighting pits in pursuit of his father's sword. Her status during these scenes is not stated in the notes provided.

IX. The Two Truths She Carries

The Cover — What She Tells Them

She is a soldier whose company was crushed in a battle she'd rather not speak of. She fought against drow — she has the bond and the bitterness to sell it. She is helping Lokar because the propaganda war is dragging his half-orc people into a crusade they have no business in, and the front is a meat-grinder. Plain enough; uncontroversial enough. She lets them think she is what they expect.

The Truth — What She Doesn't

She is drow. She is behind enemy lines — very far behind — sabotaging the war effort from the inside. The reason no one has clocked her is that the war is fought at the edges of the nation and the heartland has only ever known the caricature. She does not match the caricature, so she is “a strange elf,” and that has been enough.

Her interest in Lokar is real, but not for the reason she's given. Lokar's uncle is marching the tribe to the front lines. If Lokar takes back the tribe, the tribe doesn't go. If the tribe doesn't go, the front lines have that many fewer soldiers to kill her people. She is not helping the party. She is removing a future battalion. The party has never figured this out.

From the back leaf of a small leather field-book, Druidic script

The young one still tells himself the story of his father's sword as if the sword were the thing. Let him. The story keeps him pointed south, away from his uncle — and every day he is pointed away is a day the tribe is not yet at the line.

He is not a stupid man. He may guess, in time, what I am doing for him. I would prefer he does not. A favour seen is a favour owed, and I have no use for his debt. I want the tribe off the road. That is all.

The others think me a necromancer. Let them. It is a useful misunderstanding. A drow who raises the dead is what they were ready to find; a drow who walks among them and means them no harm is not. I will be the first thing for a while longer.

If they ever ask me plainly — and the ranger nearly has — I will lie, plainly. I have practiced.

— S.
