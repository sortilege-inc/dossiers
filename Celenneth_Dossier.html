Celenneth of the Dúnedain

Ranger of the North · Messenger of Gandalf the Grey · Bearer of Nurhael's Warden Blade

Rangers of the North Calling: Messenger Patron: Gandalf the Grey Safe Haven: The Angle Standard of Living: Common

A Dúnedain woman of the Northern line, auburn-haired and green-eyed, helmed in the Númenórean fashion. Beneath the leather and the cloak, the mithril mail-shirt given her by Balin in Moria. At her hip, the Warden Blade of Nurhael — reforged by Eadric of the smiths, with the help of his Dúnedain master Niril, from the fragment Brandor pressed into her hand at the altar beneath the Frost Maw.

I. Identity & Standing

Celenneth is a Ranger of the North, of the line of Arnor, raised by Brandor and Orlec after the loss of her family — her mother killed by goblins, her sister taken from a wood when Celenneth was nine. She has carried Gandalf the Grey's confidence since Bree at the start of her road, and her Calling is the Messenger's. The Angle is her safe haven; with Linnea, Brynja, and the infant Hallas, it is also her home.

Bearing

Auburn-haired, green-eyed, of the Dúnedain register: composed, sparing of speech, slow to declaration and faster to act. Brandor's and Orlec's pedagogy was the example given without the language of declaration, and she carries it forward; she has learned, across the years of the chronicle, to receive love without armour, but she has not learned to perform it.

IV. Fellowship & Bonds

Linnea — wife, bound by the AegisBree-folk
Cut free from a bandit's wagon at the start of Book II; rode east at Celenneth's side from that day. Named Linnea of Bree by Thalion in the Woodland pavilion. The Binding of Aegis was sworn between them in Rohan at Yule. Her Fellowship focus.
Brynja — elder daughter, adoptedFoundling, Riddermark
Infant found in the Riddermark, near the Anduin. Eofred said Rohan shall be her father; Celenneth adopted her the morning after the sister was named. Tracked under the Heir mechanic; XP accumulating toward her succession.
Hallas — son, infantDúnedain, born in the Angle
Born in the Angle, Linnea midwifing from Freowyn's lessons in Rohan. Named for Hallas the ranger. Our little Hallas. Welcome to our family.
Gandalf the Grey — PatronIstari
Came to her in Bree at the end of Book I with the sealed parchment and the line about her being a child when they last met. Reappeared on the road in Book IX, speaking to Linnea of stories of young Celenneth studying old stones and runes.
Lord Elrond — summoner; healer of RivendellRivendell
Received the brazen head of Dero from Balin via Celenneth. Sent the healing draught she carried for years and unwrapped for Linnea's broken arm on the plains. Sent the missive of Súlimë 3, 2969 that opened the Book VIII pivot.
Belegon — elven scout of RivendellRivendell
The bow-bearing elf who met her on the threshold of Rivendell at the end of her first journey south of Moria, and led her into the valley.
Brandor & Orlec — foster fathersRangers of the North
The two rangers who found her and raised her after her family was lost. Reached at last in the Frost Maw in Book X. Orlec died mid-warning, a daughter, such hope, I'm proud of you. Brandor lived long enough to pass her Nurhael's Warden Blade with its fate is yours now, and to send her forward: Go. I can hold on. But don't tarry. In the morning Brandor chose his herbs — I can't leave Orlec alone in this cold earth — and Celenneth buried them in the frozen hills, hands clasped, weapons crossed, a woodcarving of the old blade's runes set with them.
Damrod & Hallas — ranger allies on the eastern lineRangers of the North
Met in the snowfields above the Frost Maw, arrived as equals with their own mission. We'll help you root out whatever curses these lands, you help us into the Frost Maw. Hallas recognised Celenneth as Brandor and Orlec's protégé — the detail that made the alliance real. After the Frost Maw, the watchtower, and the spring festival, they rode east tracking what was stirring in the deep places.
Eryndil — summoner of LindonElf of Lindon
Arrived in the Angle with patience rather than urgency, bearing Círdan's errand and the Adamant Relic. On the road west, an unresolved disagreement: Celenneth chose to release a prisoner; Eryndil chose differently, around the next bend.
Eorlas — herbalist of MirkwoodMirkwood
The wandering herbalist with staff raised who commanded a shadow-figure begone. Named the iron eye pendant as carrying the strength of its maker, shrouded in shadow. Gave her the elixir she still carries.
Balin — Lord of the brief reclamationDwarves of Moria
Gave her the mithril mail-shirt, entrusted her with the brazen head of Dero for Rivendell, substituted mithril for gold from the hoard. News of his death reached her at Rivendell.
Eadric & Niril — smith and his Dúnedain masterEriador
Eadric reforged Nurhael's Warden Blade with the help of Niril, his Dúnedain master, in whose hands the magic of ancient blades was kept. Celenneth gave Eadric her old keen sword in gift-exchange: this one belongs to my past. The Warden Blade is for my future.

VI. Chronicle of Her Watch

Before the Chronicle Begins
Family lost; raised by Brandor and Orlec.
Mother killed by goblins. Sister, aged seven, taken from a wood when Celenneth was nine; Celenneth ran. Brandor and Orlec found her in the woods. Gandalf met her once as a child, studying old stones and runes.
Book I · Sarn Ford · The Cave
The crown taken; the wraith released; the curse received.
Took the crown from the mummified corpse, nailing it into the skull as punishment rather than honour. Released Lady Elwin's wraith into peace. The cave's curse fastened to her as the Wandering Madness. Returned to Bree to find Orlen already gone.
Book I · Bree · Gandalf
Made messenger.
Gandalf called her by name, gave her the sealed parchment with its faintly shimmering symbols. He doesn't command her; he trusts her. She sharpened her sword and checked her provisions.
Book I · Moria
Through the mountain with Balin's company.
Equipped with the mithril mail-shirt by Balin; entrusted with the brazen head of Dero for Rivendell. Uftak the trophy-collector, with his lantern at the Cave of Lights. Lif's death from the wound Uftak gave him; the lay sung in his honour. Imri the dwarf-blacksmith persuaded to join. Malech faced after. Gorgol son of Bolg named for the first time. Cermië 3, 2965 — the gates of Moria shut behind her.
Book II · Rivendell · Belegon
Delivered the brazen head; Nimroch received; Orlen's letter answered.
Met at the threshold by Belegon. Delivered Balin's gift; received Elrond's audience. The healing draught entered her pack. Orlen's letter reached her, dated the same day as her own. The iron eye pendant was found in the undergrowth the same arc.
Book II · The Road South · Linnea
Cut a Bree-woman free from a bandit's wagon.
Was meant to drop her at the next village. The road did not let it happen. By a campfire in Cermië: I am more liable to regret inaction. Her answer, looking at her hands: I don't want to. But yes.
Book III · Mirkwood, Dale, the Lonely Mountain
First successful alliance work; Bain heard them.
The orc-stolen map laid before Bain. Dol Guldur. Bain's expression tightening at the name. The inn after; ordinary stew, ordinary rest. We're stoking a fire that can dismiss the shadow.
Book IV · The Woodland Realm
Thalion's alliance sealed; Linnea named.
The pavilion. Celenneth of the North, Linnea of Bree. Eryndor's harp; Arannis at the table. The alliance with Dale granted. You will find safe passage through our lands.
Book V · Rohan · Yule
The Binding of Aegis; the sister named; Brynja adopted.
Yule with the Rohirrim. The Binding of Aegis: I swear to stand as your shield, to face the darkness at your side, and to guard your life with my own. Days later, by firelight, the sister finally given a shape — seven, brave, foolish, taken — and Celenneth nine and running. The next morning before Eofred: Rohan shall be her father. Brynja became theirs.
Book VI · Súlimë 9, 2966 · Minas Tirith
The Steward heard her; did not grant.
Steward Turgon in the great hall under the White Tree. Considered it shall be. The Easterlings, the corsairs of Umbar. A week in Minas Tirith working the city quietly. The Entwash patrol thread overheard in an inn; orcs camped where they should not have been. She rode for Edoras with information instead of an alliance.
Book VI · Capture · Fangorn
Taken north; escaped; broken leg in Fangorn.
Taken by orcs under a master's will. Six nights in captivity; Snaga, Uglat, Morga, Grusk worked against each other by patient suggestion. You've had days to break me, to kill me, but here I am. One orc dead, a belt knife in hand, the camp behind her. Into Fangorn; the leg broken on a root and set herself with a belt and a branch. The rusted dagger half-buried in the earth, runes she almost recognised. A dream of Dol Guldur's gates.
Book VII · Garin · The Trollshaws
The honesty held.
Met Garin on the open plains. Told Linnea before, and after. Hold me while you tell me everything. The chain-scars finally given air at the Trollshaws.
Book VIII · Súlimë 3, 2969 · Elrond's Missive
Rivendell summons; Bree confronted; the home named.
The grey-cloaked elven messenger; the cylindrical case of dark wood with silver filigree; this missive comes from Lord Elrond, it is for your eyes alone. News of Balin's death received at Rivendell. Linnea, watching her among the elves: there will be times we'll have to let you go. Brynja's fever broken with Linnea's bitterroot. Brynja's leg set by the same hands that set Celenneth's own in Fangorn.
Book IX · The Angle · Gandalf on the Road
Home chosen; Gandalf met again.
Linnea: you've spent so much time helping me reconnect with my past. I want to do that for you. Gandalf on the road, telling Linnea stories Celenneth had never shared. The Angle accepted them. Brynja's tracking lesson in the woods — the rabbit, the deer-tracks like this big, the quiver of feathers and smooth stones.
Book X · The Frost Maw · Brandor and Orlec
Found and buried the two rangers who had raised her.
The warg in the snow, the mithril shirt taking the mortal wound. Linnea spotted the overhang in the blizzard. Damrod and Hallas met in the snowfields. The runes restored from ranger archives; something awakened, or at least stirred, in the deep places beneath their feet. The frost hounds in the cramped tunnel; the wraiths in the chamber, bound by the binding-curse chanted from old memory — your lore saved us, love. Orlec dying mid-warning: a daughter, such hope, I'm proud of you. The Guardians at the altar; Celenneth wounded in the side. Brandor, alive barely, passing the Warden Blade fragment: its fate is yours now. Finish what we began. Go. I can hold on. But don't tarry. The altar sealed; peace returning to old bones of the earth. In the morning, Brandor's chosen herbs and his calm announcement: I can't leave Orlec alone in this cold earth. Buried together in the frozen hills, hands clasped, weapons crossed, a woodcarving of the runes set with them.
Book XI · The Watchtower · The Glade · Homecoming
The shadow wolves; the spring festival; the parting; the chalice; home.
The hidden compartment at the watchtower — the coded ranger's journal, the map fragment, the silver clasp with the watchful eye. The cultists' canyon ambush. The spring festival's garland-crowning and ring-toss stall. The chalice received at the dream-glade and left at a Dúnedain holy spring — may it refresh their spirits as it did ours. The parting with Damrod and Hallas: the kind of understanding that needed no words. Brynja's you smell like horses at homecoming; it smells like home again as she fell asleep. Hallas the son born in the Angle, Linnea midwifing from Freowyn's lessons. Our little Hallas. Welcome to our family.
Book XII · The Angle · The Warden Blade Reforged
The pregnancy named; the blade made whole; the grief tended.
Eadric at the forge, recognising the fragment as Nurhael's Warden Blade. Niril sent for, his Dúnedain mentor in the magic of ancient blades. Celenneth's old keen sword given to Eadric in gift-exchange: this one belongs to my past. The Warden Blade is for my future — for my family's future. Linnea's grief named plainly: I just want to be enough. Held, not solved. Brynja's hand on the moving belly.
Book XIII · Eryndil · The Sea
West toward the warded ruin.
Eryndil's arrival in the Angle — sea-grey eyes, shell-beads, a blue cloak. Almedra met; Berthor's notebook received. The Eärlindë readied at Mithlond under Círdan. Celenneth's recitation of Númenórean lineage accepted: in you, I glimpse a spark of Númenor's old light. On the road west, an unresolved disagreement: a prisoner Celenneth released; Eryndil's arrow at the next bend.
