Class Features
Umbral Corruption Class
As Tamros accumulates scars, he is slowly consumed by the Umbra. When he deals damage, he deals extra damage equal to his number of scars. When he marks his last Hope slot with a scar, he does not retire — instead he succumbs to the Umbra's influence and charges forever into the dark to join the terrors that stalk lightless spaces.
Rogue's Dodge Hope Feature
Spend 3 Hope to gain a +2 bonus to Evasion (raising it to 14) until the next time an adversary attacks him. He is very good at not being where the blow lands.
Cloaked Nightwalker
Any time Tamros would be Hidden, he is instead Cloaked — a stronger condition. While Cloaked: all rolls against him have disadvantage. If he is stationary when an adversary moves to where they would normally see him, he remains unseen. He loses the Cloaked condition only after making an attack or ending a move within line of sight of an adversary.
Sneak Attack Nightwalker
When Tamros succeeds on an attack while Cloaked, or while an ally is within Melee range of the target, he adds a number of d6s equal to his tier to his damage roll.

Shadow Stepper Nightwalker
Tamros can move from shadow to shadow. When he moves into an area of darkness or a shadow cast by another creature or object, he can mark a Stress to disappear from his current position and reappear inside another shadow within Far range. When he reappears, he is Cloaked.

Character

Appearance

Tamros is Ribbet — broad-shouldered and low to the ground, his slate-grey skin mottled in the way of all Ridgeborne Ribbets who have lived in weather. His eyes are wide, dark red at the iris, and rarely blink at the expected moment. He is not large. He moves as though he has already decided where each surface will bear his weight, each shadow admit him. He carries a spear across his back when travelling. He rarely uses it.

Manner

Calm. Not the calm of someone who has found peace — the calm of someone who returned from somewhere that re-sorted his priorities so completely that most things which alarm other people simply don't register anymore. He is attentive, genuinely curious, unhurried in a way that can read as unsettling. He smiles easily. He does not explain himself.

What He Found

He went into a fissure in the titan's flank on a dare, at fifteen, and came back at seventeen saying only that he'd found something inside and that it had taken some time to leave. He was not distressed. He was not hungry. He was calm in a way he had not been before, and he has been calm ever since. No one has been able to get a full accounting out of him. He doesn't refuse the question — he answers it, every time, with something slightly different and equally incomplete. Vaetra has filed this under unverifiable. Ygva has filed it under debt outstanding. Neither category captures it.

On Debts

He swore to repay Ygva for pulling him out of the ravine when they were twelve. He has not forgotten this. He has not disclosed exactly what form repayment will take, and he has not yet decided if the form matters — or if simply staying close is enough. He is not sure it is.

History

Tamros was born in Ulmira's Step to a Ribbet family of the Ridgeborne — stone-cutters and quarry-workers, people who understood the mountain as labor rather than mystery. He grew up among the childhood circle: Ygva, Vaetra, Sarru, Kelun, Mellan. He was the smallest of them and the quickest, and he used both without apology.

At twelve, he slipped into a ravine on the lower Griefcleft and would have died there if Ygva had not come back for him. He was at the bottom for most of a day. He came out with a debt he has never made light of and a quiet terror of confined spaces that he has since, somehow, resolved into its opposite.

At fifteen, on a dare that he accepted without hesitation and has never blamed anyone for, he entered a fissure in the titan's side — a crack in the deep stone that the settlement's elders called the listening place and advised people away from. He was inside for two years. He returned at seventeen, muddy and unhurried, with no satisfactory account of the interval. The settlement's elders examined him and found nothing wrong. They have not stopped watching him since.

He returned to the circle, trained quietly in the arts the darkness had made legible to him, and has been present in Ulmira's Step in the particular way of someone who could leave at any moment and has simply chosen not to yet. When Ygva came back down from the mountain and Vaetra asked where he'd been, he said: around. This was not a lie.
