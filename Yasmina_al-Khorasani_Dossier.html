On the Subject

Yasmina was born into a family of caravan-guides on the western edge of the Kara-Kum. Her grandfather had been a Zoroastrian fire-keeper before the family's quiet conversion two generations back; her father kept the trade alive by leading merchants across the sand to Merv and back, and by the time Yasmina could ride she could read dunes the way other children read faces.

The desert taught her early that it lies. Heat-shimmer above the sand looks like water; a distant copse of trees that resolves, on approach, into a pile of bones; a caravan track that fades into nothing because the wind has rewritten it overnight. She learned to tell a true thing from a false one — by smell, by light, by the way her own pulse changed when something was wrong.

She learned, also, that some of the desert's lies were not natural. Once, when she was nineteen and guiding a small spice-caravan toward Merv, she watched an oasis appear ahead of them — palm trees, a clear pool, the smoke of a cook-fire — and felt, in her chest, that it was not there. She held the caravan back. The merchants cursed her. She made them wait until the sun moved, and the oasis went with it, and beneath where it had been there was only sand and the half-buried bones of an older caravan that had not been so careful. After that the older guides took her aside and taught her things they did not teach everyone. There were techniques, they said. There were ways of seeing. There were people on the Silk Road who manipulated mirages, and people who could unmake them, and her family was one of the latter.

She has been working the Khorasan routes for eight years now. She knows the wells. She knows the stones. She knows, sometimes before she sees them, the things that walk between the planes and try to pass themselves off as oases.

Class Features

Ranger — Base
Favored Enemy · Fiends
Level 1

Advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track fiends, and on Intelligence checks to recall information about them. Yasmina also learns one language they speak: Infernal.

Choice rationale: the Mirage Seeker's later "Touching the Fabric of Reality" feature references creatures "not native to this plane"; in the Silk Road setting, malevolent djinn and lesser planar intruders are most naturally mapped to the Fiend type.

Natural Explorer · Desert
Level 1 · Arid terrain

While traveling for an hour or more in desert terrain, Yasmina and her party gain:

  • Difficult terrain doesn't slow group travel.
  • Cannot become lost except by magical means.
  • Remains alert to danger even while foraging, navigating, or tracking.
  • If traveling alone, can move stealthily at a normal pace.
  • Finds twice as much food when foraging.
  • While tracking other creatures, also learns their exact number, sizes, and how long ago they passed.
Fighting Style · Archery
Level 2

+2 to attack rolls with ranged weapons. Stacks with Sharpshooter at long range to make her longbow effective at distances most archers would call madness.

Spellcasting
Level 2 · Wis-based

Wisdom is her spellcasting ability. Spell save DC 12 · Spell attack +4. Spells known: 3. Spell slots: three of 1st level.

  • Hunter's Mark — concentration bonus action; +1d6 damage on attacks vs. target. Signature.
  • Cure Wounds — touch, 1d8 + 2; reliable field heal.
  • Detect Magic — concentration, 10 minutes, 30-ft. radius; synergy with her illusion-detection role.
Primeval Awareness
Level 3 · Action · expend a slot

Spend one ranger spell slot. For 1 minute per slot level, sense whether aberrations, celestials, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, or undead are present within 1 mile (or 6 miles in Natural Explorer terrain — desert). Reveals presence only, not location or number.

Mirage Seeker — Empires of the Silk Road
The Mirage Adept
Level 3 · Subclass core

Yasmina learns minor illusion as a ranger cantrip. She also gains free subclass spells by ranger level (always known, no count against spells known):

  • Minor Illusion
  • Silent Image L3

When she uses Primeval Awareness, she also gains a 30-ft. aura that detects illusion spells within range for the next 10 minutes. Up to two creatures she chooses (Wis mod) — herself included — gain advantage on the next Wisdom saving throw against any spell made within those 10 minutes while within her aura. The aura also pinpoints natural phenomena within Primeval Awareness range — oases, water sources, hidden wells.

The Mirage Power
Level 3 · Once per turn per target

When Yasmina hits a creature with a weapon, she deals extra psychic damage. A given target can take this damage only once per turn.

Mirage Power 1d4 psychic, on hit — scales to 1d6 at Ranger L11

Combined with Hunter's Mark and Sharpshooter: a marked target takes 1d8 (longbow) + 4 (Dex) + 1d6 (mark) + 1d4 (mirage) = avg ~14 damage per hit, before the −5/+10 toggle.

Spell Rebound · Universe Note
Historica Arcanum · Mirage Seeker grant

When Yasmina casts a spell from the illusion school, her first illusion spell of the day does not count against the campaign's Spell Rebound Table.

Feat

Sharpshooter
Level 1 · Granted feat (no ASI applied)

Years of guiding caravans across open desert have taught her to read wind, distance, and target with an uncommon eye. Her longbow strikes at impossible ranges, and where lesser archers' arrows would skip off a ridge, hers find the chest.

  • Attacking at long range does not impose disadvantage on her ranged weapon attacks.
  • Her ranged weapon attacks ignore half cover and three-quarters cover.
  • Before a ranged attack with a proficient weapon, she may take −5 to hit for +10 damage on a hit.

Background — Khorasan Guide

Knowing the Way

If Yasmina has ever visited a place in the Material Plane or seen it on a map, she can find it without getting lost, except when hindered by magical means.

Specialty · Caravan-Guide of Three Generations

Her family has worked the desert routes between the western Persianate cities and Merv for three generations. She knows the wells, the stone-piled cairns, the names of every caravanserai keeper between Tabriz and Mary, and which checkpoints take which bribes.

Your Journey

Lifepath · rolled and chosen entries from the Silk Road book

Birthplace
A mud-brick compound in a caravan-town on the western edge of the Kara-Kum. Her family has lived there for at least three generations.
Family
Both mother and father alive. An intact guide-family household — her father still works the routes; her mother runs the household and the small date-grove they own.
Siblings
Three. Two older brothers (both caravan-guides; one has gone into trade) and one younger sister.
Lifestyle
Modest. A working guide-family — they eat, they have a roof, they own their date-grove. The desert pays in survival, not in coin.
Strings to Arcanum
The village holds the supernatural at arm's length — djinn-stories told to keep children near the well at night, charms against the desert wind, half-remembered Zoroastrian rites her grandfather kept. Most of it is superstition. Some of it is true. The older guides know which is which.
Past Education
She did not attend a madrasa. Her father sheltered a wandering Turkish scholar for the winter of her fifteenth year, and the scholar taught her enough Turkish to read trade-letters and enough astronomy and cartography to use a navigator's astrolabe. Grants the Turkish language.
Event I · Arcane · d20 = 4 → d8 = 6

Found a magical item, used it, lost it.

At nineteen, leading her first solo caravan toward Merv, she found a small silvered compass-disk half-buried where a mirage-oasis had been. It pointed not to north, but to truth — toward whichever direction held the realest thing within a mile. She used it for two seasons. She lost it in a sandstorm outside Mary the spring she turned twenty-one. She has been looking for it on every route since, and has never told anyone what it actually did.

Event II · d20 = 15

Spent time at inns playing games — gained a game set proficiency.

Caravan stopovers at Merv and Nishapur involve long evenings in caravanserai courtyards. She plays Nard (the medieval ancestor of backgammon) well enough to keep her purse fat between commissions.

Event III · d20 = 18

Always felt followed throughout her life.

Since she lost the compass-disk, something has been on her trail. Maybe the previous owner. Maybe whatever made the false oasis in the first place. Maybe nothing — she has felt the pull of paranoia on long desert crossings before, and knows it can lie. But the feeling has not gone away, and it has been six years.

Event IV · Battle · d20 = 9 → d10 = 6

Escaped from a battlefield. Some say fled.

Two years ago, the caravan she was guiding was caught in a raider-skirmish on the road south of Merv. She got the merchants and their goods to safety by riding hard down a side-trail she knew, while the hired guards held the road. The guards died. The merchants paid her well. One of the guards' younger brothers, who survived, has been telling a different version of the story to anyone who'll listen in the Merv caravanserai. She has not gone back to Merv since. She is overdue.

Profession

Silk Road PEX system · Apprentice rank

Smuggler
Apprentice · Rank 1 PEX 0

Apprentice baseline (Cha-floor): When making an ability check using Charisma or a related skill, she cannot roll below 7. At her Cha 13 / +1 mod, this protects her social rolls from low dice — a Cha check has a floor of 8.

Yasmina is not a smuggler in the criminal-archetype sense — she does not move contraband for a living. But a Khorasan-Guide who hires out to a caravan is also frequently the person who knows which checkpoint to detour around, which border-officer takes which bribe, and which oasis-village will hide a wagon for a price. Her Apprentice rank reflects skills she has watched, not yet practiced. Natural first PEX purchases when she spends: City Secrets, Right Tools for the Job (Disguise + Forgery Kits).

Personality

Trait

"The desert tells you what it wants you to see. The trick is to ask what it does not want you to see, and to look for that instead."

Ideal — Truth · Neutral Good

"There is enough that wants to deceive us in this world. I will not add my voice to it. I will not see what I am told to see."

Bond

"I lost something six years ago that showed me the realest thing within a mile. I do not know where it went. I do not know who is looking for me because of it. I will find both answers before I die."

Flaw

"I trust my eyes more than I trust any other guide's. Sometimes my eyes have been wrong."

Connections

  • Khalid ibn Sinan al-Alamuti — They have crossed paths three times in two years, at three different caravanserais between Tabriz and Merv. The first time Yasmina noticed him because he was the only other guest in the courtyard who refused the wine and watched the doorways instead of the dancers. The second time he noticed her because she was the only other guest reading a small folded paper she did not want anyone to see. The third time they nodded across a fire and did not speak. They have a wary, unspoken professional respect — both work in margins where seeing-clearly matters. Neither yet knows the other's order. Both suspect.
  • Aftab Singh the Arbiter — They have not met. They share an inheritance neither knows the other carries: the feeling, every few weeks now, of being watched. Yasmina has been carrying it for six years and has assumed all that time it was tied to the lost compass-disk. If she meets Aftab and learns he has carried the same feeling, in the same texture, for roughly the same span of years, she will have to ask whether the feeling has one source or two. She does not know which answer she would rather have.
