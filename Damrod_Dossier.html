I. Identity & Standing

Damrod is a Ranger of the North, paired with Hallas. He and Hallas arrived in the snowfields above the Frost Maw at the moment of Celenneth and Linnea's blizzard, not as rescuers but as equals with their own mission. The bargain struck was clean: we'll help you root out whatever curses these lands, you help us into the Frost Maw. He fought beside the family through the descent, the wraith chamber, the Guardians at the altar, the burial of Brandor and Orlec; through the watchtower, the cultists' canyon ambush, and the spring festival; and rode east at the parting, carrying the watchtower's tokens toward whatever country called the corruption.

V. Chronicle of His Watch

Book X · The Snowfields above the Frost Maw
Arrived with Hallas at the right moment.
Damrod and Hallas arrived not as rescuers but as equals with their own mission. The bargain spoken plainly: we'll help you root out whatever curses these lands, you help us into the Frost Maw. Hallas, not Damrod, recognised Celenneth as Brandor and Orlec's protégé — the detail that made the alliance feel real.
Book X · The Surface Runes
Helped restore the wards.
Celenneth worked from ranger archives she had studied years ago; Hallas supplied corrections from older knowledge; the four of them stabilised the corruption's surface expression. Something awakened, or at least stirred, in the deep places beneath their feet.
Book X · The Frost Hounds, the Wraith Chamber
Joined the binding curse.
In the cramped tunnel against the bound hounds. In the wraith chamber the curse spread voice by voice around the chamber, each person adding weight to it — Linnea's joining last, hesitant then growing stronger. Tea in battered cups afterward; four rangers who had been strangers that morning.
Book X · The Guardians at the Altar
Damrod and Celenneth hammered at the gaps.
Linnea's flaming arrows became the decisive weapon, opening gaps that Celenneth and Damrod struck through before the ice resealed. Celenneth was wounded in the side; Damrod's shield arm was punished repeatedly.
Book X · The Burial in the Frozen Hills
Brandor and Orlec laid down.
Buried together with hands clasped, weapons crossed over them, and the woodcarving of the old runes set among them.
Book XI · The Lodge by the Fire
The toast; the question asked; the rare warmth given.
Linnea's toast: to surviving what we shouldn't have. Damrod's response carried rare warmth. Hallas's unfair, isn't it opened the door teasingly. Damrod asked Celenneth: what about Linnea? — the condition of trust. While Celenneth and Linnea were elsewhere, Hallas, seeing them find that sort of closeness — it makes you wonder what it might be like to have that kind of connection. Damrod, you're not the only one who wonders. A brief look exchanged; sitting close enough to feel the other's presence.
Book XI · The Watchtower
The hidden compartment.
The coded ranger's journal, the map fragment with symbols along the Frost Maw's borders, the silver clasp bearing a watchful eye. The chronicle records that the clasp unsettled Damrod for reasons he could not name. Between two who had both been shaped by old things: the past never seems to stay buried, does it? — No, it doesn't. But sometimes that's not the worst thing.
Book XI · The Spring Festival
Garland-crowned; the ring-toss stall.
The four of them mistaken for newlyweds by the harvest crowd, garland-crowned and toasted, accepting wooden rings and ceremonial mead. Damrod's resignation: as you say... for the harvest. Linnea led him to the ring-toss stall; ranger's precision applied to a carnival game; the carved prize handed silently to her with a smile.
Book XI · The Canyon Ambush
Cultists, on the road south.
The party fought through cultists in the canyon ambush on the road south from the Frost Maw.
Book XI · The Parting
East and south.
No long farewell. Damrod's rare softness in the embrace with Celenneth. Hallas's forearm clasp — warrior to warrior — and then the embrace. The kind of understanding that needed no words. Damrod and Hallas riding east with the journal, the map fragment, and the silver clasp. Their figures soon swallowed by the morning's shifting light.
