I. Identity & Role

Operator Azimuth is Zenith's most dangerous member in terms of raw capability. She handles direct action on the perimeter alongside Keshet — Keshet calls the play, Azimuth executes it. She does not carry a visible weapon. She is the weapon. She is a Talent of the Telekinesis subclass, and everything she affects is mediated through the psionic extension of her mind into physical space — forces applied at range, objects repositioned without contact, enemies lifted and discarded.

She is also the member of Zenith with the most turbulent biography. Mercenary life before the Office, then religious service at an institution, then a near-death experience that surgically removed her memory of having worked there at all. She did not lose her identity. She lost one specific chapter, and nobody explained why.

Her relationship to uncertainty is the most comfortable in the unit. Where Vellpryte refuses the unknown, Keshet plans around it, and Nomos narrates it into order, Azimuth simply absorbs it. Her entire operational life has been structures collapsing and things working out anyway. The Lucky complication is both mechanic and philosophy: she has learned to trust outcomes that a more cautious Memonek would find deeply uncomfortable.

The plan is a hypothesis. The engagement is the test. I am comfortable with either outcome. // Azimuth, pre-mission briefing transcript, Case #ZEN-001

Cultural Background

Culture: Mercenary Band — Nomadic, Bureaucratic, Martial. Before the Office, she operated among soldiers for hire: chaotic, transactional, violent work. This gave her something most Memoneks lack — a foundational comfort with uncertainty. She has seen plans fail catastrophically and watched competent people adapt and survive. She has internalized this as a worldview.

Career: Disciple. She worked in a church, temple, or other religious institution as part of the clergy. She studied religion, timescape cosmology, and psionics. She accrued 240 project points — substantial investment in long-form research. And then a near-death experience erased the entire chapter from her accessible memory. The institution remains. Her time there does not.

III. Clarity — The Heroic Resource

Azimuth's Heroic Resource is Clarity — psionic bandwidth, the fuel of telekinetic force. Unlike Drama (which accrues through crisis) or Wrath (which accrues through combat pressure), Clarity is a metered utility: gained steadily, spent on capability, with a throttle that distinguishes her from every other member of Zenith.

She can spend Clarity she does not have. She can drive it negative. She can overclock.

  • 1d3 — at the start of your turn (baseline generation)
  • +1 — the first time each combat round that any creature is force moved
STRAIN: You can spend Clarity you don't have, pushing it negative to a maximum of −(1 + Reason score) = −3. At the end of each turn with negative Clarity, you take 1 damage per negative point — unavoidable. This is called being strained. Some abilities have additional effects if used while strained. Cascading Strain (higher level) would allow her to redirect that pain outward.
◈ The Throttle

The overclock mechanic is the most important thing to understand about Azimuth's tactical role. She can spend past her resource floor to deliver output beyond standard parameters — at a cost paid in her own body. In a unit where every other resource system is clean and orderly, Azimuth's is the only one that can deliberately hurt her. Keshet calls the play. Azimuth decides how much to spend to execute it.

V. Key Abilities — Telekinesis Suite

All abilities use Reason (+2) for force output or Presence (+2) for higher-order control. She has no kit, no weapon. Every mechanical action she takes is an exercise of her mind on the world.

Free Strike / Range Anchor

Mind Spike
Main Action (also free ranged strike) · Psionic, Ranged 10 · Telepathy · No Clarity cost
A telepathic bolt that zaps a creature's brain. Reason-based power roll. This is her free strike — the action she takes between larger expenditures, the baseline that proves she is never not doing something. The fact that her free strike is a mental assault rather than a physical attack is a design statement: she does not punch people. She hits them with her mind and doesn't break eye contact.

Subclass Signature

Minor Telekinesis
Maneuver · Psionic, Ranged 10 · No Clarity cost
Wisps of psychic energy move a target — self or one size-1 creature or object — up to Reason squares (2 squares at current level). Spend 2 Clarity to increase target size by 1; spend 3 to vertical slide. The most visible expression of her subclass: objects and people relocated without contact, as a maneuver, for free. She is constantly repositioning the battlefield in the margins of other actions.

Reactive Defense

Repel
Triggered Action · Psionic, Ranged 10 · Trigger: target takes damage or is force moved · No Clarity cost
When an ally takes damage or is force moved, Azimuth halves the damage or reduces the forced movement by her Reason score. If the force move is reduced to 0, the source gets pushed back instead. This is the Telekinesis subclass's defensive anchor. She intercepts force applied to her allies and redirects or absorbs it. This is what the perimeter pairing with Keshet enables: Keshet positions, Azimuth protects.

Area Denial

Gravitic Burst
Main Action · Psionic, Area, Burst 1 · Telekinesis · 5 Clarity cost
Everyone get away from me. Reason power roll against each enemy in burst. Tier 1: 3 damage, vertical push 2. Tier 2: 6 damage, vertical push 4. Tier 3: 9 damage, vertical push 6. A clearing action — when the perimeter collapses and something gets too close, she detonates a field and everything in range goes away. The vertical component means enemies can be sent upward, not just back.

Single-Target Control

Levity and Gravity
Main Action · Psionic, Ranged 10, Strike · Telekinesis · 5 Clarity cost
She raises the target into the air, then smothers them against the ground. Reason power roll. Tier 1: 6+R damage, Might resistance or prone. Tier 2/3: escalating damage and knock-prone. A targeted takedown for anything that needs to be removed from play fast. This is Keshet calling a priority target and Azimuth answering. The target goes up, then comes down hard.

Mobility Support (Moderate Cost)

Levitation Field
Maneuver · Psionic, Area, Burst 3 · 3 Clarity cost (or 8 for 1 hour)
She extends her telekinetic field outward, letting allies fly. Each ally in burst 3 gains flight until start of her next turn and can immediately shift up to their speed. She shifts too. Stability drops to 0 for duration. The 8-Clarity extended version grants an hour of flight — requiring overclock if she does not have the resources. One of the most significant tactical options in the unit's kit: vertical repositioning for everyone.

Perception Perk

Thingspeaker
Perk — Supernatural · 1 minute of contact · Career: Disciple
When she holds an object for one uninterrupted minute, she can sense whether it bears emotional resonance. If it does, she learns the most dominant emotion associated with it — the emotional signature of whoever last cared about this thing. For an investigative unit handling contractual disputes, this ability is a quiet intelligence asset. Evidence has feelings. She reads them.

VI. Psionic Augmentation & Ward

Every Talent selects a Psionic Augmentation and a Talent Ward through meditation. These can be changed during a respite. Azimuth's current selections are Force Augmentation and Repulsive Ward.

Force Augmentation

Her psionic pathways are tuned for output. She gains +1 damage on all Psionic abilities. No defensive or utility component — she did not take Battle Augmentation (armor/weapons), Density Augmentation (bonus Stamina), Distance Augmentation (extended range), or Speed Augmentation (faster movement). She took more force. When in doubt: more force.

◈ Augmentation Note

Force Augmentation is the highest-damage-density choice and the lowest-versatility choice. It is a statement of operational philosophy: she is not trying to be harder to kill or harder to hit. She is trying to ensure that whatever she hits doesn't need to be hit again.

Repulsive Ward

When an attacker damages her, she can push them up to her Reason score (2 squares) away. A reactive spatial assertion: someone hits her, they move. There is no opt-in. It simply happens. This is the ward Azimuth's character demanded — it's not armor, it's not evasion, it's the telekinetic equivalent of a reflex she has developed over years of being the person who gets hit first.

◈ Ward Note

Repulsive Ward pairs with her operational role. She is on the perimeter, close to threats. When something gets through — as things do — she doesn't absorb it passively. She puts it at a distance.

◈ Religion & Timescape Knowledge — The Gap

Azimuth knows Religion and Timescape at the lore level — skills accrued during her time as a Disciple. VIII. Complication — Lucky

Azimuth has always had a lucky streak. When she leaves things in the hands of fate, she succeeds more than she fails. But luck is fickle — when she doesn't trust it, it deserts her.

AspectMechanicSignificance
Lucky Benefit When she spends a hero token to succeed on a saving throw or reroll a test, she rolls a d10. On 6+, she gains the benefit without spending the token. Hero tokens are rare. This mechanic means she can cash one in and potentially get it back. Luck as resource conservation — the universe occasionally refunds her bets.
Lucky Drawback Whenever she rolls tier 1 on a test and does not spend a hero token to reroll, she takes a bane on her next test. If she accepts a failure without contesting it, fate punishes her reluctance. The mechanic enforces engagement: she cannot simply accept bad outcomes passively. She must either spend to fight it or pay the cost of acquiescence.
◈ Lucky as Philosophy

This complication is not just mechanical. It is a statement about how Azimuth moves through the world. She has spent her career in environments where outcomes were not guaranteed, and she has come to trust something that isn't quite skill and isn't quite instinct. Her teammates — especially Keshet — find this uncomfortable. You cannot plan around luck. You cannot account for it in a contingency tree. Azimuth would say that's the point.

IX. Worldsickness — Her Method

Azimuth's strategy is absorption. She takes the hit so the structure holds. Where Vellpryte refuses emotion, Keshet suppresses it through planning, and Nomos weaponizes it through performance, Azimuth simply lets it pass through her — the way she lets physical force pass through her ward and be redirected outward.

This is either the most resilient coping strategy in Zenith or the one that will produce the most sudden catastrophic failure. She has spent her life absorbing disruptions and trusting things to work out. At some point, the worldsickness will deliver an emotional impact she cannot absorb — something that does not redirect, that stays. Her arc asks what happens when luck runs out and she finally has to reckon with what she has accumulated.

X. The Excised Memory

Azimuth's inciting incident is a near-death experience that removed from her accessible memory all record of having worked at a religious institution. This is not amnesia in the organic sense. It is surgical. The professional knowledge remains — Religion, Timescape, Psionics. The 240 project points remain. The emotional texture of the experience, the names and faces, the details of what she did there and what was done to her: gone. Someone, or something, decided she should not remember.

The Lucky complication may or may not be related. Her unusually reliable relationship with favorable outcomes began, as near as she can determine, around the same time as the gap in her memory. She cannot establish causation. She has not stopped noticing the coincidence.

XI. Position Within Zenith

Azimuth forms Zenith's perimeter team with Keshet. Keshet calls the play, Azimuth executes it. In the unit hierarchy, she is the throttle — the member whose capability can exceed standard parameters when the mission requires it, at personal cost. She is the element most willing to absorb a bad situation and most equipped to process it without transmitting it upward to the unit's decision-making layer.

◈ The Perimeter Partnership

Keshet and Azimuth operate in a handler-operator pairing. Keshet provides tactical architecture and calls priority targets. Azimuth provides the force projection to execute those calls, the reactive defense to protect the perimeter, and the overclock option when standard parameters are insufficient. Keshet trusts the plan; Azimuth trusts the outcome. They disagree on which of these is actually safer.

XII. Character Arc

The Central Question

The excised memory, the institution that took something from her, and a lucky streak that might be something else entirely. // Arc summary, Zenith.md

Azimuth's arc is structured around a missing chapter and the question of whether she wants it back. The knowledge remains; the context is gone. She functions. She is, arguably, the unit's most operationally stable member. The arc asks: is that stability genuine, or is it what's left after something essential was removed?

The Lucky streak is the most pressing narrative question. If it is a residue of psionic conditioning from the institution — a modification made to her without her consent — then it is a violation of her autonomy that she has been benefiting from without knowing it. If it is simply who she is, the complication resolves differently. Either way, the answer changes how she understands herself.

XIII. Relations

Executor Vellpryte — Senior Field Agent Active
Commanding officer. Azimuth executes Vellpryte's rulings without editorializing — the most formally compliant member of the unit in terms of chain of command. She watches Vellpryte's emotional refusal strategy with something that might be concern. She has been where the absorption breaks. She suspects Vellpryte has not yet found that edge, and will.
Protocol Keshet — Field Coordinator Active
Operational partner; the person who calls the play she executes. Their combined Keeper of Order gives the unit two probability normalizations per round — they impose order on dice as naturally as they impose order on scenes. Keshet cannot account for luck in their tactical models. Azimuth does not explain it. They have reached a functional truce on this point.
Secretary Nomos — Communications & Support Active
Mutual respect, genuine difference. Nomos turns everything into material; Azimuth leaves things unremarked. She appreciates that he evaluates her on results — he noticed the procurement negotiation and filed it accurately rather than theorizing about it. He is the most likely member of the unit to notice something is wrong with her before she does.
The Institution — Former Employer / Origin of Gap Unknown
Name unknown. Nature unknown. What they did to her: unclear. What she did for them: unclear. The residue is her skill set, her project points, and possibly her luck. She carries what they gave her and does not know who gave it. This is the most significant open thread in her biography and the one she is least equipped to investigate without help.
The Mercenary Band — Culture of Origin Antecedent
The foundation of her comfort with uncertainty. She spent her formative operational years in chaotic, transactional, violent work, and came out of it with a fundamental comfort with structures collapsing and things working out anyway. She does not talk about the band specifically. She carries their epistemology — outcomes over process, results over elegance — as her default operating mode.
