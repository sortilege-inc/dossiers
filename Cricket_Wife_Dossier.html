Specter — Species Traits

Cricket Wife is a spirit who chose not to pass on. Her body is provisional. Most of the time she walks the world as the dead sometimes do — convincingly human, save for the small giveaways: a long pause between breaths; feet that do not quite touch ground; a faint trail of pale flame in a strong wind.

Ghostly DispersalSpecter
As a reaction to being hit by an attack, she may spend her reaction to disperse: she becomes resistant to the triggering damage and fades into mist and pale motes of flame, taking all her items with her. She may also disperse as an action. While dispersed, her creature type is undead, she is invisible, she has immunity to all non-psychic damage and all conditions, and she gains a flying speed of 10 ft. She perceives the world through a supernatural haze (disadvantage on Perception checks; GM may withhold details). She still occupies her previous position but cannot be perceived by mundane senses; truesight or comparable abilities can see her, and the spiritually attuned can feel her presence (DC 14 Intelligence (Religion) check). She can move through solid objects of 1 inch or thinner not warded against the passing of spirits, but if she ends a turn inside such an object she suffers 1d6 psychic damage. While dispersed she cannot attack, perform invocations, or take any action other than to reincorporate. Once she has dispersed and reincorporated, she cannot disperse again until she completes a short rest.
Pull of the AfterlifeSpecter
Being dispersed pulls her closer to the afterlife. At the end of each of her turns if she began the turn dispersed, she must spend her action to reincorporate or suffer 1d6 psychic damage, plus 1d6 additional psychic damage per round delayed beyond the first. If dispersed outside a combat encounter, she suffers this increasing damage every minute instead. If her hit points are reduced to 0 in this way, she counts as being killed — see Queue of the Dead.
Queue of the DeadSpecter
She is not alive, so she cannot die in the traditional sense. If she is killed (in any manner), she vanishes back to the Realm of Waiting, leaving only her items behind. There she must wait either until her soul is judged and processed (hundreds of years) or until she gets another chance to escape (also hundreds of years). Barring exceptional circumstances, being killed removes her from the campaign just as it would any other PC.
Spiritual AnchorSpecter
During a long rest, she can form a spiritual bond with one willing living creature (typically another PC, provided they are not also a mazoku or specter), designating them as her spiritual anchor. Within 30 ft of her anchor, she can ignore the increasing damage of Pull of the Afterlife. While she is dispersed and invisible, her spiritual anchor can perceive her. She may have only one spiritual anchor at a time. Anchor: unassigned — awaits a companion.
Watchful PresenceSpecter
While within 30 ft of her spiritual anchor, she can choose to disperse and remain dispersed during a long rest. Instead of sleeping, she remains conscious, watching over her allies; she can wake her anchor or return to her physical form at will. As long as her anchor completes that long rest, she counts as completing one too.

Pilgrim — Class Features

She did not seek Enlightenment. Enlightenment found a woman who refused to move on, and offered her a discipline by which to remain.

Cultivated PotentialPilgrim 1
She has additional d8 Hit Dice that do not increase her maximum hit points but can be used normally on short rests, or spent by effects that require Hit Dice. At 7th level her bonus is 4 dice, giving her 11d8 total.
Strength of Body and SpiritPilgrim 1
While unarmored and wielding no shield, her AC equals 10 + Dex + Con. She is proficient with unarmed strikes and may use her Constitution modifier for unarmed attack and damage rolls in place of Strength. Her unarmed strikes deal 1d4 instead of normal unarmed damage. (On the sheet she records this trait as “Strength in Body & Mind.”)

Each form ties to a state, modifies movement or saving throws, and shifts her energy at end of turn.

Path of Justice — Cosmic Path

Those who follow the Path of Justice see a world full of injustice, needless cruelty, and the failure of ideals. Instead of despairing, pilgrims who walk this path seek to channel their inner energy not just to align with the world, but to change it. They often seek to meet tyranny and evil with force, striking down those who trample upon the lives of others.

Yang AlignmentPath 2
Her innate energy shifts towards Yang. Her resting energy position moves closer to the Yang apex of the Balanced track. When her energy is at the Yang apex, her Yang value is 5 instead of 4.
Philosopher's ArsenalPath 2
She becomes skilled in externalizations that overwhelm foes with direct power. Her cosmic path ability modifier is Intelligence, which sets her externalization save DC. She learns the Consuming Strike and Storm of Fists externalizations, and as she levels she gains the ability to perform additional path-specific externalizations using SRD spell rules at the costs given on Table 2-11.
Consuming StrikeExternalization
Activation: 1 action. Range: touch. She makes an unarmed attack against a creature within range. On a hit she may spend and roll a number of Hit Dice up to her Yang value to make that strike deal bonus radiant damage equal to three times the result of the highest rolled Hit Die. Energy shift: toward the Yin apex on every Hit Die result of 5 or higher.
Storm of FistsExternalization
Activation: 1 action. Range: Self, line of 5 ft × Constitution modifier — for her, a 20-foot line. She makes many unarmed attacks against creatures in range, spending and rolling Hit Dice up to her Yang value. Each Hit Die result may be added to the attack or damage roll for each attack (each die assigned to only one roll). Energy shift: toward the Yin apex on every result of 5 or higher; her walking speed also increases by 5 ft until end of turn.
Blazing StrikesPath 3
Her strikes overflow with brilliant energy. When she makes an unarmed attack she can have it deal an additional 1d4 radiant damage. (At 12th level this rises to 1d6.)
Forceful DefensePath 6
She is especially adept at counterattacking. When a creature within 5 ft hits her, she may use her reaction to spend and roll one Hit Die; if its result is 5 or higher she moves one step toward the Yin apex. She reduces the damage she takes by the result of the Hit Die. She may then make an unarmed attack against the creature that damaged her; on a hit she deals bonus radiant damage equal to the result of the Hit Die.

Haunting Phenomena

Prerequisite: Specter species.

Haunting PhenomenaFeat
She has become capable of manifesting chilling phenomena in her environment. Two benefits:
  • Her Charisma score increases by 1, to a maximum of 20. (Already reflected: Cha 16 is post-feat.)
  • She gains the ability to cast message and prestidigitation from the SRD at will. She can do this even while she is dispersed under Ghostly Dispersal.
These are her uncanny tells: a whisper at the ear of someone who is alone; a candle that gutters in a windless room; a name written in the condensation on the inside of a window.

Sheet Reconciliation

A reading of the sheet against the source material yields the following notes — recorded here so that nothing is silently corrected:

  • Hit Dice 11d8. Not a typo. Cultivated Potential grants 4 bonus d8 at level 7, on top of 7 d8 from class levels.
  • Save proficiencies. Sheet shows Con + Int; class grants Con + Wis. Likely a marking slip on the saves list. See errata above.
  • Strength in Body & Mind. Player's phrasing for Strength of Body and Spirit.
  • Form of Enlightenment. Singular on the sheet; book grants access to all five forms (Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Void) from level 7 — she chooses which to enter as a bonus action.
  • Cosmic Path written in the alignment slot. “Cosmic Path of Justice” corresponds to the Pilgrim's Cosmic Path: Path of Justice. The alignment field is otherwise unused; AiR sheets often repurpose it.
  • Clan blank. Specters do not typically maintain clan affiliation; the sheet is left empty.
  • Background. Not assigned on the sheet; languages noted only as “3 languages.” Per the Specter rules, Language comes from the chosen Background.
  • Guqin. Listed both in equipment and in the Other Proficiencies block — she carries it and is proficient with it.

Provenance

Cricket Wife was built for a one-shot that never ran. The sheet is preserved as written; no play has occurred. Her spiritual anchor was never assigned, her background never chosen, her languages never named — the parts of her that the table would have filled in remain blank, waiting for a session that did not come.

If the dossier ever wakes

The mechanical chassis is complete enough to play tomorrow. Anchor a living companion. Pick a background and the two languages it grants alongside Common. Mark Wisdom proficient and Intelligence not. Then she steps into the road, a finger's-breadth above it, the guqin slung at her back, the staff in her hand, the dagger in her sleeve.
