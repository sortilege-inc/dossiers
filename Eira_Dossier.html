I. Identity & Standing

Eira is a Bree-folk woman. The chronicle met her at the Silver Lily in Bree on the road north, where Brandor and Orlec had been guests before. She had felt their desperation the last time they came — the rangers who knew the risks and went anyway. They had left at night last summer and had not returned. She gave the family the directions she had — a watchtower, a forked stream, old Númenórean script — and asked to ride with them. Celenneth's answer: she knew them, can guide us. I trust her enough to give it a chance. She travelled north with them through the rougher tavern at the Broken Branch, the road in between, and the ranger station before the Frost Maw, where she stayed when the family descended. She returned to Bree, where she met them again in Book XIII at the tavern after the family's reckoning with Linnea's kin.

Book X · The Road North · The Broken Branch
The rougher tavern; the mercenary turned back.
The contrast with the Silver Lily was sharp — weight of stares, no discretion. Eira moved closer to Linnea without being asked. She remembered the Silver Lily's discreet respect and wished for it now. When the mercenary approached, Celenneth stood like a coiled bowstring; Eira's voice was steel-edged: best step back. The barkeep intervened; the mercenary read the room and sat down.
Book X · The Ranger Station
The parting at the threshold.
Celenneth, refusing to believe Brandor and Orlec dead without proof; Linnea, the Binding spoken plainly in the ranger station: I don't know what this means, but I'm not leaving your side, and I'm going to make sure we come home together. Eira's read of the room that the tension of doom needed relief, that in a place of ghosts someone should claim a piece of life. She returned to find Celenneth and Linnea already holding each other close. She did not descend with them.
Book XIII · Bree, the Tavern
Returned, grinning, wry.
After the family's confrontation with Linnea's kin in the market and the long evening that followed, Eira returned at the tavern — grinning, wry, calling it a feast of chatter. She released the tension without dismissing it.
