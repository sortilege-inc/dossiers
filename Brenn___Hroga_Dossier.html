i.Identity & Bond

Brenn is a dwarf — stone-skinned, dense, descended through long lines from the long-extinct steel dwarves. She has a rune carved on her skin and an explosive collar she chose to keep. She came up among bandits, smugglers, and outlaws, and walked away from that life when one of her thefts ended somewhere it shouldn't have.

She is a Beastheart: a hero who fights bonded to a wild creature, never as a trainer with a pet but as a partner sharing senses and instincts. Her companion is Hroga, a boar. The boar is not domesticated and is not a mount. He is a creature with his own statistics and his own ability rolls, who shares Brenn's Stamina pool and her Recoveries, who can be summoned to her chest in emergencies, and who can — under pressure of accumulated ferocity — slip into a battle frenzy that may strike at allies and foes alike.

Her subclass is Prowler. She works from cover, takes opening shots, and uses Hroga to flank. The Pugilist kit fills out the close-quarters work; the Criminal career provides skills the new life still benefits from. She is the cell's most direct melee threat and its most resilient body — and, by some margin, its most physically loaded character: shared stamina between two creatures, two heroic resources, three runes' worth of utility, one explosive collar.

iii.Hroga — Specimen Note

The bond is not training. Brenn shares Hroga's senses and primal instincts; Hroga honors her wishes the way a partner does. Hroga has his own statistics, his own movement, and his own action economy. He shares Brenn's Stamina (their Stamina maxima are the same pool) and her Recoveries. He has no Recoveries of his own — when an effect would let him spend one, Brenn spends one of hers.

⸙ The Shared Body

The mechanic of "Hroga's Stamina maximum equals Brenn's" is structurally important. The two creatures don't have separate hit-point pools — damage to either drains the same number, and healing either restores from the same total. The pair is functionally a single very-high-stamina entity that occupies two squares and gets two action sets. Brenn at 33 Stamina is unremarkable. Brenn + Hroga together with 33 shared Stamina is enormous, because they can split damage between two bodies the enemies have to address separately. Heart of the Beast (the maneuver) makes the pool even more flexible — she can spend a Recovery to teleport Hroga to her space with temporary Stamina equal to her recovery value.

iv.Ferocity & Rampage

The Beastheart has two heroic resources — one belonging to Brenn, one to Hroga — and the relationship between them is the class's defining mechanic.

Ferocity (Brenn's resource)

Brenn and Hroga tap into a predator's bloodlust. Ferocity is what Brenn spends on her heroic abilities and what amplifies Hroga's strikes.

TriggerGain
Start of your turn1d3
The first time in a round that a creature adjacent to Hroga takes damage+2

Rampage (Hroga's resource)

Whenever Brenn or Hroga spends ferocity, Hroga gains rampage equal to the ferocity spent. He doesn't spend rampage to activate abilities — it accumulates, and when it crosses thresholds, it changes him. He loses all rampage at the end of the encounter.

RampageLevelEffect
8 L1+ Hroga is rampaging. At the end of each of Brenn's turns, Hroga must use his Feral Strike ability as a free maneuver. Brenn can't willingly decrease the power roll outcome to a lower tier. For each ally damaged this way, Brenn gains 2 surges, which she can use on this strike.
12 L1+ Hroga has damage immunity equal to his Intuition score (2).
16 L4+ When Hroga uses Feral Strike, he deals extra damage equal to his Intuition score to each target. Brenn gains 1 additional surge for each ally damaged this way.
20 L7+ As a free maneuver, Hroga can increase his size up to size 2, or by 1 if already size 2+. Lasts the encounter.
⚠ The Edge They Walk

Rampage is a runaway feedback loop. The harder Brenn fights — more ferocity spent — the closer Hroga gets to the threshold at which he will turn on her teammates. At 8 rampage he will strike at allies who happen to be in the area of his Feral Strike, on a free maneuver, every turn, and Brenn can't pull the outcome to a lower tier to spare them. The mechanical compensation is that she gets surges for each ally so damaged — the class explicitly converts harm-to-friends into power-for-her. The character is a story about how someone who used to do harm-to-strangers-for-survival now does harm-to-friends-for-power, even though both of them want better. The whole point of the design is that the threshold sneaks up on you.

v.Dwarf Traits

Dwarves are silico-organic — flesh infused with stone, physically denser than other humanoids. They are savvy engineers and technologists, inheriting lore from their elder siblings, the long-extinct steel dwarves. Brenn's three ancestry points are spent on the Stand Tough / Spark Off pair, and the Runic Carving baseline is always available.

Always Available — Runic Carving

Brenn can carve a rune onto her skin with 10 uninterrupted minutes of work, activated by the magic within her body. The rune determines the benefit. She can have one rune active at a time, and can change or remove a rune with the same 10 minutes of work.

  • Detection: Pick a specific type of creature (e.g., "goblins") or object (e.g., "gems"). The rune glows softly when she is within 20 squares of any creature/object of that type, even without line of effect. Type changeable as a maneuver.
  • Light: Her skin sheds light for 10 squares. Toggle on/off as a maneuver.
  • Voice: As a maneuver, she can communicate telepathically with a willing creature she has met before and who is within 1 mile of her. She must know the creature's name, and they must speak a language she knows.
Active Rune to specify

The currently carved rune is not recorded on the character sheet. Worth specifying at Session Zero — and the choice has campaign implications: Detection on "Saffron Seal agents" would be a meaningful first move once she wakes; Voice aimed at a specific contact (former crew, an old fence) would set up that contact as recurring; Light is the most utilitarian choice for a dwarf working in low-light cells and tunnels.

vi.Beastheart Class Features

Companion in Combat — How Hroga Acts

Hroga acts on Brenn's turn as part of her action economy. He has his own movement and his own ability use, including the Feral Strike main action and the Gore maneuver. When Brenn dies or is incapacitated, Hroga's behavior is governed by the Companion Death rules (a companion can become dying at 0 Stamina, etc.). Brenn's death does not automatically end Hroga.

Class Ability — Heart of the Beast

Heart of the Beast
"Better look away — this might not be pretty."
Maneuver · Self

Brenn must spend a Recovery without regaining Stamina. Hroga gains temporary Stamina equal to her recovery value (8) as he leaps out of her chest. Hroga teleports to Brenn's space from any distance, even if he doesn't have line of effect to her.

Spend 1 Ferocity: Hroga can shift up to his speed.

Spend 1+ Ferocity (repeatable): Hroga gains additional temporary Stamina equal to his Might score for each ferocity spent this way.

Spend 5 Ferocity: Brenn restores her dead partner to life with 1 Stamina, even if his body was destroyed. He gains no temporary Stamina if she uses the ability this way.

vii.Prowler Subclass

"You are an unseen ambusher that strikes from the shadows. Your prey is dead before they even know you're there."

The Prowler shapes the pair's combat doctrine: Brenn opens by hiding (Hide skill, granted by the subclass) and lets Hroga draw attention; she steps in from the side, ideally invisible, ideally on a target who hasn't seen her yet.

Lightning Leap

Lightning Leap

Maneuver · Beastheart, Melee 1, Weapon · One creature

The target takes damage equal to 3 + Brenn's Might score (5). Before she uses this ability, she can jump up to her Intuition score (2 squares) in a straight line. During this jump, enemies' spaces don't count as difficult terrain for her. The target takes extra lightning damage equal to the number of squares she jumped.

Spend 1 Ferocity: The jump doesn't provoke opportunity attacks.

Spend 1 Ferocity: She can move up to twice her Intuition score (4 squares) and ignore difficult terrain during this movement.

⸙ The Loadout's Shape

The picks form a coherent ladder. Come On! is a turn-by-turn standard — strike, Hroga gets a free strike, both reposition. Herd the Sheep is the burst-damage option for a single target, with Brenn and Hroga following the displacement. All of You Versus All of Me is the panic button: when surrounded, Brenn taunts everyone within 3, Hroga taunts a second area for an extra ferocity, and Brenn gets temporary Stamina scaling with the target count plus a free Recovery spend. The 5-cost burns her cheapest resource (a Recovery, which she has 12 of) and converts it into a survivability spike right when she needs it most.

Maneuver · Burst 1 · Each enemy in the area

Brenn's loyalty collar detonates, dealing fire damage equal to 5 + her level (6 at L1) to each enemy in a Burst 1 area. Once she has used this ability, she can't use it again until she spends 1 minute out of combat resetting the collar.

⚠ Ajax's War Dogs — Faction Note

The complication ties Brenn to a faction by name: Ajax's War Dogs. The phrasing — "Even if you are a war dog yourself" — leaves open whether Brenn is, was, or has never been a war dog herself, and whether she stole the collar, was issued one, or is impersonating one. The fact that she rigged it to fire outward instead of inward is the operational tell: she does not trust Ajax to leave her collar passive, so she made the collar her weapon. The Saffron Seal's relationship to Ajax's War Dogs is, at present, unrecorded.

Open Threads

The incident text does not specify: what she stole, who was harmed, how badly, or whether the people she used to steal with would still recognize her. The War Dog Collar complication implies she was involved with Ajax's War Dogs at some point — possibly part of her crew, possibly her targets, possibly her employers. The collar is hers now. The relationship to the incident is for Session Zero. Hroga is not present in the incident text: where the boar came from, when she found him (or he found her), whether the bond predates her turning her back on the old life or began after, is unrecorded.

xiv.Position in Collateral draft

Provisional · Drawn from Campaign Concept

The Collateral campaign concept does not list Brenn & Hroga in its primary roster — the pair was created after the campaign was first drafted, alongside Pelorajax, to test the Beastheart class. Connecting her to the campaign's central premise is a Session Zero exercise.

The premise: the player characters wake in the same holding cell in Alloy and are told "The debt has defaulted. You belong to the Saffron Seal now." Brenn's complication makes her arrival in the cell more loaded than most. She is wearing a loyalty collar belonging to a faction the Seal may or may not be aligned with. The collar is rigged to explode outward — she can use it as a weapon. She is also, by every operational measure, the cell's expert at getting out of places she shouldn't be.

Role on the Team

Combat: front-line bruiser plus mobile striker. 33 shared Stamina across two creatures, 12 Recoveries, the Pugilist kit's slide-and-follow signature, plus a 5-Ferocity panic taunt that converts Recoveries into bulk Stamina. She tanks for the casters, and Hroga flanks anything that won't engage her. If pressed, Posthumous Retirement is a maneuver-action burst of 6 fire damage to every enemy within 1 square — a once-per-reset weapon.

Out of combat: escape artist, sneak, sabotage specialist, tracker. Five intrigue skills. Gum Up The Works (career perk) prevents mundane traps from activating. Hide (Prowler) lets her start fights unseen. Hroga's nose (Search) finds things she can't. Brenn's runic carving gives her a rotating utility slot — detection of a named threat, telepathic conversation with a known contact, or 10-square illumination.

Faction Connections

Per her complication, Brenn is tied to Ajax's War Dogs. Whether the War Dogs are allied with, opposed to, or unaffiliated with the Saffron Seal is a campaign-level question. The Inciting Incident does not name her former crew, the location of the theft, or the harmed parties — any of which could be relevant. The Director has wide latitude.

xv.Character Arc

Three threads pull through Brenn's sheet.

The first is the bond and the threshold. Ferocity-spent becomes Rampage-built; at 8 Rampage, Hroga must Feral Strike at the end of every turn, allies-in-area included, and Brenn collects surges for each ally so damaged. The character runs toward a mechanic in which the cost of her power is harm to her own teammates. The campaign asks whether she can manage the threshold — and what it means when, inevitably, she doesn't.

The second is the skills and the life. The Inciting Incident says she turned her back on her old life but kept the skills. Five intrigue skills, a criminal career perk, an explosive collar from a faction she may or may not still be in good standing with — the new life uses every artifact of the old life. The arc question is whether using them is the same as keeping them: whether someone who picks locks for good reasons stays separated from someone who picks them for survival.

The third is two bodies, one stamina pool. Hroga is not a pet or a mount. He is a separate creature whose Stamina is hers, whose Recoveries are hers, whose teleport-to-her-chest move costs her one. The most physically loaded character in the cell is also the most dependent on the body she came in with sharing space — literally — with another creature she loves. The campaign asks what happens to her when, at some point, that pool runs out for one of them and not the other.
