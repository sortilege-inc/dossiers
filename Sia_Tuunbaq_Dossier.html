Dramatis Personae

The Woman Who Wears the Bear

Sia is a Tiefling thirty-seven winters into a quiet, methodical life of hunting things that should not be hunting people. She came south to the frozen continent in pursuit of a question she has never quite spoken aloud, and stayed because the work was steady. In the snowport of IcePyre she takes contracts the local clans will not: revenants risen from sealed cairns, charm-bound werecreatures whose minds have gone foreign, things older than the silence of the gods.

The bearskin she wears is not decoration. It is Tuunbaq — the shape her blood remembers, granted by the Hunter's Bane and the lycan rite that followed. When the work turns to teeth, the pelt drinks the change up over her and Sia becomes something larger, broader, hungrier. She has learned to come back from it. So far.

She is a scholar by training, a tracker by trade, and a bleeder by necessity. The braid down her front is pink because she dyed it; it is the only soft thing about her.

— II —

The Bleeder's Art

She survived the Hunter's Bane. The blood remembers everything, now. It listens for kin in other veins, and it answers when she calls it back.

Hunter's Bane — Blood Hunter 1

Advantage on Survival checks to track fey, fiends, and undead; advantage on Intelligence checks to recall information about such creatures. Her body has been ritually altered to channel hemocraft. Hemocraft DC 16; Hemocraft modifier +4 (Int).

Blood Maledict — BH 1, 6 · Recharge on Short or Long Rest

Twice between rests, Sia channels a blood curse. She may amplify a curse by taking 1d8 necrotic damage (unreducible) to add an additional effect. Creatures without blood are immune unless amplified.

CURSE OF THE MARKED   (Bonus action, 30 ft.) Mark a creature; until end of turn, hitting it with her active-rite weapon rolls an extra hemocraft die for rite damage. Amplify: the next attack against the target this turn has advantage.

CURSE OF THE EYELESS   (Reaction, 30 ft., after seeing the roll.) Roll a hemocraft die and subtract from a creature's attack roll. Immune if blind-immune. Amplify: applies to all attacks until end of that creature's turn, rolled separately.

Crimson Rite — BH 2, 7 · Bonus action; lasts until rest

Activating a rite costs 1d8 necrotic damage. While active, the chosen weapon is magical and deals an additional 1d8 of the rite's damage type on every hit. One rite per weapon.

RITE OF THE FROZEN  Extra damage: cold. The natural rite of the wastes.

RITE OF THE FLAME  Extra damage: fire. Her inheritance, turned to a weapon's edge.

Brand of Castigation — BH 6 · 1 use / rest

When she damages a creature with a rite-active weapon, she may sear a hemocraft brand into it. She always knows the direction to a branded creature on the same plane. Each time the creature deals damage to her or anyone within 5 ft. of her, it takes +4 psychic damage (Hemocraft mod). Lasts until dismissed or replaced. Dispel as a 4th-level spell.

Grim Psychometry — BH 9

Advantage on Intelligence (History) checks when recalling the sinister or tragic history of an object she touches or a location she stands in. On a high roll, she may experience brief visions of the past. She has stopped being surprised by what comes through.

Recorded Hunts

Out on the Ice · While Tracking

The Revenant's Mistake

The party was tracking a mark across the flat white. The revenant came for them as they were spread thin — old hunger and older grievance, the kind of thing that knows what blood is for. When it closed on Sia, she did not parry, did not retreat. She let it take her. She let it bleed her.

The mistake was its own. What it drew into itself was not just blood. It was hers — and the blood remembers. From inside the revenant, she reached. From inside the revenant, she pulled. Her own hemocraft turned on its new host and tore at it from within.

The party watched the thing fold around a wound it could not see.

RULING   The blood the revenant drained served as the conduit for an amplified Blood Curse of the Marked — with her own blood already inside the creature, the curse needed no save to take hold. The amplify cost (necrotic damage to herself) was already paid by the revenant's feeding. A clean, costly trade.
— The Form Beneath —

Tuunbaq

Hybrid Transformation · Order of the Lycan

When the work demands it — when the cold cannot reach what they hunt, when the pelt asks to be worn from the inside — Sia opens herself to the change. The bearskin is not put on. It is remembered. Her shoulders broaden under the hide, her hands lengthen into claws the colour of wet bone, her teeth crowd her mouth. She is still Sia in there, watching, narrating; but the body that holds the hunt is Tuunbaq's.

Heightened Senses — Lycan 3

Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell. The wind is louder for her now. The dead smell of metal.

Hybrid Transformation — Lycan 3 · 2/rest · 1 hour

As a bonus action, she takes the shape of Tuunbaq. In the form, she gains temporary hit points equal to 1d6 per blood hunter level (9d6 at her current level), her speed increases by 10 ft, her jump distance doubles, and she gains advantage on Strength checks and saves and on saves against being charmed. Her natural claws (1d6, finesse, Str-based) are weapons she is proficient with. She may activate her Crimson Rite on them.

Stalker's Prowess — Lycan 7

Her walking speed is permanently increased by 10 ft (already reflected). She may also make a Dexterity (Stealth) check with advantage as part of her move. She has learned to disappear into the white between steps.

— Bloodlust —

While in Hybrid form, if Sia ends her turn without having attempted to attack a creature or having taken damage since her last turn, she must succeed on a DC 8 Wisdom save or move her full speed toward the nearest creature and use her action to make a melee attack against it. The bear does not understand peace. It must be reminded by violence or by the taste of her own blood.
