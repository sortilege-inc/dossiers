I. Identity & Standing

Eryndil is an elf of Lindon, of the western shores. She came to the Angle in Book XIII bearing Círdan's errand: the Adamant Relic with its motifs of waves and stars and ships, which would open only to Númenórean blood. She brought too the news of Dol Guldur — the darkness there does not yield easily; yet the allies have bought precious time, with men from the West joining Thranduil's forces. The summons she carried respected what the family had built and could be refused. They accepted; she has travelled with them from the Angle through Bree, the road west, the Barrow-downs, the Númenórean ruin, the canyon ambush, the rockfall, the farmhouse of Martha and Luthan, and into Mithlond. The voyage on the Eärlindë followed.

Her named Patron. The errand she carries is his. He tested Celenneth's knowledge of Arnor's lineage, Tar-Ancalimon's era, Elendil — and named what he saw: in you, I glimpse a spark of Númenor's old light.
Celenneth — bearer of the Númenórean blood the relic requiresRangers of the North
Eryndil came to summon her west. At the parting at Mithlond, Celenneth's invitation: consider yourself part of the family. Eryndil's response was a rare flicker of vulnerability. They have ridden, walked, fought, and now sail together. The unresolved thread between them is the arrow loosed at the released prisoner.

V. Chronicle of Her Errand

Book XIII · The Angle
Arrived under the birch trees.
Sea-grey eyes, shell-beads in her hair, blue cloak catching light like water. Camped under birch trees and waited for the family to decide. The summons could be refused. Brought too the news of Dol Guldur: the darkness there does not yield easily. Yet the allies have bought precious time. Men from the West had joined Thranduil's forces. Celenneth's answer: we go as a family. Brynja, Hallas, Linnea, and I — we will not be separated.
Book XIII · Almedra and Berthor's Cottage
Met the widow with awe and condolence.
Almedra, you are an elf — not a question. Eryndil's immediate recognition of her grief, condolences offered before any request. Berthor's notebook given forward because the knowledge belongs to the quest now, not to grief.
Book XIII · Bree's Market
Present at the family's reckoning with Bree.
Travelled with the family through the confrontation with Linnea's kin. The elf at their side as Linnea named her home aloud: Bree isn't home, not anymore. Home is where my family is.
Book XIII · The Road West · The Bear
Spoke to the bear.
We see you, we're merely passing through. Patience and knowledge resolving what would have cost otherwise.
Book XIII · The Númenórean Ruin
Worn carvings; star-and-wave motifs.
The Guild of the Star and Sea named on the stones. Brynja gathered pottery shards. Treasure can be knowledge, too.
Book XIII · The Canyon Ambush
Eryndil's precision; the Warden Blade.
The fight was competent — Celenneth's ribs taking a hit, Linnea's arrows going wide from adrenaline, Eryndil's precision, the Warden Blade finding purpose against shadow-corrupted enemies. We are not butchers (Celenneth), holding the line even injured, even with children behind her. The prisoner spoke: a presence. That's all I know. We found you — or maybe it found you. Released with stern clarity.
Book XIII · Around the Bend
The arrow.
Eryndil lingering, one fluid motion, the arrow vanishing into brush, a faint cry, then silence. She replaces her bow without expression and rides forward. If Celenneth suspected anything, she said nothing.
Book XIII · The Rockfall
The road costing them.
The rockfall that nearly buried them; the detour costing half a league; Brynja silent and distant afterward; Eryndil's hands trembling on the axe handle when she thinks no one is watching.
Book XIII · Martha & Luthan's Farmhouse
Driving storm; vegetables in the kitchen.
The middle-aged couple who opened their door in a driving storm. An elf is no more welcome or unwelcome than any other soul soaked to the skin. Eryndil chopped vegetables; Luthan marvelled at her uncommon cooking methods.
Book XIII · The Barrow-downs
Goblin dealt with offscreen.
Celenneth and Eryndil disappeared around the bend together, returning with grim and victorious expressions. It won't trouble anyone again. The children sheltered behind rocks.
Book XIII · Mithlond · Her Dwelling
The family hosted in her home.
Modest, cedar-scented, nautical maps annotated in flowing Elvish script, shells on the carved shelves. Offered the bed without hesitation; took the sofa with graceful acceptance. The family in the narrow bed: Celenneth and Linnea side by side, Brynja curled at their feet, Hallas snug between them.
Book XIII · Mithlond · Círdan
The testing; the recognition.
Círdan tested Celenneth's knowledge of the lineage of Arnor, Tar-Ancalimon's era, Elendil. He named what he saw: in you, I glimpse a spark of Númenor's old light.
Book XIII · The Eärlindë
Aboard.
Silvery-white wood, enchanted timbers, the pivoting cradle mounted to the cabin wall. Laerion's biscuit for Brynja: a taste to calm your first sail. The horn; the moorings released; Brynja gripping the railing — we're really moving! Like dancing, learning to move with the roll. Celenneth's invitation: consider yourself part of the family. Eryndil's rare flicker of vulnerability in answer.
